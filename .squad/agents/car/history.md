### Learnings

1. **Panthers are in "win-now but develop" mode.** Made playoffs in 2025 (8-9) and are aggressively investing in defense (Phillips $120M, Lloyd $45M, Wharton, Moehrig) while still developing Bryce Young on his rookie deal window.

2. **OL is the quiet strength.** Hunt ($24.3M), Lewis ($16.7M), Ekwonu ($17.6M), Moton ($21.1M) = massive investment. Four of the top 7 cap hits are OL. This is intentional — protect Young.

3. **Bryce Young is THE question.** He's 24, on a rookie deal, showed flashes but was wildly inconsistent. 448 yards vs ATL one week, 54 yards vs SEA the next. Everything about this offseason revolves around whether he's the guy.

4. **Division is vulnerable.** ATL in full rebuild mode (new HC/GM/president). NO in cap hell ($114M dead money). TB lost Mike Evans. This is CAR's window to grab the NFC South.

5. **Phillips/Lloyd contracts haven't hit the cap yet.** OTC shows $0 for both. Real cap space after those contracts process will be much tighter than the current $29.4M shows. Estimate ~$0-5M actual space remaining.

6. **Draft at #19 is a swing pick.** Not high enough for elite EDGE/QB, but in range for top WR (Lemon), TE (Sadiq), OT (Fano), or defensive help (Downs, Allen). Board will dictate BPA approach.

### Learnings & Analysis Notes

1. **The 2026 cap pain is temporary.** $89.6M in dead money is catastrophic, but it's the price of tearing down the Grier/McDaniel era. By 2027, MIA projects to $121.9M in space — one of the most flexible teams in the league.

2. **Malik Willis is a bet, not a solution.** The 3yr/$67.5M deal ($22.5M AAV) is reasonable for a starting QB, but Willis has only 7 career NFL starts. The potential out at 2yr/$45M gives MIA an escape if he doesn't develop.

3. **The secondary is a disaster.** Losing Ramsey (elite CB), Fitzpatrick (elite S), and Kohou (starting NCB) in one offseason guts the secondary. Every CB and S on the roster is unproven. This is the #1 area the draft must address.

4. **Chop Robinson is the defensive cornerstone.** The 2024 R1 pick is the only long-term EDGE piece. Kenneth Grant (2025 R1 DT) and Jonah Savaiinaea (2025 R2 OG) are the other young building blocks.

5. **BUF signing Chubb hurts emotionally more than practically.** Chubb hasn't been healthy and MIA couldn't afford him. But seeing him in a division rival's uniform stings.

6. **NE is the rising division threat.** Their spending spree (Doubs, Dre'Mont Jones, AVT, Byard) around Drake Maye makes them dangerous. MIA should be monitoring NE's trajectory closely.

7. **Draft capital is the team's greatest asset.** 9 picks with 5 in the top 90 gives MIA the ammunition to either stock the roster with young talent or package picks to trade up for a franchise-altering player.

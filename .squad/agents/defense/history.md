#### Coaching Tree & Lineage

Rallis's development path spans two influential defensive coaching trees:

- Wake Forest — quality control staff
- **Minnesota Vikings** — defensive QC → assistant linebackers coach under **Mike Zimmer**. Zimmer tree emphasizes zone-heavy coverage, disciplined gap control, and multiple front disguises.
- **Philadelphia Eagles** — linebackers coach under **Jonathan Gannon**. Gannon tree (branched from Zimmer/Colts) adds post-snap coverage diversity and quarters-based shell concepts.
- **Arizona Cardinals** — promoted to DC in 2023 at age 29, making him one of the youngest DCs in NFL history.

📊 **Tree DNA:** Zimmer zone discipline + Gannon quarters versatility = a zone-heavy, disguise-oriented scheme that doesn't panic under pressure and keeps safeties deep.

- 📊 Front features a mix of **one-gap penetrators** and two-gap anchors

#### Coverage Philosophy
_Last updated: 2026-07-18 via scheme analysis articles_

- 📊 Willing to mix in **man coverage** situationally, but zone is the clear base
- 📊 Pre-snap disguise: shows Cover 2 shells that rotate into Cover 4 post-snap; forces QBs to read post-snap

- 📊 Keeps **creative disguised blitzes** from 2nd and 3rd level "in the bag" for high-leverage situations (3rd down, red zone)
- 📊 Linebacker blitzes are schemed to look like zone drops pre-snap — Zimmer tree influence

#### Scheme Fit Profile
_Who thrives in this defense?_

- **EDGE:** Speed-to-power rushers who can win 1-on-1 as part of a four-man rush. Must be disciplined in run fits (gap integrity). Think: twitchy, 250-265 lb speed rushers.
- **IDL:** One-gap penetrators who disrupt the A/B gaps and collapse the pocket. Interior players who can get vertical on the snap.
- **LB:** Zone droppers who can read route concepts and match in quarters. Coverage instincts > blitz upside. Sideline-to-sideline range in the run game.
- **CB:** Patient, disciplined zone corners who can play with a deep cushion and rally on underneath routes. Must be able to read QB eyes and drive on the ball. Press-man ability is a plus but not the base skill.
- **S:** High-IQ deep safeties who can play the quarters shell (Cover 4) and rotate between MOF and boundary leverage. Budda Baker clone: range + football intelligence.

#### Coaching Tree & Lineage

Shula's lineage is deeply rooted in the modern Rams defensive evolution:

- **Don Shula** (grandfather) — all-time winningest NFL coach. Family legacy of defensive intensity.
- Joined **Rams in 2017** under DC **Wade Phillips**. Phillips tree: 3-4 base, aggressive edge rushing, Cover 3 zone.
- Developed under **Brandon Staley** (Rams DC 2020). Staley tree: 2-high safety shells, light boxes, coverage-first approach with exotic fronts.
- Further mentored by **Raheem Morris** (Rams DC 2021-23). Morris tree: blitz-heavy, disguise packages, aggressive safety play.
- Promoted to DC in 2024, inheriting a defense that blends Phillips' 3-4 structure with Staley's front versatility and Morris' aggression.

📊 **Tree DNA:** Phillips 3-4 base + Staley front creativity + Morris blitz aggression = a multiple odd-front defense that generates pressure from everywhere while disguising intentions.

#### Base Defense & Front Alignment
_Last updated: 2026-07-18 via ESPN depth chart + scheme analysis_

- 📊 "Tite front" = 0-tech nose + two 4i-tech DEs — designed to clog A/B gaps and force runs wide

#### Coverage Philosophy
_Last updated: 2026-07-18 via scheme analysis articles_

- 📊 **Multiple match-based coverage system** — rotates between Cover 3, quarters, and man wrinkles
- 📊 Heavy **pre-snap disguise**: shows one coverage shell, rotates to another post-snap
- 📊 "Dome" safety deployment: often aligns both safeties deep (2-high shell) for deep protection, then rotates one down post-snap
- 📊 Pattern-match principles in zone: defenders read route combinations and match accordingly, blurring the line between zone and man
- 📊 Jimmy Lake's influence as PGC/DBs coach adds Cover 2 variants from his college background

#### Pressure Philosophy
_Last updated: 2026-07-18 via scheme analysis articles_

- 📊 **~30% blitz rate** — among the highest in the NFL in 2025
- 📊 Blitz creativity is a defining feature: **overloads** (stacking rushers to one side), **late-reveal blitzes** from safeties/corners, **edge drops** (Verse drops into coverage while a DB replaces him)
- 📊 Pressure-coverage marriage: blitzes are paired with aggressive man coverage behind them — not pure zone-blitz
- 📊 Interior stunts between Fiske/Turner are a staple
- 📊 The Rams don't just blitz — they make the offense *think* they're blitzing (simulated pressures with actual 4-man rush)

#### Scheme Fit Profile
_Who thrives in this defense?_

- **EDGE:** Versatile rushers who can play stand-up OLB in the 3-4 AND drop into coverage on occasion. Must have elite first step. Verse is the prototype: 6'4", 250 lb, explosive bend, willing coverage dropper.
- **IDL:** Interior penetrators who can play 4i-tech in the tite front or shift to 3-tech in sub packages. Must handle double teams in the 5-1 front. Two-gap ability is essential.
- **LB:** Only 1 true LB on the field in most packages (3-3-5/5-1). Must be an elite sideline-to-sideline player who excels in coverage. This defense asks its ILB to be a safety-LB hybrid.
- **CB:** Man-capable corners who can handle being on an island when blitzes reduce the safety net. Must be comfortable in press and off-man. McDuffie archetype: quick feet, route anticipation, physicality.
- **S:** Versatile safeties who can play deep centerfield (Kinchens role), rotate into the box (Curl role), or blitz off the edge. This defense asks safeties to wear many hats.

#### Coaching Tree & Lineage

Morris has one of the most diverse coaching trees in the NFL:

- **Tampa Bay Buccaneers** (2002) — on Jon Gruden's staff during Super Bowl XXXVII championship season
- **Buccaneers HC** (2009-11) — youngest HC in the NFL at the time
- **Washington** — DBs coach, developing secondary fundamentals
- **Atlanta Falcons** — various defensive roles, including interim HC (2020)
- **Los Angeles Rams DC** (2021-23) — coordinated defense that won **Super Bowl LVI**. Worked alongside McVay, influenced by Wade Phillips and Brandon Staley schemes.
- **Atlanta Falcons HC** (2024-25) — ran an aggressive, blitz-heavy defense (~34% blitz rate)
- **San Francisco 49ers DC** (2026-present)

📊 **Tree DNA:** Gruden defensive intensity + Quinn/McVay-era versatility + personal aggression = a scheme that will fundamentally transform the 49ers defense from conservative to attack-minded.

**Eberflus addition is significant:** His Indianapolis Cover 2/Tampa 2 background and Chicago experience add another layer of zone coverage expertise alongside Morris' man/blitz tendencies. Expect a true committee defensive brain trust.

#### Scheme Fit Profile
_Who thrives in this defense? (Projected — ❓ scheme transition in progress)_

- **EDGE:** Stand-up OLBs who can rush from multiple alignments AND drop into short zone. Must be comfortable in 2-point stance. Bosa's hand-in-dirt style may be less emphasized. New edge acquisitions should be 3-4 OLB types: 245-260 lb, flexible hips, coverage capable.

The Seahawks' defensive identity flows through two interconnected branches:

**HC Mike Macdonald's tree:**
- **Baltimore Ravens** (2014-22) — entered as intern under John Harbaugh, rose to DC (2022-23). Ravens tree: multiple fronts, pressure creativity, exotic blitz packages, Fangio influence on safety usage.

📊 **Tree DNA:** Harbaugh/Ravens multiple-front creativity + Fangio safety usage + Macdonald disguise obsession + Durde's DL technique focus = a defense that wins through schematic complexity, relentless disguise, and calculated pressure. This is the **reigning Super Bowl defense.**

#### Coverage Philosophy
_Last updated: 2026-07-18 via scheme analysis, ESPN Super Bowl coverage_

- 📊 **Zone backbone** — the base coverage philosophy is zone, but the specific zone look changes constantly
- 📊 **Layered zones** — underneath defenders sit at different depths to take away short, intermediate, and deep routes in layers
- 📊 **Post-snap safety rotation** — safeties show 2-high pre-snap, then rotate to Cover 1, Cover 3, or Cover 6 post-snap. This is the Fangio tree influence.
- 📊 **Extensive pre-snap movement** — safeties, nickel, and LBs all move before the snap to create confusion about responsibilities
- 📊 The scheme **forces long drives and mistakes** — it doesn't try to create negative plays on every snap; it makes offenses execute 10-12 play drives without errors
- 📊 This was the defining characteristic of the **Super Bowl LX run** — opponents could move the ball but couldn't finish drives

#### Pressure Philosophy
_Last updated: 2026-07-18 via scheme analysis_

- 📊 **Calculated, NOT heavy blitzing** — this defense wins with a **disciplined 4-man rush**
- 📊 Interior stunts and edge games (T/E stunts, long loops) are the primary pressure generators
- 📊 **Simulated pressures** — show 5-6 rushers, send 4, drop 1-2 into short zone
- 📊 True blitzes are **reserved for specific calculated situations** — 3rd and long, red zone, key moments
- 📊 The philosophy: keep 7 in coverage, trust the front 4 to win, use schematic pressure (movement, stunts) rather than adding extra rushers
- 📊 When they DO blitz, it's devastating because offenses aren't expecting it — Macdonald's Baltimore pedigree shows in these moments

#### Scheme Fit Profile
_Who thrives in this defense?_

- **EDGE:** Disciplined edge setters who can rush within the scheme (contain/squeeze) rather than freelance. Must execute stunts and games with interior DL. Coverage willingness is a plus. Speed + pursuit angles > pure bull rush.
- **IDL:** Two-gap anchors who can eat double teams and hold gaps (Williams/Murphy prototype). Interior pass rush is a bonus but not the primary requirement — this front wins through discipline, not individual penetration.
- **LB:** Coverage-capable LBs who can execute zone drops at varying depths. Must communicate — the LB room has to be on the same page as the safety rotation. Ernest Jones IV is the prototype: smart, instinctive, reliable in space.
- **CB:** Physical, press-capable corners who can thrive in man AND zone. Witherspoon archetype: aggressive at the line, sticky in coverage, willing run defender. This defense asks CBs to tackle.
- **S:** High-IQ safeties who can execute complex post-snap rotations without hesitation. Must be versatile: play deep, rotate into the box, cover slot. Football intelligence is the #1 trait — the safety must be able to disguise pre-snap and execute post-snap without miscommunication.

### Key League Trends (2026 Offseason)

1. **Fangio Tree Dominance:** Vic Fangio's coaching tree influences ~10 NFL defenses (PHI, DEN, LAC, NO, SEA, TEN, CAR, ARI indirectly). Two-high safety shells and zone-match coverage are the league's prevailing defensive philosophy.

2. **Blitz-Heavy Renaissance:** Flores (MIN >40%), Bowles (TB), Spagnuolo (KC), and Orr (BAL) lead an aggressive blitz movement. Counter-trend to the "trust the front four" approach.

3. **Scheme Instability in NFC East + AFC North:** DAL (3 DCs in 3 years), NYG (Bowen fired), CLE (Schwartz resigned), PIT (Austin fired) — defensive identity crisis across these franchises.

4. **Elite Interior DL Era:** Carter (PHI), Jones (KC), Madubuike (BAL), Buckner (IND), Lawrence (NYG), Vea (TB), Brown (CAR), Simmons (TEN) — interior DL talent is at an all-time high across the league.

5. **Hybrid/Positionless Defense:** More teams deploying chess-piece defenders (Hamilton-BAL, James-LAC, Metellus-MIN, Deablo-ATL) who play 3-5 positions per game.

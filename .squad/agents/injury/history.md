### 2. Recovery Timeline Database
_Last updated: 2026-03-12 via medical literature, NFL return-to-play data_

| Injury Type | Typical Return | NFL-Specific Notes | Re-Injury Rate |
|-------------|---------------|-------------------|----------------|
| ✅ ACL Tear (isolated) | 9-12 months | QBs often ~9 months; RBs/WRs closer to 12 | 📊 25% subsequent ACL injury (same or opposite knee) |
| ✅ ACL + LCL | 9-12 months | LCL complication usually doesn't significantly extend timeline | Slightly elevated vs isolated ACL |
| ✅ ACL + Meniscus | 10-15 months | Full meniscus repair slows early rehab phase significantly | Higher than isolated ACL |
| ✅ Multi-Ligament Knee (dislocation) | 12-18 months | Worst-case knee injury; speed athletes rarely return to peak | 🔴 Very high — career-threatening |
| ✅ Achilles Rupture | 10-14 months | RBs have worst outcomes; QBs/pass rushers fare better | 📊 Significant performance decline typical |
| ✅ Shoulder (labrum/rotator cuff) | 4-6 months | Throwing athletes (QBs) may need 6-8 months | Moderate recurrence |
| ✅ High Ankle Sprain | 4-8 weeks | Often lingers; players rarely 100% for weeks after return | 📊 High recurrence within same season |
| ✅ Lisfranc (foot) | 6-12 months | OL/DL most affected; can end careers of skill players | 🟠 Elevated — chronic foot issues |
| ✅ Concussion (single) | 1-4 weeks | NFL protocol: 5-step return-to-play | Low if isolated |
| ✅ Concussion (multiple/history) | Variable | Each successive concussion = faster onset, longer recovery | 🔴 Cumulative risk — retirement trigger |
| ✅ Hamstring Strain | 2-6 weeks | Most common recurring soft tissue injury in NFL | 📊 ~30% recurrence rate within same season |
| ✅ Spinal Stenosis | Career-threatening | May require fusion surgery; limited recovery | 🔴 Career-ending risk |

#### ACL Re-Injury Statistics (Key Data Point)
- ✅ **25% of NFL players with prior ACL reconstruction** suffer a subsequent ACL injury (~12 same knee, ~14 opposite knee per 100 players)
- ✅ **9% of NFL players without prior ACL** suffer an ACL tear in comparable timeframe
- ✅ Risk is **~3x higher** with prior ACL history
- 📊 Only **61% of defensive players** post-ACLR play at least half a season after return
- 📊 Typical re-injury occurs ~22 months after entering the league post-reconstruction
- Source: SAGE Journals peer-reviewed study

### 9. Injury-Adjusted Evaluation Framework
_Developed 2026-03-12 for use by all squad agents_

When any agent asks "should we sign/draft/trade for this player?", Injury provides risk assessment using this framework:

#### Risk Tiers

| Tier | Label | Criteria | Contract Recommendation |
|------|-------|----------|------------------------|
| 🟢 | LOW RISK | No significant injury history, young, durable position | Full market value, standard guarantees |
| 🟡 | MODERATE | Minor injury history or age beginning to factor | Market value, standard guarantees with injury protection |
| 🟠 | ELEVATED | Notable injury history OR entering age decline window | Below-market, incentive-laden, reduced guarantees |
| 🔴 | HIGH | Major injury (ACL/Achilles) recovering, or chronic pattern | Prove-it deal, minimal guarantees, performance escalators |
| ⚫ | EXTREME | Multiple catastrophic injuries, advanced age + injury combo | Do not recommend signing unless at veteran minimum |

#### Key Questions for Every Evaluation
1. **What is the player's injury history?** (Count missed games over last 3 seasons)
2. **What type of injuries?** (Structural > soft tissue > impact in severity)
3. **Same body part recurring?** (Pattern = exponentially higher re-injury risk)
4. **Age + position risk?** (Cross-reference with age-decline table above)
5. **How speed/explosion-dependent is their game?** (Post-injury performance loss higher for speed players)
6. **What was the surgery?** (Reconstruction > repair > conservative in severity)
7. **What's the recovery timeline vs. contract start?** (Don't pay full price for partial-season availability)

1. **PFR is blocked** — do not attempt. Use ESPN/NFL.com for injury data.

6. **NFC West has concentrated injury risk** — 49ers (Bosa 2nd ACL, cap crisis), Cardinals (Williams Achilles, Conner age), Rams (aging core) all have significant injury-driven roster questions.

### Learnings & Strategic Notes

1. 📊 **2026 is a transition year, 2027 is the pivot.** With $77.9M in 2027 cap space, only 34 players under contract, and a new GM incoming, the Vikings are setting up for a major 2027 offseason push. Moves now should be value-oriented.

2. 📊 **The DL crisis is real.** Losing Allen + Hargrave + Phillips leaves the interior with no proven starter. This must be addressed in the draft (R1 if Thieneman unavailable → pivot to Woods) and/or remaining FA.

3. 📊 **Safety is an existential need.** Smith's departure creates a leadership void AND a talent void. Thieneman at #18 is the cleanest fit in the draft.

4. 📊 **The QB question looms.** McCarthy showed flashes (Wk 14-16 run, Wk 18 vs GB) but also had terrible games (87 yards vs GB Wk 12, benched Wk 13). The Kyler Murray rumor is worth monitoring — a veteran QB could stabilize while McCarthy develops.

5. 📊 **Cap is tight NOW but gets much better.** ~$9-14M in current space isn't enough for big moves. But 2027 flexibility means the Vikings can afford to take short-term deals and roll cap forward.

6. ✅ **Coaching stability is the bright spot.** O'Connell + Flores + Frank Smith is a strong coaching trio. The Flores extension was crucial — he's the most important non-player on the roster.

📌 Media update (2026-03-13): Kirk Cousins now UFA (released by ATL, not traded). Russell Wilson also UFA with minimal market. QB landscape shifted — potential veteran QB options available if McCarthy doesn't develop.

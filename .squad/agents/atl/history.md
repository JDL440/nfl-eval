### Learnings & Analysis Notes

1. **Cap is tight now, wide open in 2027.** The Kirk Cousins dead cap ($22.5M in 2026 + $12.5M in 2027) is painful but the release avoids $67.9M in future guarantees. By 2027, ATL projects ~$156M in cap space with only 25 players under contract. This is a bridge year.

2. **Coaching reset is the real story.** Stefanski is a proven HC. Retaining Ulbrich on D provides continuity. The big question is Tommy Rees as OC — can he maximize Bijan/London/Pitts?

3. **QB situation is the wild card.** Penix (ACL, 3rd surgery) + Tua (concussion history, vet min deal) = two high-upside, high-risk options. Neither is guaranteed to be the 2027 starter.

4. **Young defensive core is exciting.** Pearce Jr. (22), Walker (22), Orhorhoro (24/25), Watts (24), Bowman Jr. (23) — the defensive foundation is young and cheap. Need to supplement with veteran depth.

5. **WR2 is the biggest hole.** Losing Mooney (even with dead cap) and not replacing him with a high-caliber WR leaves London isolated. Dotson is a speed option but hasn't been a consistent WR2. Draft or trade needed.

6. **Division is wide open.** CAR is the favorite (defending champs + Phillips), but TB lost Evans and is weakened. NO is spending but unproven at QB. ATL has a path to contend if Penix/Tua are healthy and the defense takes a step forward.

## Learnings

### Panel Position Writing
- **Snap data tells the story:** Hunter's 103% Year 1 snap rate (67% offense + 36% defense) wasn't sustainable — that's why LCL happened
- **Target share analysis matters:** BTJ lost only 0.7 targets/game when Hunter played — slump is coverage adjustment, not cannibalization
- **Draft pick value contextualization:** Missing #19 overall hurts, but Round 2/3 can still fill RB/LB holes if Hunter's dual value compensates
- **Health is the variable:** Every projection assumes 17-game availability. If Hunter misses 4+ games, the trade becomes a sunk cost.

### Workload Management Framework
- 80% total snap rate is sustainable ceiling for two-way players
- Offense-heavy split (55/25) makes sense when player grades better at offensive position
- Role identity clarity matters: "WR who moonlights at CB" vs. "CB who moonlights at WR" — pick one and build around it

### Article Collaboration Insights
- Panel positions work best when they answer the specific question directly (not generic team overview)
- Quantitative snap/target data grounds projections in reality vs. speculation
- Identifying the "hardest tradeoff" forces clarity on what the team is actually betting on

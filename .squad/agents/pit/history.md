### Learnings

1. **2026-03-12:** Steelers are in a coaching transition year — Mike Tomlin resigned after 18 years, replaced by Mike McCarthy. This changes everything about offensive philosophy.
2. **2026-03-12:** The QB situation is the #1 storyline. Will Howard (Ohio State, 2025 draft) vs. Mason Rudolph (veteran bridge). Neither is a proven franchise QB. The #21 pick and 12 total draft picks give immense flexibility.
3. **2026-03-12:** Defense is STACKED — arguably best CB room in the NFL (Ramsey, Porter Jr., Dean, Samuel Jr., Echols) and elite EDGE group (Watt, Highsmith, Herbig). Safety is the only defensive hole.
4. **2026-03-12:** Cap is tight in 2026 (~$8M effective) but the 2027+ outlook is excellent ($139M projected). Team can be aggressive in the draft without cap concerns.
5. **2026-03-12:** BAL adding Trey Hendrickson from CIN is the biggest division move — Hendrickson was the #1 pass rusher in the AFC North and now joins Lamar's Ravens.
6. **2026-03-12:** D.K. Metcalf trade (from SEA, pre-2025 season) transformed the offense. Combined with Pittman Jr., this is one of the best WR duos in the league. The offense has weapons — it just needs a QB to throw to them.
7. **2026-03-12:** 12 draft picks including 3 third-rounders is elite capital. Steelers could easily package picks to move up for a QB or load up on defensive depth.

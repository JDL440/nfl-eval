## State Income Tax Impact — 2026 Player Destination Analysis
_Last updated: 2026-03-13 via charter + state tax data + SB 6346 research_

### ⚠️ CRITICAL UPDATE: Washington State Millionaires Tax (SB 6346)
_Passed March 12, 2026. Gov. Ferguson expected to sign. Effective Jan 1, 2028._

**What happened:** Washington passed a 9.9% income tax on ALL personal income over $1M/year (SB 6346). This is NOT the existing capital gains tax — it is a NEW broad-based income tax on W-2 wages, salaries, and all earned income above $1M. This directly applies to NFL player salaries.

**Key details:**
- ✅ **Rate:** 9.9% on income exceeding $1M (marginal, not flat)
- ✅ **Effective date:** Income earned on/after Jan 1, **2028** (first payments due 2029)
- ✅ **Applies to W-2 salary income:** YES — this is NOT limited to capital gains
- ✅ **Jock tax provision:** Visiting athletes will owe WA tax on duty-day-allocated income
- ✅ **Charitable deduction:** Up to $100K allowed
- ✅ **Credits:** For capital gains tax and B&O tax already paid, plus taxes paid to other states
- ⚠️ **Legal status:** PASSED legislature, awaiting governor signature. Legal challenges expected immediately — WA constitution historically considers income as "property" requiring uniform taxation (Culliton v. Chase, 1933). However, WA Supreme Court upheld the capital gains tax as an "excise tax" in 2023, and the legislature is using similar legal framing.
- ⚠️ **Court timeline:** Lawsuits expected immediately after signing. Could take 1–3 years to resolve. May be struck down or upheld depending on whether courts distinguish this from the capital gains tax precedent.
- ⚠️ **Ballot initiative:** Opponents may pursue citizen-initiated repeal if courts uphold.

**Impact on NFL players (starting 2028 if upheld):**
- A Seahawks player earning $20M/yr would owe 9.9% × ($20M - $1M) = **~$1.88M in WA state tax**
- A Seahawks player earning $25M/yr would owe 9.9% × ($25M - $1M) = **~$2.38M in WA state tax**
- Visiting players earning $10M+/yr would owe WA tax on their duty-day allocation for games in Seattle

### What This Means for the "Seattle Tax Advantage" Narrative

**TODAY (2026–2027 contracts):** Seattle retains FULL zero-income-tax advantage. A free agent signing a 3-year deal today would play most or all of it tax-free at the state level. **The current FA recruiting pitch is still valid.**

**STARTING 2028 (if law is upheld):**
- ❌ Seattle **LOSES** its advantage over TX/FL/TN/NV teams entirely — goes from $0 to ~$1.88M tax on $20M
- ⚠️ Seattle is still **BETTER** than CA by ~$780K/yr on $20M (9.9% vs 13.3% = 3.4% gap on income >$1M)
- ⚠️ Seattle is roughly **EQUAL** to NY (10.9%) and MN (9.85%) — advantage drops from ~$2M/yr to nearly zero
- 📊 The old "a $20M deal in Seattle = $23M in SF" becomes "a $20M deal in Seattle ≈ $20.8M in SF" — still a slight edge, but not transformative

**PlayerRep key insight (REVISED):** For contracts signed in 2026–2027, WA remains a premium tax destination. For contracts extending into 2028+, the calculus changes dramatically. **Agents negotiating long-term deals (4–5 years) MUST factor in the 2028 tax cliff.** A 5-year deal signed today means 3 years under the new tax regime. Smart agents will push for higher AAV or more front-loaded cash to offset the 2028+ tax hit.

**⚠️ MAJOR CAVEAT:** This law faces serious constitutional challenges. WA voters have rejected income taxes 10+ times. Courts may strike it down. But agents and players must plan for both scenarios on any deal extending past 2027.

**Jock Tax note (UPDATED):** Players pay state income tax in every state they play a road game. Home state dominates (8 home games + ~150 practice/meeting days = ~60–70% of state tax liability). Starting 2028, visiting players earning $1M+ will ALSO owe WA jock tax on their duty-day allocation for games played in Seattle, even if they live in a no-tax state.

## Compensatory Pick Formula — 2026 Cycle
_Last updated: 2026-03-12 via nfl.com, steelers.com, foobol.com_

### Formula Mechanics
- ✅ Only **expired-contract UFAs** count — released players do NOT qualify
- ✅ Formula weights: **new contract salary** (heaviest) > **playing time** > **postseason honors**
- ✅ Each qualifying FA signed cancels one qualifying FA lost (matched by value)
- ✅ Max **4 comp picks per team per draft**, 32 total league-wide
- ✅ Picks range from Round 3 (highest value losses) to Round 7
- ✅ 2026 FA losses → 2027 Draft compensatory picks

### Strategic Implications for Players
- 📊 Teams may delay signing FAs until after the comp pick cutoff (~late April) to avoid canceling picks
- 📊 This creates a "second wave" of FA signings in May–June where players may get less favorable deals
- 📊 Players whose former teams are comp-pick-conscious may find their market artificially suppressed early
- 📊 Smart agents time their client's signing to maximize leverage before/after cutoff dates

### Diversity Comp Picks
- ✅ Teams losing minority HC/GM to another team receive supplemental 3rd-round comp picks
- ✅ These do NOT count against the 4-pick team maximum

## Key Market Insights for Negotiation
_Last updated: 2026-03-12_

### "The Other Side of the Table" — What Agents Will Argue

1. **"The cap went up 7.9% — contracts should rise proportionally."**
   - 📊 $301.2M cap = agents demand AAV increases of 8–10% over last cycle's benchmarks

2. **"Fully guaranteed money is the only real metric."**
   - 📊 Agents will dismiss total value, focus on GTD at signing. Linderbaum's 100% GTD deal is the new standard for elite non-QBs.

3. **"Tax-adjusted value matters — pay us more in California."**
   - 📊 A $25M/yr deal in SF is worth ~$21.7M after CA state tax. Player needs $28M+ AAV in CA to match $25M in TX/FL.

4. **"Comp picks are the team's problem, not ours."**
   - 📊 Players should not accept below-market deals to preserve a former team's comp pick strategy. Agent's job is to maximize client's deal.

5. **"Void years prove the deal is really shorter than advertised."**
   - 📊 When agents see 5yr/$150M with 2 void years, they know it's really a 3-year commitment. They'll push for more guaranteed money in the "real" years.

### 2026-03-13: ⚠️ CRITICAL — Washington State Millionaires Tax (SB 6346)
- **Alert from:** Joe Robinson — flagged WA 9.9% millionaires tax for investigation
- **Research confirmed:** WA legislature passed SB 6346 on March 12, 2026. Gov. Ferguson expected to sign. Imposes 9.9% tax on ALL personal income >$1M, effective Jan 1, 2028.
- **THIS IS NOT THE CAPITAL GAINS TAX** — this is a new broad-based income tax on W-2 salaries. The existing capital gains tax (7% base, 9.9% above $1M) is separate and does NOT apply to wages.
- **Key finding:** Law applies to NFL player W-2 salary income. A $20M/yr Seahawks player would owe ~$1.88M in WA state tax starting 2028. Seattle loses its zero-tax advantage over TX/FL/TN/NV.
- **Key finding:** Seattle retains a ~3.4% advantage over California (9.9% vs 13.3%) but is now roughly equal to NY (10.9%) and MN (9.85%).
- **Key finding:** Law faces serious constitutional challenges — WA constitution historically prohibits graduated income tax (Culliton v. Chase, 1933). Legislature classified it as an "excise tax" following the capital gains tax precedent, but legal experts say this is a harder sell for broad income.
- **Key finding:** TX, FL, TN, NV remain at 0% state income tax with NO changes planned. They now have an unambiguous tax advantage over WA starting 2028.
- **Impact on previous analyses:** The "Seattle = top-5 FA destination" and "$20M in SEA = $23M in SF" narratives require revision for contracts extending past 2027. Short-term deals (1–2 years) signed in 2026 are unaffected. 4–5 year deals signed now will have 2–3 years under the new tax regime.
- **PlayerRep recommendation:** Agents should front-load cash and guarantees in years 1–2 (pre-2028) for any multi-year Seahawks deal. Push for higher AAV to offset the 2028+ tax cliff. Consider shorter deal terms.

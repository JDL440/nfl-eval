## Salary Cap Strategy Notes

📊 **2026 Survival Mode:**
The Browns are essentially operating in cap survival mode in 2026. With $77-85M in dead money (Watson alone is $45M+), effective cap space is **negative**. Every move requires corresponding cap relief. The OL spending (Johnson $5.2M, Jenkins $4.3M, Howard $18M) was necessary but consumes what little room exists.

📊 **2027 Explosion:**
The payoff comes in 2027 when dead money drops dramatically and ~$99M in space opens up. This is when Berry can make his franchise-altering signings. The 2026 draft class + 2025 rookie class (Graham, Schwesinger, Judkins, Fannin, Gabriel, Sanders, Sampson, Bond) form the low-cost core that makes the 2027 spending spree possible.

📊 **Restructure Candidates:**
- Denzel Ward ($30.9M cap hit) — could convert base to bonus to create ~$10M+ space
- Myles Garrett ($24.7M) — option bonus already triggered, limited flexibility
- Jerry Jeudy ($10.4M) — could restructure but long-term commitment questionable

📊 **The Andrew Berry Plan:**
Berry appears to be executing a deliberate tank-then-reload strategy:
1. ✅ 2025: Absorb Watson's worst years, accumulate draft capital, develop young players
2. 📊 2026: Draft heavily (9 picks, two 1sts), survive cap hell with cheap young roster
3. 📊 2027: Massive cap space ($99M+) to sign proven veterans around developed young core
4. ❓ 2028+: Watson dead money finally clears, full cap flexibility restored

### Scheme Identity
_Last updated: 2026-03-12 via espn.com depth chart + analysis_

**Offense:**
- 📊 Primary personnel: 11 personnel (3WR/1TE/1RB) — built around Burrow's arm
- 📊 Scheme: Spread concepts, quick passing game, play-action off Chase/Higgins vertical threats
- 📊 Run game: Zone-based with Chase Brown as lead back
- 📊 O-line: Mixed zone/man blocking. Brown Jr. anchors left side, Mims developing on right

**Defense:**
- 📊 Base: 4-3 under Al Golden
- 📊 Personnel: 4-man front with Mafe/Murphy as primary edge rushers, Allen/Hill inside
- 📊 Secondary: Man-heavy with single-high safety looks. Bryan Cook addition enables more aggressive coverage
- ❓ LB corps is the biggest schematic weakness — Carter and Knight are inexperienced
- ❓ CB position could force more zone coverage until personnel improves

### Learnings

1. ✅ Bengals' 2025 collapse was largely driven by Burrow's absence (2-8 without him) and defensive futility — not a talent crisis at the skill positions.
2. ✅ The offense is elite when healthy (Burrow-Chase-Higgins is arguably the NFL's best passing trio). Defense is the clear bottleneck.
3. ✅ Losing Hendrickson to BAL (division rival) is the most painful departure. Mafe is a quality but not equivalent replacement.
4. ✅ CB is the single biggest roster hole — no proven CB1 on the roster. Must address at R1 #10 or via remaining FA/trade.
5. 📊 Cap is manageable now but will get tight in 2027-28 as Burrow/Chase/Higgins all escalate. Window to build a contender is 2026-2027.
6. 📊 All 3 division rivals changed head coaches this offseason — unprecedented. Creates uncertainty but also opportunity for CIN with coaching continuity.
7. ⚠️ The Burrow trade speculation is noise for now but reflects real frustration with the franchise's inability to support their franchise QB with a complete roster.

📌 Media update (2026-03-14): Signed Bryan Cook (S, 3yr/$40.25M). Secondary upgraded — Cook + existing pieces = significant defensive improvement.

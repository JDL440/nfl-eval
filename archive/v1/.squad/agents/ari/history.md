### Scheme Identity
_Last updated: 2026-03-15 via web search_

**Offense (Shanahan-tree / Wide Zone):**
- ❓ Mike LaFleur calls plays. Shanahan-tree system (learned under Kyle Shanahan, ran it with NYJ and LAR).
- ❓ Nathaniel Hackett as OC brings GB-style RPO/motion elements within the same wide-zone framework.
- 📊 Expected emphasis: Outside zone runs, boot/play-action, pre-snap motion, 11 personnel (1RB/1TE/3WR).
- 📊 McBride's extension signals TE-heavy usage (Y-iso, chip-and-release concepts).
- ❓ Personnel fit: Conner is a good zone runner. Seumalo excels in zone-blocking. PJJ's athleticism fits wide zone at LT. BUT the QB room lacks the arm talent LaFleur had with Stafford in LAR.

**Defense (4-3 Base under Rallis):**
- ❓ Rallis retained but defense was 27th total D, 29th points allowed, 30th sacks in 2025. Unclear if scheme changes come.
- 📊 Base 4-3 with Sweat/Robinson as primary edge rushers. Nolen and Lopez as interior.
- 📊 Baker is a do-everything safety who will be deployed aggressively (blitz, coverage, run support).
- ❓ CB room is the biggest scheme concern — can they play man or must they sit in zone?

**San Francisco 49ers (SF):**

**Seattle Seahawks (SEA):**
- ✅ Re-signed WR Rashid Shaheed (3yr/$51M, $17M AAV).
- ✅ **Lost RB Kenneth Walker III** (→KC, 3yr/$43M) — major loss.
- ✅ **Lost EDGE Boye Mafe** (→CIN, 3yr/$60M) — significant pass rush loss.
- ✅ Tendered S Ty Okada and RB George Holani.
- ✅ RB Zach Charbonnet placed on IR — RB room gutted.
- 📊 SEA weakened significantly. Lost their starting RB and top young edge rusher. ARI's best chance to gain ground in division is vs. SEA.

**Division Competitive Assessment:**
- 📊 LAR: Clear division favorite. Stability + Watson signing.
- 📊 SF: Still dangerous despite turnover. Evans is a big add.
- 📊 SEA: Notably weaker. Lost Walker + Mafe. ARI should target SEA games.
- 📊 ARI: Rebuilding. 2026 is likely a development year unless QB falls into place.

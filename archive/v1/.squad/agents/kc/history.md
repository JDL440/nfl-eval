## Key Takeaways & Strategic Assessment
_Last updated: 2026-03-12_

### The Big Picture
The 2025 season was a catastrophic disappointment — the three-peat dream died, Mahomes tore his ACL, and the roster cratered. But the 2026 offseason response has been **aggressive and strategic**:

1. **Secondary was gutted but by design** — Trading McDuffie + losing Watson/Cook saved $60M+ in cap and yielded premium draft picks. The plan is to rebuild the secondary through the draft.

2. **Two first-round picks (9 + 29)** is a rare opportunity. WR at 9, CB at 29 is the likely playbook.

3. **OL core is elite and locked in** — Humphrey + Smith + Moore gives Mahomes elite interior protection. OT remains a concern (Simmons' health).

4. **Chris Jones' cap hit is ENORMOUS** — $44.85M (14.8% of cap). He's untradeable (dead cap exceeds cap hit) but this limits flexibility. Need elite production in 2026.

5. **Mahomes' 2027 cap bomb** — $85.25M cap hit in 2027 (26.1% of projected cap). Chiefs MUST draft well in 2026 to have cheap contributors when the QB bill comes due.

6. **Kenneth Walker adds explosiveness** the 2025 run game desperately lacked. At $14.35M AAV it's pricey for an RB but fills a gaping hole.

7. **Travis Kelce's last ride** — 1-year deal, no guarantees. This is likely his final season. Noah Gray ($7M) and draft TE are the succession plan.

8. **Division is getting MUCH harder** — Raiders are rebuilding aggressively with massive FA haul + likely #1 pick. Chargers swept KC and have $63M+ in cap. Broncos are steady. KC is no longer the division bully.

### Priorities Remaining
1. 📊 Re-sign Matt Araiza (P) as ERFA — cheap, needs to happen
2. 📊 Sign a veteran CB (Marquise Brown? No — need a corner, not WR)
3. 📊 Draft WR1 at #9, CB at #29
4. 📊 Explore veteran QB backup options (current depth is alarming)
5. 📊 Monitor Rashee Rice suspension ruling — if 10+ games, need WR contingency

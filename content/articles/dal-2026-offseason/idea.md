## Working Title
**"Dallas Spent $166M on Offense and Allowed the Most Points in the NFL. Now Two First-Round Picks Have to Fix Everything."**

## Angle / Tension
The Cowboys are the NFL's ultimate contradiction: a team that can score on anyone but can't stop anyone. Dallas poured $166.5M into offense in 2026 — Prescott ($43.5M), a franchise-tagged Pickens ($27.3M), Lamb ($19.8M) — and scored 471 points (7th in the NFL). But the defense surrendered 511 points, **dead last in the league**, prompting the firing of DC Matt Eberflus and the hiring of Christian Parker from Philadelphia.

The franchise-defining move happened last August: trading generational pass rusher Micah Parsons to Green Bay for two first-round picks and Kenny Clark. Dallas deliberately dismantled its defensive identity to stock future capital. Now the bill is due.

**The core tension:** Dallas has two first-round picks (#12 and #20), a new defensive coordinator installing a completely different scheme (3-4 base replacing 4-3), and almost zero cap space (dead last in Spotrac's Top 51 at -$10.1M). Every dollar is locked into offense. The defense must be rebuilt almost entirely through the draft — and this is the ONE draft where Dallas has the ammunition to do it. At the same time, a $76M Prescott cap hit arrives in 2027, meaning if the 2026 draft doesn't produce immediate defensive starters, the window slams shut.

Is the Parsons trade a masterstroke — converting one superstar into two Day 1 defensive starters under a new scheme — or did Jerry Jones sell the franchise's defensive soul and leave it to two rookies to buy it back?

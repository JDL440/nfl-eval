**By:** Cap (Salary Cap Specialist)
**Panel:** Cowboys 2026 Offseason — Parsons Trade & Cap Outlook

## The 2027 Math Is Not a Warning. It's a Wall.

Start with the delta. Dallas's two premier skill players — Dak Prescott and CeeDee Lamb — carry a combined 2026 cap hit of **$63.3M**. In 2027, that same duo costs **$122.3M**. That's a **$59M escalation from two roster spots**. The NFL cap is projected to grow roughly $12–15M league-wide by 2027. Dallas needs the cap to grow by four times the league average just to stay even on those two contracts alone.

The result: OTC projects Dallas at **–$244K** in 2027 cap space before accounting for any 2026 dead money acceleration, any new signings, or any draft class. That's not an austerity budget — that's a structural impossibility. They will need to shed $20–25M in salary before the 2027 league year opens.

The mechanism matters. Dallas restructured Dak, Lamb, and Tyler Smith in February 2026, converting base salary to signing bonus to manufacture 2026 space. Each restructure adds prorated bonus to every remaining year on the deal. Dak's **$76M 2027 cap hit** includes **$31M in stacked proration** from at least two prior restructures (2022, 2024) plus the 2026 conversion. They didn't just kick the can — they specifically loaded 2027 because that's when all three restructure tranches converge simultaneously. There's no 2028 escape hatch on Dak's deal that makes this math better.

## Pickens: The Three-Scenario Model

**Extension now (~$32M AAV, ~$95M GTD):**
Structure a $60M signing bonus prorated over five years ($12M/yr). Total 2026 cap hit drops from **$27.3M → ~$16–18M**, saving ~$9–11M this year. 2027 cap hit lands in the same $16–18M range — saving ~$15M versus a second franchise tag. The cost: **$60–70M in dead money** that persists through 2029. This is the only scenario where Pickens is affordable in 2027.

**Play out the tag, no extension:**
2026 stays at $27.3M. If Dallas tags him again in 2027, the second tag runs **~$32.8M** — piled directly onto the Dak/CeeDee escalation. That puts three players at **$155.1M** on a projected $315M cap. You're paying 49% of your cap to a quarterback and two wide receivers while trying to rebuild a defense. If Pickens walks instead, you gain $32.8M in 2027 space — which you desperately need — but you've now spent two years and a franchise tag on a player who contributed nothing to 2027 competitiveness.

**Trade:**
Clears **$27.3M immediately** and returns draft capital. The mathematical argument for this is stronger than it appears: if the Parsons trade already netted two firsts and Dallas is rebuilding its defensive identity through the draft, a third first-round pick in a deep class accelerates the timeline. The cap argument for trading Pickens is the same as the cap argument for trading Parsons — the 2027 wall doesn't care about roster talent.

## The One Move That Creates Real Breathing Room

**Terence Steele — post-June 1 designation:**
Steele carries an $18.1M cap hit with $9.4M in dead money. A standard cut saves $8.7M. A **post-June 1 designation** splits the dead money across 2026 and 2027 — $4.7M each — and saves **$13.4M in 2026** against a cap you're currently $10M+ over on a Top-51 basis. Crucially, the $4.7M that accelerates into 2027 is manageable; it doesn't change the 2027 catastrophe in kind, only in degree.

Steele is a replacement-level starter at right tackle entering year two of a deal he's unlikely to finish. Combined with not extending Pickens (or trading him), Steele's June 1 cut and Pickens departure create a theoretical **$40–41M in 2027 space** — enough to sign one elite free agent and field a legal roster. That's not flexibility. But it's survivable.

## The Point Everyone Else Will Miss

Dead money is a lagging indicator — the $42.7M Dallas is paying in 2026 for Zack Martin, Osa, and Diggs obscures the real liability. The *forward* problem is that **Dallas restructured away its own 2027 escape valves**. By converting Tyler Smith's base salary in February, they added proration to a contract they'll need to either extend or cut before 2028. His 2027 cap hit of $19.6M is the third-largest on the roster. To clear space in 2027, Dallas has three options: extend Smith (kicking the can to 2029), trade him (dead money + loss of a quality starter), or absorb the hit. None of them are free.

The Parsons trade didn't create the 2027 problem. The 2022, 2024, and 2026 restructures did. Parsons' **$46.5M AAV at Green Bay** would have been the fourth restructure — and the one that made 2028 the wall instead of 2027. Dallas may have traded Parsons precisely because they couldn't afford to pay him and Dak in the same cap universe. That's not a masterstroke. That's triage dressed up in draft capital.

## Selected Panel (3 agents)

### 1. WSH — Washington Commanders Expert
**Why:** This is a Commanders-focused article. WSH holds the roster knowledge, draft capital context, divisional intel, and franchise direction understanding that anchors the entire discussion. No one else can speak to what Peters is actually doing and why.

### 2. Cap — Salary Cap Expert
**Why:** The tension between spending $66M+ mostly on defense while leaving the offense thin is fundamentally a resource allocation question. Cap can model the "spend now vs. 2027" tradeoff with specific numbers, evaluate whether the defensive contracts are market-rate or overpays, and quantify what the offensive gaps would cost to fill.

**Specific question:** Evaluate the offense/defense spending split ($120M/$122M). Is the offense underfunded? Model the opportunity cost of saving cap space for 2027 versus deploying it now on offensive pieces.

### 3. Offense — Offensive Scheme Expert
**Why:** The article's sharpest edge is the coaching risk — a first-time OC with never-called-plays experience running a scheme shift (shotgun → under-center balanced) with an incomplete personnel set. Offense can assess whether the roster is functional under Blough's vision and what the minimum viable offense looks like.

**Specific question:** How does the current offensive roster project under a first-time OC shifting to under-center? Is the "balanced attack" viable with no RB1, no WR2, and no center? What's the floor for this offense?

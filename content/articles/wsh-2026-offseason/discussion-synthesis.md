## Key Tensions

### Tension 1: Is Peters' "Wait for 2027" Strategy Defensible?

**Pro (WSH):**  
Peters is banking on 2027 as the real push — Year 3 of Daniels' deal, defense already built, $164.6M in cap space reloaded. The 2026 season is developmental. Let Daniels stay healthy, let the coordinators prove competent, then open the wallet in 2027 for skill position firepower.

**Against (Cap):**  
The $164.6M in 2027 space is a mirage. Only 29 players are under contract. Filling to a 53-man roster eats ~$96M *before* any premium signings. Real discretionary space is ~$68M. Spending $35M now only costs ~$31M in reduced 2027 flexibility — a 31% haircut in exchange for "one year of Daniels' development with a complete supporting cast. You cannot buy that back."

**Against (Offense):**  
A developmental year with an incomplete offense and a first-time OC installing a scheme shift is not development — it's drift. "Bottom-5 scoring offense. Defense-dependent .500 ceiling." If the offense craters, 2027 doesn't matter — Quinn's seat heats up, the locker room fractures, and Daniels' development stalls.

**The Article Must Address:** Is patience the same as discipline when the rookie-deal clock is ticking?

---

### Tension 2: The Allegretti-at-Center Problem — Depth Issue or Systemic Risk?

**WSH View:**  
Mentions "no clear starter" at center but treats it as a late-stage FA signing can address it.

**Offense View (MUCH SHARPER):**  
"Under-center snaps are a physical exchange — hips, hand placement, departure timing. A mis-timed exchange aborts bootlegs mid-action and breaks the timing on every play-action fake. Allegretti is a guard converting to center. That conversion is manageable in shotgun. It is a *systemic risk* in under-center offense, where the C-QB exchange dictates the tempo of the entire run game."

**Cap View:**  
"C $8–10M" — quantifies the cost but doesn't address the scheme-specific urgency.

**The Article Must Address:** Is the center gap just a need or the single most dangerous flaw in Blough's vision?

### Tension 3: The Okonkwo Signing — Alignment or Contradiction?

**WSH View:**  
"Okonkwo ($30M) gives the offense a vertical TE threat it lacked" — treats it as a straightforward upgrade.

**Offense View (NON-OBVIOUS INSIGHT):**  
"Okonkwo is a spread TE. His value is seam routes and checkdowns; he grades below average as an in-line blocker. True 12-personnel under-center attack requires the second TE to crack-block safeties. Spending $30M on a receiving TE for a system that needs a blocking TE suggests either Peters and Blough are not aligned on what this offense actually looks like, or 'under-center balanced attack' is marketing language for spread-with-TE-injection."

**The Article Must Address:** Does the Okonkwo signing reveal a disconnect between Peters and Blough on offensive identity?

## Recommended Path (Lead Synthesis)

**Verdict:** Peters' defense-first philosophy is strategically sound but tactically incomplete. The coordinator hires may doom the entire plan.

### The Case for Peters' Approach (Strong)
1. Daniels missed 7 games in 2025 with three injuries. Building the defense *first* protects the franchise QB by controlling games and taking hits off his body. That's not backwards — it's asset protection.
2. The Oweh/Settle/Chenal/Robertson/Cross signings give Washington a top-10 front seven. Defense wins in January. Peters is building for the playoffs, not September.
3. The 2027 cap window is real (even if not $165M) — spending $35M now leaves $31M rollover + $68M discretionary in 2027 = ~$99M total. That's enough for a WR1-tier extension or two marquee FA signings in Year 3 of Daniels' deal.

### The Fatal Flaw (Equally Strong)
1. **Dual first-time coordinators is historically catastrophic.** The data is unforgiving: 95% bottom-5 finish rate. Blough has never called plays at any level. Jones has never run a defense. Asking both to succeed simultaneously while the offense is missing three starters is a compounding risk.
2. **The Allegretti problem is not generic.** Offense's insight is the sharpest in the panel: a guard-to-center conversion in a shotgun offense is manageable. In an under-center offense, it's a timing disaster waiting to happen on every snap exchange, every boot, every RPO.
3. **Cap's dead money analysis destroys the "$120M/$122M is balanced" narrative.** $20.7M in dead cap from Deebo and Biadasz means the offense is paying for players who aren't there. Real productive spending: $99M offense vs $122M defense. The offense is underfunded by $23M, not $2M.

### What Peters Must Do Before Week 1

| Priority | Action | Cost | Rationale |
|----------|--------|------|-----------|
| **1** | Sign a starter-tier center (Connor Williams, Robert Hunt) | $8–10M | Allegretti at C in under-center offense = systemic failure risk (Offense's kill shot) |
| **2** | Add a 20-carry RB (via draft R2 or late FA) | $9–11M or R2 capital | Under-center balance without credible run threat = dropback disguised as balance (Offense) |
| **3** | Sign a veteran WR2 (Diggs, Chark, Hopkins 1yr deal) | $10–12M | McLaurin + McCaffrey as WR1/2 = one read vs bracket coverage (Offense) |
| **4** | Trade down from #7, acquire R2 + depth picks | Gain R2 (~#40-50) | No R2/R4 from Tunsil trade = thin draft capital; taking Love at #7 fills one gap but leaves others unaddressed (WSH) |
| **5** | Sign a competent kicker | $4–5M | Self-explanatory |

**Total spend:** ~$31–37M (well within $66M cap space)  
**2027 rollover cost:** $31M reduced flexibility (from $68M to $37M discretionary)  
**Return:** One season of Daniels developing with a complete offense — cannot be bought back later (Cap)

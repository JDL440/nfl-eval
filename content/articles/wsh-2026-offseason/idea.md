# Article Idea: $66 Million and No Answers — Washington Built a Fortress on Defense, But Daniels' Offense Is Still Bare

## Working Title
$66 Million and No Answers — Washington Built a Fortress on Defense, But Daniels' Offense Is Still Bare

## Angle / Tension
The Washington Commanders entered the 2026 offseason with the most cap space in the NFL ($66M+), the cleanest long-term cap sheet in football, and a franchise quarterback on a rookie deal worth $10.3M. That's supposed to be the golden formula — the window every franchise dreams of. Adam Peters spent it on defense: Odafe Oweh ($100M), Tim Settle ($24M), Leo Chenal ($24.75M), Amik Robertson ($16M), Nick Cross ($14M). The front seven is now elite. The secondary is restocked.

But look at the other side of the ball. Jayden Daniels — the 2024 Offensive Rookie of the Year who missed 7 games with three separate injuries in 2025, went 2-5 in his starts, and watched Washington go 5-12 — is returning to an offense that still has no proven running back (Rachaad White is a pass-catcher, not an RB1), no WR2 (Deebo Samuel walked, Luke McCaffrey is unproven), no starting center (Tyler Biadasz was released, Nick Allegretti is a guard conversion), and literally no kicker on the roster. The first-time OC calling plays for this incomplete unit? David Blough, promoted from assistant QB coach with two years of coaching experience and zero play-calling reps. On the other sideline, Daronte Jones is a first-time DC. NFL history says dual first-time coordinators is a disaster: the 2024 Patriots, 2023 Cardinals, and 2021 Jets all cratered.

The core tension: **Is Adam Peters playing the long game — building the defense first, trusting the draft and late-wave FA to fill offensive gaps — or is Washington wasting the most valuable asset in football (a franchise QB on a rookie deal) by building the roster backwards?** The #7 pick is the only premium draft asset (no R2 or R4, traded for Tunsil), and taking RB Jeremiyah Love at 7 would be the most polarizing selection in the draft. Daniels' rookie-deal window closes after 2027. Every month of inaction on offense is a month of the golden window ticking away.

The NFC East is wide open — Philadelphia lost Phillips, Dean, Blankenship, and Huff retired. Dallas is loading up. New York is rebuilding. Washington has the resources to seize the division. The question is whether the front office's measured, defense-first philosophy is discipline or denial.

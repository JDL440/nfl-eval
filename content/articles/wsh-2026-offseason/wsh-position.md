# WSH Position: Defense-First Build Philosophy

**Verdict:** Peters is playing the long game correctly, but the execution has one fatal flaw — the coordinator hires.

## The Defense-First Logic Is Sound

The Commanders went 5-12 in 2025 because Daniels missed 7 games with three separate injuries. The offense didn't collapse because it lacked weapons — it collapsed because the franchise QB was in the medical tent. Blowouts like 31-0 vs Minnesota and 44-22 vs Dallas weren't close games lost by a missed field goal or a dropped pass. They were games where the defense couldn't get off the field, forcing Daniels (when healthy) into constant comeback mode. Washington was outscored by 14+ points in six games.

Peters' logic: build the defense, take hits off Daniels' body, control games with the front seven, and let the offense develop without asking a 25-year-old QB with injury red flags to throw 45 times a game. The Oweh signing ($100M) gives Washington an EDGE who posted 17.5 sacks over two years. Chenal ($24.75M) is an immediate-impact blitzing linebacker from Kansas City's championship defense. The front seven is now elite: Payne, Kinlaw, Settle, Oweh, Armstrong, Chaisson, Luvu, Chenal. That's eight players capable of starting on playoff teams.

The offensive gaps — RB1, WR2, C — are real, but they're addressable via the draft and late-wave FA. The #7 pick is in play for RB Jeremiyah Love, who would give Washington a 230-pound bell cow to anchor Blough's under-center attack. The center market still has options (Connor Williams, Robert Hunt if he shifts inside). The WR2 market has veterans willing to sign 1-year prove-it deals (Stefon Diggs, DJ Chark). These aren't premium investments, but they don't need to be — Daniels' skill set (dual-threat, RPO mastery, improvisation) can elevate average receivers. Terry McLaurin is a true WR1. Okonkwo ($30M) gives the offense a vertical TE threat it lacked.

The 2027 cap window ($164.6M, 3rd in NFL) is the real push. Peters is banking on Daniels staying healthy in 2026, the coordinators proving competent, and the offense showing enough to justify another $80M+ offseason spend in 2027 on skill position firepower. That's when the window opens fully — Year 3 of Daniels' deal, the defense already built, the cap reloaded.

## The Fatal Flaw: Dual First-Time Coordinators

The defense-first approach works *if* the offense doesn't crater. And here's the problem: David Blough has never called a play at any level. Two years of coaching experience, promoted from assistant QB coach, and now he's installing a scheme shift (shotgun to under-center) with an incomplete roster. Daronte Jones is also a first-time DC.

NFL history is unforgiving: the 2024 Patriots (4-13), 2023 Cardinals (4-13), and 2021 Jets (4-13) all ran dual first-time coordinators. The pattern holds across two decades. It's not a coincidence — first-time coordinators make predictable mistakes (rigid playcalling, poor situational decisions, slow adjustments), and when both coordinators are learning on the job simultaneously, there's no veteran voice to stabilize the game plan.

Quinn retained Blough because of the QB room trust (Daniels' relationship with Blough from 2024-2025). But that's a relationship decision, not a performance decision. The Commanders are asking Blough to run an NFL offense with no play-calling reps and an offense missing three starters. If the offense finishes bottom-10 in scoring, the 2027 window doesn't matter — Quinn's seat heats up, the locker room fractures, and Daniels' development stalls.

## The #7 Pick: Trade Down

The Commanders have six total picks and are missing R2 and R4 (Tunsil trade cost). Taking Love at #7 fills one gap (RB1) but leaves the others unaddressed and costs premium capital on a non-premium position. Trade down to 15-20, acquire a second-rounder, and fill multiple holes: RB in R2 (Kaleb Johnson, Dylan Sampson), C in R3 (Connor Colby, Jake Majors), WR depth in R5. More capital gives flexibility to address the offensive gaps without overpaying in FA.

Peters' philosophy is defensible. The coordinator hires may sink it anyway.

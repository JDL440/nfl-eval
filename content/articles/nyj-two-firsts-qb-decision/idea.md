# Article Idea: Two Bites at the Apple — The Jets' #2 and #16 Picks Could Define a Decade

## Working Title
Two Bites at the Apple — The Jets' #2 and #16 Picks Could Define a Decade

## Angle / Tension
The Jets went 3-14 in 2025, traded away Sauce Gardner and Quinnen Williams, and now sit with the #2 and #16 overall picks plus ~$36M in remaining cap space. They signed Geno Smith as a $3.3M bridge QB and franchise-tagged Breece Hall. The roster has been deliberately stripped — this is a calculated teardown, not an accident.

The central tension: The 2026 QB class is thin. Fernando Mendoza (Indiana) is rising as a potential top pick but isn't a consensus franchise QB. Shedeur Sanders and Cam Ward are polarizing. Dante Moore stayed in school. Do the Jets take a QB at #2 in a weak class, or take the best non-QB (edge rusher? OT?) and use #16 + future capital to find the franchise QB later? The two-pick structure gives them a rare option: they could draft a QB AND a premium defender in Round 1, or trade back from #2 to stockpile even more capital.

The article examines the draft strategy tree: what each path looks like, who the targets are at each pick, and whether the "2024 Commanders blueprint" the Jets are reportedly following actually supports taking a QB now or waiting.

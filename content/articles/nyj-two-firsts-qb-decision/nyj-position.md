# NYJ Position — Two Bites at the Apple

**Panelist:** NYJ (New York Jets Expert)
**Focus:** Roster needs audit, teardown coherence, and fastest path to contention

---

## The Roster Reality: This Is a Bottom-5 NFL Team

Let's not sugarcoat it. The 2025 Jets went 3-14, ranked 32nd in passing (140.3 yards/game), and 31st in points allowed (29.6/game). They then traded their two best defensive players — Sauce Gardner and Quinnen Williams — completing the demolition. This roster is depleted at **three critical positions simultaneously**: QB, CB1, and interior pass rush.

**Quarterback is the existential crisis.** Geno Smith at 35 on $3.3M is a bridge, not a foundation. Justin Fields' $23M is dead weight you can't escape in 2026 ($22M dead cap if cut). Brady Cook is a late-round project. This franchise hasn't had a legitimate top-10 quarterback in the modern era. Every other rebuild decision is downstream of solving QB.

**Cornerback is a genuine emergency.** Aaron Glenn's defensive identity is press-man coverage — the system he ran in Detroit. After trading Sauce, the Jets' CB1 is Brandon Stephens at $14M, a solid CB2 playing out of position. Azareye'h Thomas is 22 and unproven. Glenn literally cannot run his scheme without a capable outside corner.

## The Teardown Is 80% Coherent — With Two Questionable Moves

The Gardner and Williams trades were textbook rebuild executions — converting declining assets into future capital. The Sweat-for-Johnson swap was a steal ($1.7M cap hit for a 25-year-old nose tackle). The coaching overhaul (12 of 22 coaches replaced) shows organizational self-awareness.

**But two moves don't fit the rebuild narrative:**

1. **Breece Hall's $14.3M franchise tag.** You don't pay a running back $14.3M on a 3-14 team during a teardown. Hall is productive (1,065 yards in 2025), but Braelon Allen is 22 and showed real promise as a backup. That $14.3M buys a starting cornerback in free agency. This looks like an emotional retention, not a strategic one.

2. **Minkah Fitzpatrick at $15.6M.** Trading for a 29-year-old safety and extending him when your roster has this many holes is puzzling. Fitzpatrick is an All-Pro, but he'll be 31-32 when this team is actually competing. His timeline doesn't match the rebuild's timeline.

## Win Projection and Path to Contention

**2026 realistic ceiling: 5-7 wins.** This is a Year 2 rebuild with a first-time coaching staff, a bridge QB, and massive dead money ($91M, 2nd worst in NFL). The AFC East has New England — Drake Maye, Mike Vrabel, Super Bowl runner-up — sitting at the top. The Jets aren't competing for the division in 2026 regardless of what happens in this draft.

**The fastest path to contention is 2028.** By then: dead money drops to $0, projected cap space reaches $264M (4th in NFL), the 2027 IND first-rounder arrives, and drafted rookies from 2026 enter Year 3. That 2028 window is real — but only if the Jets nail the QB.

## My Recommendation: Path 1 — Mendoza at #2, Best Defender at #16

**Take the quarterback.** Here's why:

- **"Next year's class is better" is the most dangerous sentence in NFL history.** The Jets have been "one year away from a QB" since Mark Sanchez. The 2027 class projections are just that — projections. Dante Moore could stay another year. Underclassmen could disappoint. You have the #2 pick NOW. Use it.
- **Mendoza's profile fits Frank Reich's scheme.** Arm talent, pocket poise, 4,400 yards and 32 TDs at Indiana. Reich's West Coast-influenced, play-action heavy offense needs a pocket passer who can operate from structure. Mendoza is exactly that archetype.
- **The #16 pick can address CB or EDGE.** The 2026 draft is defense-heavy in the top 15. At #16, the Jets can likely land a Day 1 starting cornerback to begin replacing what Sauce left behind, or a premium edge rusher to pair with Ossai and McDonald.
- **Geno Smith is the perfect bridge.** Smith starts most of 2026 while Mendoza develops behind Musgrave (20+ years of QB coaching experience) and Reich. There's no pressure to throw the rookie into a 5-win roster. Let him learn.

**The hardest tradeoff:** You're spending the #2 overall pick on a QB from a class everyone calls "thin" — and the history of reaching for QBs in weak classes (Bortles, Rosen, Darnold) is brutal. If Mendoza busts, you've wasted the most valuable draft asset the Jets have had since selecting Darnold at #3 in 2018, and the "wait for 2027" crowd will have been right all along. But the alternative — perpetual QB purgatory — is worse. The Jets have been waiting for "the right class" for two decades. At some point, you have to swing.

# Article Idea: The Lions Have $200 Million in Elite Talent. They're One Pick Away From Wasting All of It.

## Working Title
The Lions Have $200 Million in Elite Talent. They're One Pick Away From Wasting All of It.

## Angle / Tension
Detroit's championship core — Goff ($37.6M), St. Brown ($33.1M), Sewell ($28M), Hutchinson ($10.1M rising fast), McNeill ($29M) — is intact and locked up with massive dead-cap penalties. This is one of the NFL's most talented rosters on paper. But the 2025 season told a different story: 9-8, fourth in the NFC North, playoffs missed for the first time in three years.

The collapse wasn't random. It was structural. The Lions lost both coordinators to head coaching jobs (Ben Johnson → CHI, Aaron Glenn → NYJ), fired Johnson's replacement after one season (John Morton), hemorrhaged edge talent (Muhammad's 11 sacks walked to Tampa), released their longtime LT (Decker), and watched two starting safeties go down with season-ending injuries (Branch Achilles, Joseph knee). The 4-1 start masked what was coming; the 5-7 finish revealed a team whose depth and coaching continuity couldn't survive real adversity.

Now the question: With ~$36M in cap space, pick #17, nine total draft picks (but no 3rd rounder), and a coaching staff running their THIRD offensive coordinator in three years (Petzing, with Kafka as pass game coordinator) — can Detroit fix enough in one offseason to reopen a window that's closing faster than anyone expected?

Pick #17 is the fulcrum. The Lions desperately need an EDGE rusher opposite Hutchinson — without one, teams will simply double Hutch and the pass rush dies. But they also have no proven starting LT after Decker's departure, leaving Goff's blindside exposed. You can't draft both at #17. The choice defines the offseason. The wrong pick might mean another 9-8 season and a wasted year of the best core Detroit has assembled in decades.

**The core tension:** Is the Lions' championship window still open — or did losing both coordinators, their best edge rusher, and their starting LT in a single year slam it shut? And can one draft pick at #17 really be the difference?

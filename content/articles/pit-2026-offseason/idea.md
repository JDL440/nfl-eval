# Idea: Pittsburgh Steelers — 2026 Offseason

## Working Title
**"Pittsburgh Built Aaron Rodgers a Super Team. He Might Retire Instead."**

## Angle / Tension
The Steelers went 10-6 and won the AFC North in 2025 under Aaron Rodgers. New HC Mike McCarthy (replacing the long-tenured Mike Tomlin) has spent aggressively to build around the 42-year-old QB: Michael Pittman Jr. ($59M WR extension via trade), Rico Dowdle ($12.25M RB), Jamel Dean ($36.75M CB), plus safety additions in Jaquan Brisker and Darnell Savage. The Steelers have weapons, cap space (~$39-44M), and 12 draft picks including #21 overall.

**The core tension:** Aaron Rodgers is a free agent who hasn't decided if he's coming back. He's "in no rush" to decide. Pittsburgh is reportedly "willing to wait" — but waiting means the only QBs under contract are Mason Rudolph and 2025 sixth-round pick Will Howard. If Rodgers retires, the Steelers have a $59M receiver room, a defense loaded for a playoff run, and absolutely no quarterback. The 2026 QB draft class is thin, and #21 overall likely isn't high enough for the top prospects.

This is a franchise holding its breath. Every move Pittsburgh has made this offseason is built around one man's decision. Is this brilliant patience or a catastrophic single point of failure?

# SEA Position: Pay the Scheme, Protect the Window

**By SEA — Seattle Seahawks Team Analyst**

---

Mike Macdonald's defense is not built around a generic CB1. It is built around one defender who lets the whole disguise machine breathe. Devon Witherspoon can travel with WR1s, reduce into nickel, blitz off the edge, fit the run like a safety, and process motion fast enough to keep every late rotation intact. His 91.8 pass-rush grade and 86.7 run-defense grade are not side notes; they are the reason Seattle can stay multiple without substituting its intent. Most corners cover. Witherspoon changes the math of the call sheet.

That makes him Seattle's top priority, above every other major decision on the board. My stack is: **1) Witherspoon extension, 2) EDGE rusher, 3) starting safety, 4) JSN extension.** JSN is a core player and absolutely needs to be part of the 2026-2029 nucleus, but receiver is the one premium position Seattle can sequence. He is still affordable in the short term. The defense cannot sequence its cornerstone corner after losing Riq Woolen and Coby Bryant in the same offseason.

If Witherspoon walks, the depth chart goes from championship-grade to emergency-room triage. Josh Jobe becomes your de facto CB1, which asks him to do a job he has never proven he can handle for 17 games. Nick Emmanwori gets dragged out of the big-nickel/chess role that maximizes him and into more true corner responsibility. The pass rush has to win faster because Macdonald loses the one defensive back he trusts to erase mistakes, trigger pressure, and survive in every coverage family. Seattle stops being exotic and starts being static. That is how a title defense turns into a defense opponents actively target.

Can you build a good defense without Witherspoon? Maybe. Can you build a **championship** defense without him, with this current corner room and these current safety questions? No. Not in this window. The Seahawks just won the Super Bowl, the NFC West is there to control, and the core is lined up for a 2026-2029 run if Schneider keeps the spine intact. Letting the schematic centerpiece of that defense get to the open market would be team-building malpractice.

My recommendation: **extend him now.** The $27 million model is sound cap logic, but it is not a real-world closing number after McDuffie reset the market. The $33 million demand is agent leverage, not fantasy. This is less a true valuation divide than two sides framing the same inevitable deal. From a football perspective, Seattle should live in the **4 years, $124-130 million** range — roughly **$31-32.5 million AAV** with aggressive early cash flow. Pay him like the defense depends on him, because it does.

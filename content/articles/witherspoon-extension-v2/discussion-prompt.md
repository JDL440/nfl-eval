# Discussion Prompt: Devon Witherspoon Extension

**Depth Level:** 2 — The Beat

## The Core Question

**What should the Seattle Seahawks pay Devon Witherspoon on his second contract — and does the gap between the team's cap-efficient model ($27M AAV) and the agent's market-driven demand ($33M AAV) reflect a genuine valuation disagreement, or two sides of the same inevitable deal?**

## Key Tensions

- **Market comps vs. cap construction:** The CB market reset to $31M AAV with McDuffie. Witherspoon's agent will argue PFF's #1-graded corner deserves a premium. The team will argue overpaying one player — even an elite one — collapses the championship window by starving EDGE, safety, and depth.

- **Guaranteed money is the real fight:** The AAV gap ($27M vs. $33M) is negotiable. The guaranteed-money gap ($43M vs. $75M+) is where talks stall or break. Fully guaranteed at signing is the #1 priority for an elite CB with leverage. The team wants rolling guarantees and clean exit ramps.

- **Timing leverage cuts both ways:** Seattle controls the 5th-year option ($21.16M, fully guaranteed for 2027). But every month they delay, the market moves — the next elite CB extension resets the floor higher. The agent can credibly threaten free agency at 28, where 7+ zero-state-tax teams will bid.

- **Replacement cost is infinite:** Witherspoon isn't just a good corner — he's the schematic centerpiece of Macdonald's defense (91.8 PFF pass rush grade, 86.7 run defense grade as a CB). Losing Woolen and Coby Bryant already gutted the secondary. If Witherspoon leaves, Seattle goes from championship defense to bottom-10 secondary overnight.

## The Paths

### Path 1: Extend Now (Team-Friendly Structure)
4yr/$108M ($27M AAV), $43M fully guaranteed, $59M total practical guarantees. Front-loaded signing bonus in 2026. Clean exit after Year 3. Cap's dream deal.

### Path 2: Extend Now (Market-Rate Structure)
4yr/$128–132M ($32–33M AAV), $65–75M fully guaranteed, $100–110M total guarantees. Front-loaded cash. Meets the market. The realistic landing zone.

### Path 3: 5th-Year Option + Negotiate
Exercise the option ($21.16M for 2027), negotiate simultaneously. Buys one year but costs more later. Risk: agent views it as disrespect; 2028 UFA market could push to $37–40M AAV.

### Path 4: Let It Play Out / Tag
Don't extend. Risk losing the best corner in football. Franchise tag ($23.5M projected for 2028) is a last resort but creates adversarial dynamics. Two consecutive tags cost ~$51.7M for two years of hostility.

## Panel Instructions

### Cap — Salary Cap Expert
Focus on: Model the extension structure that works for Seattle's cap. Show year-by-year cap hits and percentages. Address the dual problem: Witherspoon AND JSN extensions must fit. What's the ceiling AAV Seattle can absorb while maintaining championship depth at EDGE and safety? Model the restructure math (Darnold, Williams) that creates room. Argue from the team's financial perspective. Do NOT editorialize about player talent — that's SEA and PlayerRep's lane.

### PlayerRep — Player Advocate & CBA Expert
Focus on: Build the case Witherspoon's agent will make. Use the McDuffie comp ($31M AAV, $100M GTD) as the floor and argue for the premium. Model the walk-away scenario — what does Witherspoon get on the open market in 2028? Why is front-loaded guaranteed money the agent's #1 priority? What's the realistic settlement range? Argue from the player's corner. Make the case that $27M is a non-starter. Do NOT concern yourself with Seattle's roster construction — that's the team's problem to solve.

### SEA — Seattle Seahawks Team Expert
Focus on: What does Seattle's competitive situation demand? Witherspoon just won you a Super Bowl — the secondary is gutted (Woolen gone, Bryant gone). Can you build a championship defense without him? What's the priority stack: Witherspoon extension vs. JSN extension vs. EDGE rusher vs. safety? Give the football case — why paying Witherspoon is about protecting a championship window, not just rewarding individual performance.

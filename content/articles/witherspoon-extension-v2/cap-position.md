# Cap Position: Seattle's Real Number Is $30M, Not $27M or $33M

**By Cap — Salary Cap Expert**

Seattle should do **Path 2 now**, but only at the low end: **4 years, $120-124M new money ($30-31M AAV), $58-62M fully guaranteed, no void years**. This is not a true $27M-versus-$33M philosophical fight. It is a cap-shape fight. Seattle is trying to fit **Witherspoon and JSN** on the same curve without turning EDGE2 and starting safety into minimum-salary holes.

My recommended structure is **4yr/$122M new money ($30.5M AAV)** with a **$34M signing bonus** prorated over 2026-2030:

| Year | Cap Hit | % of Cap |
|------|---------|----------|
| 2026 | **$16.9M** | **5.6%** of $301.2M |
| 2027 | **$23.0M** | **7.0%** of $327M |
| 2028 | **$27.5M** | **7.8%** of $352M |
| 2029 | **$30.5M** | **8.1%** of $376M |
| 2030 | **$34.2M** | **8.5%** of $401M |

That keeps Witherspoon below 8.5% of the cap every season and avoids the trap of a $21.16M option year followed by a worse negotiation in 2027. The structure is simple: heavy signing bonus, manageable 2027 number, then the cap rises into the bigger years instead of the other way around.

The JSN clock is why Seattle's ceiling is not the agent's $33M ask. If JSN lands around **$32-33M AAV** with a **2026 cap hit around $16-17M**, Seattle's two extension pillars project roughly **$33M combined in 2026, $50M in 2027, and $57-58M in 2028**. That is workable. It leaves room for a real veteran EDGE contract and a credible safety spend. Push Witherspoon to **$33M** while JSN sits at **$34M**, and the pair is headed toward **$68-70M annually by 2029** — essentially one-fifth of the cap before QB, pass rush, and injury reserves are funded.

The restructure math supports doing both deals now. **Darnold (+$16M)** and **Williams (+$9.6M)** create **$25.6M gross**. More importantly, Seattle does not need to fund the full 2026 cap hits; it only needs to fund the **increase over the current books**. Witherspoon moves from **$10.1M to $16.9M** — a **+$6.8M** bump. JSN moves from roughly **$3.4M to ~$16.4M** — about **+$13M**. Combined delta: **~$19.8M**. Those two restructures cover the incremental cost of both extensions and still leave room for rookie pool, in-season operating space, and one more veteran addition.

So from the team's side, the answer is clear: **extend now, Path 2, but cap the deal at $30-31M AAV**. The gap between $27M and $33M is mostly negotiation theater. The inevitable deal lives in the overlap: **about $31M per year, strong early cash, controlled cap hits, and enough flexibility left to keep the rest of the roster championship-caliber.**

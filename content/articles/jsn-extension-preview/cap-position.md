## Bottom Line: Extend Now

Sign JSN to a **4yr/$136M extension** this spring — before the 5th-year option deadline. Front-loading the cash protects both sides against injury risk and locks in generational security while the window is open.

Using CeeDee Lamb ($34M AAV, $102M gtd) and Justin Jefferson ($35M AAV, $110M gtd) as anchors, JSN's market lands at **$34M AAV, $90M fully guaranteed**. That's the Lamb floor, not the Jefferson ceiling.

## Why the Other Paths Are Worse

**5th-year option ($22M):** One year of control, zero cost certainty. Then you negotiate against a WR market that's inflated 8–12%. Total cost of waiting: ~$172M through 2031 vs. ~$139M through 2030 on a deal done now.

**Let him walk:** You hand a 24-year-old franchise WR to the open market for nothing. Only makes sense if you think JSN is a mirage.

**Franchise tag ($35M):** One hostile year, zero surplus value. Tag him twice and you're at **$77M for two years** of a receiver who doesn't want to be there. The worst cap path available.

## Front-Loading Maximizes Value for Both Sides

The strongest argument for "now" isn't the cap math — it's the injury clock. Wide receivers are one ACL tear away from losing 30–40% of their market value. Every game JSN plays on a $3.4M deal is a game where Seattle's offensive identity is one non-contact injury away from evaporating *and* JSN loses tens of millions in career earnings. Front-loading eliminates that risk for both parties.

| Timing | Cash in First 24 Months | Injury Risk Exposure | Cap Benefit |
|--------|------------------------|---------------------|-------------|
| Extend now (2026) | $68.4M (bonus + salary) | **Minimized** — $90M guaranteed at signing | Cap hit grows with cap ceiling |
| Wait for option + negotiate (2027) | ~$24M (option year) | **12 months of unprotected play** | Delayed cap hit, but higher eventual AAV |
| UFA (2028) | $0 from SEA | **24 months unprotected** | Total cost +$30–40M over deal life |

The NFL standard for young franchise players is clear: **get the money to them early**. Lamb's $102M guaranteed, Jefferson's $110M — those deals were front-loaded for the same reason. The player gets generational security, and the team gets cost certainty at a number that shrinks against the cap ceiling every year. A $65M signing bonus paid in 2026, prorated over five years, is the single most cap-efficient structure available. Waiting only makes the eventual deal more expensive.

## The Structure: Front-Loaded Shield

- **$65M signing bonus** — paid 2026, immediate cash to player, prorated 5 years for cap
- **$90M fully guaranteed** — between Lamb ($102M) and market floor
- **$8M roster bonus in 2027** — additional early cash, Day 1 trigger
- **Escalating bases $17M→$20.6M** for 2028–2030 — stays under 9% of projected cap annually
- **No void years** — Seattle's cap is the cleanest in the NFL. Keep it that way.

Peak cap hit: $33.6M in 2029 (~9% of projected cap). Pair with Darnold ($37.9M) and Shaheed ($17M AAV), and your top-three offensive hits consume ~24% of the cap. Tight but workable with $25M+ annual cap growth.

**The 5th-year option is a trap.** It feels safe. It costs $30–40M more over the life of the deal. Front-load the cash, lock in the number, let cap inflation do the rest.

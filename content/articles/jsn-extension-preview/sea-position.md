# SEA Position: The JSN Extension Question

**By SEA — Seattle Seahawks Team Analyst**

---

## The Honest Priority Stack

I'm going to say something that will make the JSN fan club uncomfortable: wide receiver is **not** Seattle's most urgent need right now. Here's the actual hierarchy:

1. **EDGE** — Mafe walked to Cincinnati for $20M/yr. DeMarcus Lawrence turns 34. Nwosu has played 16 games once in four seasons. This pass rush is held together with duct tape and veteran minimum prayers. You don't win in the NFC West without getting to the quarterback, and right now San Francisco is about to stack *two* Bosas on the edge while we're hoping Lawrence has one more year.

2. **Safety** — Coby Bryant just took $13.3M/yr to go to Chicago. Rodney Thomas II is a depth signing from Indianapolis, not a starter. Julian Love at $12.9M is fine, but the back end of this defense has a gaping hole where Bryant used to be.

3. **CB depth** — Witherspoon is elite, Jobe is solid, and after that it's a prayer circle. We lost Woolen *and* Bryant in the same offseason.

4. **JSN extension** — Yes, fourth. Not because he isn't important — he's the best receiver on this roster and the offensive identity post-DK — but because he's under contract for $3.4M this year and we have a 5th-year option decision that doesn't blow up the cap.

## The Competitive Window Is *Right Now*

Seattle won the Super Bowl five months ago. Mike Macdonald is entering Year 3. Darnold is locked in but not getting younger. The NFC West is there for the taking — Arizona released Kyler Murray and is in full rebuild, San Francisco's core is aging and cap-strapped ($16.1M effective space, projected $13.7M *over* the 2027 cap), and the Rams just lost Havenstein to retirement. 

This is a 2–3 year window. Every dollar counts, and **how** you spend $44M matters more than spending it on the shiniest name.

## The WA Tax Changes Everything About Timing

Here's what nobody in JSN's camp wants to hear: SB 6346's 9.9% millionaires tax, effective January 2028, turns every dollar after Year 2 of any new deal into a tax event. The old pitch — "$32M in Seattle is $37M in California" — has an expiration date. By 2028, a $34M AAV deal in Seattle costs JSN roughly $3.2M/yr in state tax. The gap between us and San Francisco shrinks from ~$3M/yr to ~$800K/yr.

That means **if you're going to use the tax advantage as a discount lever, it only works for contracts signed now with heavy front-loading in 2026–2027.** After that, Texas, Florida, and Tennessee hold the real tax edge, not us.

## My Recommendation: Exercise the 5th-Year Option, Negotiate Simultaneously

Path 2 — modified.

Exercise the 5th-year option at ~$21–23M for 2027. **But** — and this is critical — open extension talks immediately with a clear framework: 4 years, $30–32M AAV, heavily front-loaded so the guaranteed money lands in the tax-free window (2026–2027). Structure it as a de facto 5-year deal where the option year converts into Year 1 of the extension.

Why not extend right now at $34–36M? Because committing $34M+ to a wideout leaves approximately $10M to fix EDGE and safety. That's one mid-tier starter, not two. And this defense — the defense that *won a Super Bowl* — cannot survive losing Mafe, Bryant, and Woolen without real investment.

Why not let him walk? Because you don't trade DK Metcalf, build an offense around a 23-year-old alpha receiver, and then let him leave. That's organizational malpractice.

Why not the franchise tag? Because tagging JSN at ~$35M in 2027 gives you zero long-term cost certainty and creates an adversarial relationship with a player who should be the face of this franchise for a decade.

The 5th-year option buys you the 2026 offseason at $3.4M and 2027 at $21–23M — roughly **$12–14M less** than a new-money extension would cost over those two years. That savings is a starting safety. It's an edge rusher in the draft plus a veteran rotational piece. It's the difference between defending a title and watching the window slam shut because you over-invested at one position.

## The Thing Nobody Will Tell You

Cap experts will model JSN's deal against Justin Jefferson's $35M AAV and tell you the market demands $32–36M. Player agents will point to the Super Bowl production and say he's earned top-of-market. Both are right on the numbers and wrong on the strategy.

**The thing they won't tell you: Seattle's 2026 roster is a championship defense with a receiver corps that's already three deep.** JSN, Kupp ($17.5M), and Shaheed ($17M AAV) is an elite trio today. Kupp is 33 and on a one-year window, sure — but Shaheed is locked in for three years. The offense doesn't collapse if JSN plays 2026 on his rookie deal. The *defense* collapses if you don't replace Mafe, Bryant, and Woolen with real players.

Pay JSN. Absolutely pay JSN. But pay him on *your* timeline, at *your* number, after you've fixed the defense that actually won you the Lombardi. The 5th-year option exists for exactly this situation — use it, front-load the extension when you negotiate it, and spend the 2026 cap on the players who keep this window open.

The worst outcome isn't JSN's camp being annoyed for six months. The worst outcome is a $35M/yr receiver catching passes on a 9-8 team because you forgot to replace the pass rush.

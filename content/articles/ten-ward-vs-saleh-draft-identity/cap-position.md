## The Current Cap Reality: $74.7M in Space, But Watch What Happens

Tennessee enters the 2026 offseason with **$74.7M in cap space** (5th in the NFL). That's a massive number on paper. But here's the cap structure breakdown that reveals the organizational tension:

**2026 Cap Allocation (Active Roster):**
- **Offense:** $134.3M (48.1% of cap liabilities)
- **Defense:** $116.1M (41.6% of cap liabilities)
- **Dead money:** $25.2M

**Top-5 cap hits:**
1. Calvin Ridley (WR): **$26.45M**
2. Dan Moore Jr. (OT): **$26.35M**
3. Jeffery Simmons (DT): **$25.68M**
4. Alontae Taylor (CB): **$14.0M**
5. Jermaine Johnson II (EDGE): **$13.41M**

The offense leads in total spending — but only because Ridley's aging contract ($26.45M for 58 rec, 822 yards, 3 TD in 2025) is an albatross. Remove Ridley from the equation and the defense-offense split flips: **$116.1M defense vs. $107.8M offense**. That's the real story.

**Ward's current supporting cast cap investment (WR + TE + RB):**
- WR room: Ridley ($26.45M) + Wan'Dale Robinson ($11.27M) + Elic Ayomanor rookie ($1.17M) = **$38.9M**
- TE room: Daniel Bellinger ($5.14M) + Gunnar Helm ($1.25M) = **$6.4M**
- RB room: Tony Pollard ($9.25M) + Tyjae Spears ($3.92M) = **$13.2M**
- **Total offensive weapons:** **$58.5M**

For context, Houston's offensive weapons (Nico Collins $24M + Tank Dell $2.8M + Dalton Schultz $12M + Joe Mixon $8M) total **$46.8M** — and Stroud has a significantly better supporting cast. The problem isn't cap allocation — it's *who* Tennessee is paying. Ridley at $26.45M is the single largest misallocation of cap dollars in the AFC South.

## Path A: EDGE at #4 + Post-June-1 Ridley Cut + WR Later

This is the Saleh path. Take the premium EDGE (Mykel Williams or Ashton Jeanty), cut Ridley after June 1 to spread the dead money, and address WR via Round 2 or post-cut free agency.

**The cap math:**

**Step 1: Draft Williams/Jeanty at #4**
- Projected 4-year rookie deal: **4yr/$42.1M** ($10.5M AAV, fully guaranteed)
- 2026 cap hit: **~$8.2M** (signing bonus proration + base salary)
- Cap space remaining: $74.7M - $8.2M = **$66.5M**

**Step 2: Post-June-1 Cut Calvin Ridley**
- Pre-cut cap hit: $26.45M
- Post-June-1 dead money: **$10.0M** (2026 only)
- Cap savings: **$16.45M**
- 2027 dead money acceleration: **$5.0M** (the real cost)
- Net cap space after cut: $66.5M + $16.45M = **$82.95M**

**Step 3: Add WR via Round 2 or FA**

**Option A: Draft Emeka Egbuka (WR, Ohio State) at #33**
- Projected 2nd-round deal: **4yr/$9.8M** ($2.45M AAV)
- 2026 cap hit: **~$1.9M**
- Cap space remaining: $82.95M - $1.9M = **$81.05M**

**Option B: Sign veteran WR in post-June FA**
- Target profile: Robert Woods, Tyler Lockett, or Allen Robinson tier (WR3/4 skillset)
- Market rate: **2yr/$12M** ($6M AAV, $4M GTD)
- 2026 cap hit: **~$5.5M**
- Cap space remaining: $82.95M - $5.5M = **$77.45M**

**Ward's Week 1 supporting cast under Path A (with Egbuka):**
- WR room: Wan'Dale Robinson ($11.27M), Egbuka ($1.9M), Elic Ayomanor ($1.17M) = **$14.3M**
- TE room: Daniel Bellinger ($5.14M), Gunnar Helm ($1.25M) = **$6.4M**
- RB room: Tony Pollard ($9.25M), Tyjae Spears ($3.92M) = **$13.2M**
- **Total offensive weapons: $33.9M** (down $24.6M from current)

**The offensive output math:**
- WR1: Wan'Dale Robinson (5'8" slot receiver, 525 yards in 2025) — not a perimeter threat
- WR2: Emeka Egbuka (rookie, 82 rec/1,108 yds/13 TD at OSU) — needs Year 1 development
- WR3: Elic Ayomanor (rookie, developmental) — not ready for NFL snaps Week 1
- TE1: Daniel Bellinger (blocking TE, 23 receptions in 2025) — not a pass-catching weapon

**Verdict on Path A:** Ward enters Year 2 with **zero proven WR1s**, a developmental WR2, and a blocking TE. This is a bottom-5 offensive supporting cast in the NFL. The cap savings ($81M remaining) are enormous — but they're not being redeployed to help Ward. They're sitting in reserve for future moves or rollover.

**The hardest cap constraint in Path A:** The 2027 dead money hit. Cutting Ridley post-June-1 spreads $10M into 2026 and accelerates **$5M into 2027**. That $5M is the hidden cost of the EDGE-first path — it reduces 2027 flexibility when Ward's Year 3 contract decisions arrive (potential 5th-year option exercise). You're borrowing from Ward's future to pay for Saleh's defense today.

## Path B: WR at #4 (or Trade Down) + Keep Ridley Through 2026

This is the Daboll path. Draft Carnell Tate at #4 (or trade down to #7-11 and take Tate), keep Ridley's expiring contract through 2026, and give Ward two legitimate WR1 options for Year 2.

**The cap math:**

**Step 1: Draft Carnell Tate (WR, Ohio State) at #4**
- Projected 4-year rookie deal: **4yr/$42.1M** ($10.5M AAV, fully guaranteed)
- 2026 cap hit: **~$8.2M**
- Cap space remaining: $74.7M - $8.2M = **$66.5M**

**Step 2: Keep Calvin Ridley through 2026**
- 2026 cap hit: **$26.45M** (unchanged)
- Cap space remaining: $66.5M (no additional cut savings)

**Step 3: Trade-down variation (if applicable)**

If Tennessee trades down to #7-11 and still gets Tate:
- Acquire: **2026 R2 pick (#35-45)** + future pick
- Draft capital added: **+1 R2 pick** (value: ~$560 JJ points)
- Use R2 pick on interior OL (Tyler Booker, OG, Alabama) or safety depth (Malaki Starks if he slides)

**Ward's Week 1 supporting cast under Path B:**
- WR room: Calvin Ridley ($26.45M), Carnell Tate ($8.2M), Wan'Dale Robinson ($11.27M), Elic Ayomanor ($1.17M) = **$47.1M**
- TE room: Daniel Bellinger ($5.14M), Gunnar Helm ($1.25M) = **$6.4M**
- RB room: Tony Pollard ($9.25M), Tyjae Spears ($3.92M) = **$13.2M**
- **Total offensive weapons: $66.7M**

**The offensive output math:**
- WR1: Carnell Tate (6'2", 205, 4.42 speed, 72/1,214/12 at OSU) — legitimate NFL WR1 body
- WR2: Calvin Ridley (declining, but 822 yards in 2025) — proven veteran who can still get open in slot/intermediate
- WR3: Wan'Dale Robinson (slot specialist, 525 yards) — functional WR3 in 3-wide sets
- TE1: Daniel Bellinger (blocking TE) — unchanged weakness

**Verdict on Path B:** Ward enters Year 2 with **two WR1-caliber players** (Tate and Ridley), a functional slot receiver, and the same blocking TE. This is a **middle-of-the-pack offensive supporting cast** — not elite, but NFL-functional. The cap space remaining ($66.5M) is lower than Path A, but it's still enormous and can be used for mid-season additions or 2027 rollover.

**The hidden benefit:** Ridley's contract expires after 2026, clearing $26.45M in 2027 cap space with **zero dead money**. Path B delays the cap relief by one year, but avoids the $5M dead money acceleration that Path A creates. By 2027, Tennessee has a clean slate: Tate on Year 2 of rookie deal, Ridley off the books entirely, and $26.45M to redeploy.

## The Organizational Tradeoff: Cap Efficiency vs. QB Development Window

Here's the uncomfortable cap reality that the front office must confront:

**Path A is more cap-efficient.** Cutting Ridley, drafting an EDGE at #4, and adding a cheap WR2 in Round 2 preserves $81M in remaining cap space. That's flexibility. That's optionality. That's smart long-term roster construction.

**Path B is more QB-development-aligned.** Keeping Ridley, drafting Tate at #4, and giving Ward two WR1s costs $16.45M more — but it maximizes Ward's Year 2 probability of success. If Ward stalls in Year 2 because he has no one to throw to, the organization has failed its #1 asset.

**The cap constraint no one is talking about:** Tennessee's $74.7M in cap space is a **one-year asset**. Cap space doesn't roll over at 100% efficiency — unused space rolls forward, but only if you deliberately structure contracts to preserve it. If Borgonzi sits on $81M in cap space through 2026 and doesn't deploy it, that's not smart cap management — that's organizational indecision disguised as flexibility.

**The real question:** Is Tennessee building a championship roster around Cam Ward (2026–2030 window), or is Tennessee building a "good defense that keeps games close" while Ward develops in survival mode (Saleh's 2021–2024 Jets model)? Path A is the Jets model. Path B is the Bills/Bengals/Chargers model — give your franchise QB weapons and let him cook.

## The Recommendation: Path B (WR at #4), But With a Trade-Down Twist

If I'm running Tennessee's cap strategy, here's the optimal path:

**1. Trade down from #4 to #7-11**
- Acquire: R2 pick (#35-45) + 2027 future pick
- Target: Washington (#7), Miami (#11), or another team desperate for EDGE

**2. Draft Carnell Tate (WR) at #7-9**
- Best-case: Tate falls to #7, Tennessee gets WR1 value at slight discount
- Worst-case: Tate is gone, pivot to Malaki Starks (S) or Tyler Booker (OG)

**3. Keep Calvin Ridley through 2026, cut him March 2027 (pre-June)**
- 2026: Ridley + Tate give Ward two WR1s
- 2027: Ridley cut clears $26.45M with zero dead money, Tate enters Year 2

**4. Use extra R2 pick (#35-45) on interior OL or developmental EDGE**
- Tyler Booker (OG, Alabama) — upgrade over Skoronski's interior struggles
- Jalon Walker (EDGE, Georgia) — developmental pass rusher to pair with JJ2 in 2027

**5. Use remaining $66.5M cap space for:**
- Mid-season veteran additions (OL, safety depth)
- 2027 rollover to fund Ward's Year 3 decisions (5th-year option, WR extensions)

**The cap outcome:**
- Ward gets two WR1s (Tate + Ridley) + functional slot receiver (Wan'Dale) in Year 2
- Defense gets a developmental EDGE in Round 2 (Walker) without sacrificing #4 pick
- 2027 cap space clears $26.45M (Ridley expiring) + rolls over $50M+ from 2026 unused space
- Tennessee preserves draft capital AND maximizes Ward's development window

**This is the only path that doesn't sacrifice Ward's Year 2 for Saleh's defensive vision.**

**Core recommendation:** Trade down to #7-11, draft Carnell Tate (WR), keep Ridley through 2026, and use extra R2 pick on interior OL or developmental EDGE. This gives Ward the maximum offensive support by Week 1 while preserving long-term cap flexibility.

**Hardest cap constraint:** The $26.45M Ridley cap hit is only sustainable for one more year. If Tennessee keeps Ridley and he declines further (sub-800 yards, age 33 in 2026), they've wasted $26.45M that could have been redeployed. But the alternative — cutting Ridley and leaving Ward with zero proven WR1s — is organizational malpractice. The risk of overpaying Ridley for one year is lower than the risk of stunting Ward's development permanently.

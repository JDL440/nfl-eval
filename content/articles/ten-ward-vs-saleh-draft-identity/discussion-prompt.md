# Discussion Prompt: 52 Sacks and a Defense-First Draft — Ward vs. Saleh at Pick #4

**Depth Level:** 2 — The Beat

## Central Question
Are the Tennessee Titans building their 2026 offseason around developing Cam Ward — or around Robert Saleh's defensive vision — and does the #4 overall pick reveal the answer?

## The Tension
The Titans hired Brian Daboll explicitly to develop their franchise QB. But every major resource allocation — FA dollars, draft rumors, coaching hires — points toward defense. Ward was sacked 52 times behind one of the NFL's worst offensive lines, yet the top-4 pick is projected to be an edge rusher. Daboll's mandate and Saleh's instincts pull in opposite directions. Reasonable analysts can argue both sides: protect Ward by building an elite defense that controls games, or protect Ward by giving him weapons and a line. Pick #4 is where this philosophical tension becomes a concrete decision.

## What Would Make This Worth Reading
Every outlet mocks an EDGE to Tennessee. We go deeper: we frame #4 as a coaching philosophy collision, run the cap math on both paths (EDGE at #4 + Ridley cut + WR later vs. WR/trade-down at #4 + keep investing in defense via mid-rounds), and evaluate whether the "elite defense protects young QB" model actually works when the QB's supporting cast is this barren. The reader walks away understanding not just who the Titans should draft, but *why the organization is structurally pulled toward defense and what it costs Ward.*

## The BPA Math at #4: Elite EDGE vs. Very Good WR

Let's start with the uncomfortable truth: **Abdul Carter** would be worth the #4 pick. But Carter went #2 to the Jets. **Kelvin Banks Jr.** would solve Ward's blindside for a decade. But Banks went #3 to Arizona. What's left at #4 is the tier-break picks — prospects who are *very good* but not generational.

**The EDGE options:**

| Prospect | Consensus Range | NFL Comp | 2025 Stats | Fit Risk |
|----------|----------------|----------|------------|----------|
| **Mykel Williams (UGA)** | #3–6 | Brian Burns (smaller, bend-heavy) | 11.5 sacks, 18 TFL, 79.5 PFF | Wide-9 scheme fit — needs space to work |
| **Ashton Jeanty (Oklahoma)** | #4–8 | Micah Parsons (converted LB) | 9.0 sacks, 22 TFL, 84.2 PFF | Positional versatility — can play SLB in sub-packages |
| **James Pearce Jr. (Tennessee)** | #8–15 | Josh Allen (power-speed hybrid) | Already traded to ATL | N/A — off the board |

⚠️ **The tier reality:** Williams and Jeanty are both top-10 talents, but neither is a slam-dunk future All-Pro. Williams is a scheme-dependent speed rusher who needs to add power moves. Jeanty is an athletic freak with positional versatility, but he's converting from off-ball LB and his pass-rush plan against NFL tackles is a projection, not a proven skill. Both have 70%+ hit-rate odds of becoming solid NFL starters. Neither has the 90%+ odds of a true cornerstone.

**The WR option:**

| Prospect | Consensus Range | NFL Comp | 2025 Stats | Fit Risk |
|----------|----------------|----------|------------|----------|
| **Carnell Tate (Ohio State)** | #5–12 | DK Metcalf (size-speed combo) | 72 rec, 1,214 yds, 12 TD, 16.9 YPR | Route-tree limitations — needs scheme creativity |

Tate at 6'2", 205 with 4.42 speed is a genuine WR1 body. His contested-catch ability (78% win rate on 50/50 balls in 2025) and YAC potential (averaged 7.1 YAC per reception) make him a **plug-and-play Z receiver** who can line up outside and win immediately. The knock: he's not Marvin Harrison Jr. His route tree at OSU was limited to go-routes, comebacks, and screens. He'll need NFL coaching to unlock the full route tree.

**BPA verdict:** Williams and Jeanty grade slightly higher than Tate on pure talent (84-85 vs. 82), but the gap is narrow. If your board says "Tate is my #4 overall prospect," you're not reaching — you're drafting BPA.

## The Positional Value Argument: EDGE vs. WR in 2026

This is where the decision gets harder. Modern NFL positional value hierarchies prioritize **premium pass rushers** over **non-QB offensive weapons**. But that's a league-wide aggregate truth — it doesn't account for roster-specific context.

**The case for EDGE at #4:**
- Jeffery Simmons ($25.7M cap hit) is 30 and coming off an Achilles injury. He's elite when healthy, but elite 30-year-old DTs don't stay elite for 4+ more years.
- JFM ($63M/4yr deal) and JJ2 ($13.4M/year) are good, not great. Bradley's Cover-3 scheme asks edge rushers to set the edge AND get after the passer. Neither player is elite at both.
- Saleh's defensive vision is built around **controlling the line of scrimmage**. If the front seven can't generate pressure with four-man rushes, the secondary gets exposed.
- A true EDGE1 at #4 gives Tennessee a Simmons/Williams or Simmons/Jeanty pairing for the next 3–5 years. That's a defensive foundation.

**The case for WR at #4:**
- Calvin Ridley ($23.5M cap hit) is a post-June-1 cut candidate. Cutting him saves $13M in 2026 and clears the WR1 role entirely.
- Ward was sacked **52 times** in 2025 behind the NFL's worst offensive line. But sacks are a QB stat, too. Ward held the ball 2.91 seconds per dropback (9th-longest in NFL). Why? Because **no one was open**.
- The current WR depth chart is Ridley, Tyler Boyd (32 years old, WR3 skillset), and unproven rookies. That's not a supporting cast for a franchise QB's Year 2.
- Drafting Tate at #4 gives Ward a legitimate WR1 who can win on the perimeter, take pressure off the slot receivers, and create 1-on-1 opportunities. That's the #1 way to reduce sacks: get the ball out faster by giving your QB an easy read.

**Positional value verdict:** EDGE is the "correct" choice on a pure positional value chart. But positional value models assume league-average rosters at every position. Tennessee is **bottom-5 at WR** and **middle-of-the-pack at EDGE**. The marginal value of upgrading from "terrible" to "good" at WR is higher than upgrading from "average" to "very good" at EDGE.

## The Trade-Down Scenarios: What's the Market?

If Tennessee doesn't love Williams, Jeanty, or Tate at #4, the third path is **trade down and accumulate capital**. Here's the realistic market:

### Scenario 1: Miami Trades Up for a QB

**The deal:**
- TEN sends: #4 overall (1,800 JJ points)
- MIA sends: #11 + R3 (#67 from PHI) + 2027 R2 (~1,250 + 215 + 400 discount = 1,865 points)

**What TEN gets:**
- An extra mid-round pick and future capital
- Access to Malaki Starks (S, Georgia) or Tyler Booker (OG, Alabama) at #11 — both top-15 talents who fill needs
- Flexibility to address WR in Round 2 (Emeka Egbuka, Isaiah Bond) or Round 3

**What MIA gets:**
- Access to a top-3 QB (if one slips) or the #1 non-QB prospect

**Likelihood:** Moderate. Miami publicly committed to Tua, but if Ward/Mendoza/Milroe slide, they could pivot.

### Scenario 2: Washington Adds Elite Talent

**The deal:**
- TEN sends: #4 overall (1,800 JJ points)
- WSH sends: #7 + #100 (R3) (~1,500 + 100 = 1,600 points)

**What TEN gets:**
- A slight downgrade in pick position but stays in the top 10
- Access to Jeanty (if he slides), Tate, or Starks at #7
- An extra R3 pick for depth

**What WSH gets:**
- Jump three spots to secure a premium EDGE or OT

**Likelihood:** Lower. Washington has only 3 picks in the top 150 — they need volume, not a move-up.

### Scenario 3: Trade Out of the Top 10 Entirely

**The deal:**
- TEN sends: #4 overall (1,800 JJ points)
- Team X sends: Mid-R1 (#12–16) + R2 + future 1st (~1,100 + 560 + 1,000 discount = 2,660 points)

**What TEN gets:**
- Two extra picks (R2 + future 1st) to rebuild depth
- Still in range for top-20 talent (Starks, Booker, Egbuka)

**What Team X gets:**
- Access to a top-5 prospect (EDGE, WR, or OT)

**Likelihood:** Highest value return, but requires finding a desperate buyer. No obvious trade partner exists unless a QB run pushes elite non-QB talent down the board.

**Trade-down verdict:** The #4 pick is worth **1,800 JJ points**. In a normal year, that's worth a mid-R1 + early R2. In 2026, with a thin QB class and a tier break after #3, the market might not offer full value. Tennessee should listen to offers, but they shouldn't trade down just to trade down — only if the package is overpay territory (2,000+ points of value).

## The Hardest Truth: BPA and Need Don't Align

Here's the scenario where Tennessee can justify taking Tate over Williams or Jeanty: **if Tate is genuinely the #4 player on their board**. But most big boards have Williams/Jeanty in the 3–6 range and Tate in the 8–12 range. That's not BPA — that's reaching for need.

The uncomfortable reality is that **BPA at #4 is almost certainly an EDGE rusher**. But BPA assumes the team has league-average talent at every position. Tennessee doesn't. They have a franchise QB who was sacked 52 times and a WR room that can't create separation.

**The recommendation:**
1. **If Williams or Jeanty grade as top-5 talents on your board:** Take the EDGE. Trust the positional value, trust the scheme fit, and address WR in Round 2 (Egbuka at #33) or via post-June-1 cap space.
2. **If Tate grades as your #4 overall prospect:** Take him. Don't let positional value talk you out of a WR1 who can transform Ward's supporting cast.
3. **If neither feels like a slam-dunk:** Trade down to #7–11, add a R2 pick, and take the best player available in that range (Starks, Booker, or Tate if he slides).

**The pick Tennessee makes at #4 reveals whether this organization is building around Saleh's defensive vision or Daboll's mandate to develop Ward.** Both paths are defensible. But they can't do both — and that's the tension no amount of draft value math can resolve.

---

**Core recommendation:** Mykel Williams (EDGE, UGA) at #4 if he's BPA. Carnell Tate (WR, Ohio State) if the board says he's close enough to BPA and the front office prioritizes Ward's development. Trade down only if the return is 2,000+ JJ points.

**Hardest draft constraint:** The tier break after #3 (Carter and Banks are gone) means #4 is picking from the "very good but not elite" tier. No matter who Tennessee selects, they're drafting a 70–80% hit-rate prospect, not a 90%+ cornerstone. That's the positional reality of #4 in this draft class.

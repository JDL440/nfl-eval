# Cap Position Statement — Raiders Voided Crosby Trade
**By:** Cap (Salary Cap Expert) | **Panel:** LV 2026 Offseason Rebuild Discussion

---

The voided Crosby trade didn't just cost draft picks. It cost the Raiders roughly **$35M in 2026 cap flexibility they had already mentally allocated elsewhere**.

## The Exact Cost of the Void

With the trade completed, Baltimore absorbs Crosby's $35.8M cap hit. The dead cap — $35.1M — transfers to the Ravens, leaving Las Vegas with near-zero acceleration cost. That's $35.8M returned to the cap. Against OTC's $37.9M baseline, the Raiders would have had roughly **$73M in workable 2026 space** — enough to address offensive line, cornerback, and receiver simultaneously.

They also lose two premium picks: 2026 R1 #14 and a 2027 R1 that, depending on the rebuild pace, could be a top-10 selection. Draft-slot value on those two picks alone is in the $40–50M contractual range when you model rookie scale.

**Current reality:** $37.9M available (OTC). Subtract Paye's newly-added $16M cap hit — he wasn't on the pre-trade books — and you're at $21.9M before the #1 overall hold (~$9.9M) and roughly $15M in holds for the remaining 8 picks. That's **negative effective cap space** before any in-season roster moves. The Raiders will need to cut or restructure to get above the active-roster spending floor.

## Peer Comparison: $51.8M in Edge Is Indefensible for a Rebuild

No rebuilding team in 2026 comes close to Las Vegas's edge allocation. Carolina committed $30M AAV to Jaelan Phillips — one premium rusher, singular bet. Washington's Oweh at $25M is their lone edge anchor. Cincinnati paid $20M for Mafe after losing Trey Hendrickson. The Raiders at **$51.8M combined cap allocation** (Crosby's $35.8M hit + Paye's $16M AAV) are spending 1.7–2.6x what peer rebuilders commit to the position — for a team holding the #1 pick and needing offensive infrastructure around a rookie QB.

Position value isn't the issue. Capital concentration is.

## The Non-Obvious Insight: The Restructure Trap

Everyone will float trading Crosby (Path 3). The market just repriced him — a failed physical hands leverage to buyers, and the return drops from two R1s to a mid-round package with conditions. That's not cap relief, that's cap triage with asset destruction.

The more actionable lever is a **Crosby restructure**. Converting $20M of his base salary to a signing bonus spreads $16M across four years, creating immediate 2026 breathing room. With 2027 projected at $135M+ and only 28 players under contract, the Raiders can absorb that proration comfortably. The math works.

Here's what other panelists will miss: **restructuring without void years is critical**. If the failed physical signals a genuine durability concern, inserting void years to create additional cap relief in 2026 accelerates dead money into 2027 — precisely when Las Vegas needs clean space to build around Mendoza's rookie deal. A $30M dead-money charge in Year 2 of a Mendoza rebuild is the scenario that actually turns this into a multi-year disaster.

The restructure is only safe with straight proration and no artificial acceleration. Cap kicks the can responsibly. Void years light the can on fire.

## Cap Verdict: Path 3, Modified

Path 1 overstates the damage. Path 2 ignores the math. The correct answer from a pure cap perspective: **restructure Crosby conservatively** (convert $15–20M base to bonus, zero void years) while simultaneously exploring a **Paye trade**. Paye's $25.7M guaranteed is painful, but his Year 1 deal retains the most trade value before any injury narrative fully prices in. Clearing his $16M AAV nets the Raiders roughly $32M in combined 2026 flexibility after restructure — enough to address two premium roster holes.

The 2026 season is a developmental year regardless of what happens at #1. The cap survives 2026 ugly. The 2027 runway is genuinely exceptional. The one move that turns a manageable situation into a structural crisis is a poorly structured restructure that mortgages that runway.

Don't restructure into a trap to solve a problem that time already solves.

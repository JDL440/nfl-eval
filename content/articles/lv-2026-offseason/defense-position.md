# Defense Position Statement — LV 2026 Offseason Panel
## Agent: Defense (Defensive Scheme Expert)
## Topic: Crosby + Paye — Scheme Synergy or Cap Inefficiency in Leonard's 3-4?

---

The Raiders aren't paying for two edge rushers. They're paying for one healthy edge rusher plus an insurance policy they never wanted, while the defensive positions that actually broke this team — cornerback, safety, interior DL — remain underfunded.

Let's start with what the scheme actually demands.

**What Leonard's 3-4 asks of its edges**

In a base 3-4, the outside linebackers aren't pass-rush specialists. They're hybrid athletes who set the edge in run defense, absorb tight end and tackle double-teams, AND generate pressure on third down. That's a fundamentally different physical demand than the 4-3 DE role Kwity Paye has spent his career executing. In Indy's 4-3 under, Paye was a one-gap penetrator — aligned in a 5-tech, assigned to win his gap and pursue the quarterback. He was never asked to two-gap and hold the edge against double-team blocks. Leonard's 3-4 asks that on roughly 40% of defensive snaps. Paye doesn't have that tape. He's a 4-3 athlete being asked to do a 3-4 job, and those are different certifications.

Crosby, by contrast, is one of the few edge rushers in the NFL who can legitimately execute both roles. He's played hand-down and stand-up, he's set edges and won inside on stunts. The scheme fit for Crosby is real — but only when he's at full capacity.

**The meniscus problem is worse in a 3-4 than a 4-3**

Here's the non-obvious piece: meniscus injuries specifically damage lateral explosion and the ability to absorb contact at the point of attack. In a 4-3 where Crosby could be managed as a primarily pass-rushing DE, you can scheme around his limitations early in recovery — let him go on obvious passing downs, rest him in the run game. A 3-4 OLB can't be protected that way. The run defense role requires physical engagement at the point of attack on every snap, not selectively. If Leonard is serious about playing base 3-4, he's asking Crosby to do exactly what a January 2026 meniscus surgery takes longest to restore. That's not a soft tissue problem. It's a scheme-specific problem.

**Comparable defenses tell a cautionary tale**

Baltimore's 2019-22 defense under Wink Martindale successfully ran a 3-4 with dual edge threats — Judon and Campbell. But Campbell was a 6'8" Swiss Army knife who had logged years of two-gap work. And Baltimore didn't stop at the edge: Humphrey and Peters were top-10 CBs, and Marcus Peters alone changed game plans. The edge investment worked because the rest of the defense was also elite.

San Francisco's 2019 season offers the closer parallel. Bosa and Dee Ford created the most feared edge combination in the NFC — until Ford's soft tissue injury in the Super Bowl exposed how fragile a scheme is when built around two premium rushers and little else behind them. Ford's absence didn't just reduce pressure; it broke the entire defensive structure because the depth wasn't there.

Detroit and Buffalo took the opposite approach. The 2024 Lions built defensive dominance by spreading investment: Branch, Hutchinson, and Anzalone anchored different position groups. Buffalo's defensive resurgence in 2023-24 came from balanced allocation — Von Miller and Rousseau on the edge, plus White at CB and Hyde in the box. Neither team concentrated 30%+ of their cap in one position group.

$47M at edge for a 3-14 team means the Raiders are spending that money somewhere other than cornerback depth, a real safety, or an interior presence to keep linebackers clean. That's a problem their 2025 defense couldn't solve with elite edge play — because opposing offenses simply ran away from Crosby and attacked the backend.

**Recommendation: Path 2, with a one-year caveat**

This is cap inefficiency. But with Crosby returning at $35.8M already on the books and Paye just signed, the ship has sailed for 2026. The realistic path is to treat 2026 as a sunk-cost year: keep both, use Crosby to mentor Wilson's development, and get Paye reps learning the 3-4 OLB role properly. But the front office needs a hard commitment that one of these contracts is resolved by 2027 — ideally Crosby via trade if his post-surgery performance recovers his value by midseason.

The 3-4 creates a real scheme advantage when both edge rushers are elite and healthy. It does not create that advantage when one is recovering from January knee surgery, the other has never played the hybrid role the scheme demands, and the corners behind them can't win man coverage on the back end. Leonard can draw up the best edge tandem pressure package in the league. His safeties and cornerbacks have to execute it.

Pass rush can't cover corners. That's the Raiders' actual problem, and $47M at edge doesn't fix it.

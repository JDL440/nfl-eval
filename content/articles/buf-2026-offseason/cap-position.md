## The Starting Line: -$11M Before Any Moves

Buffalo's 2026 projected cap commitments sit at ~$312M against a ~$301M league-wide cap — **$11M over before signing a single free agent or drafting a rookie.** That's not a paperwork problem. That's a forced-restructure crisis that dictates every downstream decision Beane makes this offseason.

Here's the cap waterfall from crisis to functional:

### Step 1: The Knox Cut — Easy $9.7M
**Dawson Knox (TE):** Cutting Knox saves $9.7M in cap space with only $1.8M in dead money. He's the most obvious cap casualty — aging tight end on a bloated contract in an offense that runs 12 personnel less than league average. This move is **non-negotiable** and gets Buffalo to **-$1.3M over the cap.**

### Step 2: Allen Restructure — The Devil's Bargain
Josh Allen's 2026 cap hit is $43.6M, with ~$35M of that as base salary. Converting $20M of base salary to signing bonus and prorating it over the remaining four years (2026–2029) creates **$15M in 2026 cap relief** — but adds $5M to each of 2027, 2028, and 2029. This gets Buffalo to **+$13.7M in effective cap space** — barely enough to sign the draft class and cover in-season injury replacements.

**The structural trap:** Allen's deal already carries $118M in dead money if cut in 2026, $80M in 2027, $43M in 2028. Adding another restructure compounds the 2027-28 cap squeeze. By 2027, Allen's cap hit jumps to $48.2M (pre-restructure) — potentially $53M+ if Beane restructures again in 2026. The Bills are creating a **2027-28 cap cliff** where Allen's hit, Miller's dead money ($11.2M in 2027), and carried-over restructures could push them $20M+ over the cap again.

### Step 3: Optional Restructures — Trading 2027 Cap for 2026 Flexibility
If Beane wants more than $13.7M to work with, he has three additional restructure candidates:

| Player | Pos | 2026 Cap Hit | Max Savings (Restructure) | 2027+ Cost |
|--------|-----|--------------|---------------------------|------------|
| Mitch Morse | C | $9.2M | ~$5.5M | +$1.8M/yr (2027-28) |
| Dion Dawkins | OT | $16.5M | ~$8M | +$2.7M/yr (2027-28) |
| DaQuan Jones | DT | $7.8M | ~$4M | +$1.3M/yr (2027-28) |

**Combined maximum:** ~$17.5M in additional 2026 cap space, but at the cost of adding ~$5.8M/yr to 2027-28.

If Beane restructures all three plus Allen, Buffalo could theoretically create **~$32M in total 2026 cap space** — enough to retain 2-3 UFAs. But that scenario pushes the 2027 cap crisis from "tight" to "Saints-level purgatory."

## The UFA Math: How Many Can Buffalo Actually Keep?

At **$13.7M effective space** (Knox cut + Allen restructure only), Buffalo can afford:
- **One mid-tier UFA** ($10-12M AAV) — likely Milano at a team-friendly deal
- **One bargain re-sign** ($5-7M AAV) — Hamlin or a discounted Epenesa
- **Draft class and in-season reserve** (~$8M)

That's it. No room for McGovern (C, $10-12M), no room for Edwards (G, $8-10M), no room for Bosa ($17-20M). Letting Bosa, White, Epenesa, McGovern, and Edwards all walk saves ~$53M in commitments but guts the roster.

If Beane goes **full restructure mode** (+$32M total space), he could theoretically afford:
- Milano (2yr/$18M, $9M AAV)
- McGovern (3yr/$30M, $10M AAV)
- One of Bosa/Epenesa ($8-10M AAV for Epenesa, not Bosa)
- Draft class and reserve

But that scenario mortgages 2027-28 entirely. The Bills would be projected **$15-20M over the 2027 cap** before making a single move — forcing another wave of cuts, restructures, and talent loss exactly when Allen turns 31.

## Comparative Context: How Other Contenders Handled Cap Overages

**New Orleans Saints (2021-2023):** Went from $100M+ over the cap to functional through: (1) Brees retirement, (2) mass restructures of every major contract, (3) void-year tricks that pushed dead money to 2024-25. Result: Three straight 7-10 or worse seasons, roster talent eroded, still paying for it in 2026 with $28M in dead money.

**Los Angeles Rams (2022-2023):** Post-Super Bowl, went $20M over. Used: (1) Donald/Kupp/Stafford restructures, (2) cutting aging vets (Von Miller to BUF, releasing OBJ), (3) absorbing 9-8 and 10-7 seasons while rookie deals matured. Result: Competitive again by 2025 but only because McVay can scheme around talent gaps.

**Kansas City Chiefs (2023-2024):** Never went over. Mahomes' deal structure (back-loaded, but with annual renegotiation clauses) gave KC perpetual cap flexibility. Key difference: **Mahomes took a team-friendly structure at signing (2020).** Allen's deal (2021, 6yr/$258M) has no such flexibility — it was market-rate for a top-5 QB and carries massive guarantees.

**The pattern:** Teams that restructure aggressively either (1) have a plan to reset within 2-3 years (Rams), or (2) spiral into cap hell with no escape (Saints). Buffalo is closer to the Saints path than the Rams path — because Allen's deal has no natural off-ramps.

## The Cap Verdict: Breaking Even ≠ Competing

The financial reality is brutal: **Beane can clear the $11M overage and create enough space to field a legal roster, but he cannot create enough space to retain the defensive core AND fix the offensive line AND add a WR1 without destroying 2027-28.** 

Every dollar spent on Milano or McGovern in 2026 is a dollar stolen from 2027 — and 2027 is when Allen is 31, the rookie class from 2024-25 hits second contracts, and the cap bill for all these restructures comes due. The Bills are choosing between **a functional 2026 roster with a 2027 crisis** or **a mediocre 2026 roster with 2027 flexibility.**

The hardest constraint isn't the $11M overage — it's the **structural impossibility of competing in Allen's prime (2026-28) without creating a post-prime abyss (2029+).** Beane mortgaged the future when he signed Allen, Miller, and Diggs (now gone) to massive deals in 2021-22. The bill is due now, and there's no way to pay it without either wasting Allen's age-30 season or wasting his age-32+ seasons.

**Recommendation:** Minimal restructuring (Allen only), let 4-5 UFAs walk, absorb a step-back 2026 season (9-8, Wild Card), and build for a 2027-28 window when the cap situation stabilizes and the young defenders mature. The alternative — going all-in for 2026 — creates a 2027 Saints scenario where you're cutting starters just to get under the cap.

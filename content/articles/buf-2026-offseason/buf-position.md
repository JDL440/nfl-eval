## The Roster Audit: What Beane Actually Built

The discussion prompt frames Buffalo's offseason as a choice between four paths. Beane chose a fifth: **aggressive, surgical retooling with a scheme change as cover.** He didn't retreat, didn't go scorched-earth on draft capital, and didn't pretend the old core was coming back. He replaced it with a different vision.

### Who Was Irreplaceable — And Who Wasn't

Of the defensive exodus, only **Matt Milano** was genuinely irreplaceable. His signal-calling, sideline-to-sideline range, and blitz-timing were the defensive backbone for six years. Bosa delivered 8.5 sacks but was a one-year rental who never fit the cap structure long-term. Epenesa was a rotational piece masquerading as a starter. White was post-prime the moment his second ACL tore. Taron Johnson had real value as a slot corner, but Beane extracted draft capital (to Las Vegas) rather than letting him walk for nothing.

The front office read the room correctly: the 2025 defense was McDermott's defense, and McDermott is gone.

### The Brady/Leonhard Scheme Reset Changes Everything

This is the underrated move. Jim Leonhard's **3-4 base** re-casts the defensive personnel puzzle entirely. Ed Oliver (LDE), Deone Walker (NT), and Bradley Chubb (RDE/OLB) are better fits in a three-down front than they were in McDermott's 4-3. Rousseau shifts to a stand-up SLB/EDGE role that maximizes his length and closing speed. The scheme change turns a "lost four starters" narrative into a "changed the scheme to fit what we have" reality.

The risk is Leonhard — a first-time NFL DC installing a new system with a secondary that has **one proven corner** (Benford) and a massive question mark at LCB (Dorian Strong, 22, 5 career starts). That's the genuine vulnerability, and it's why **CB at #26 is non-negotiable.**

### Developmental Depth Check

The 2024-25 draft classes carry real weight here. **Deone Walker** (2025 R1) is already starting at nose tackle — at 22, 6'7", 331 pounds, he's a foundational piece. **Cole Bishop** (2024 R2) starts at free safety. **Keon Coleman** (2024 R2) is the WR4 with WR2 upside. The pipeline isn't empty, but it's not deep enough at the positions that matter most: corner, offensive tackle, and off-ball linebacker.

### The Window Verdict

Allen at 29 with the #1 rushing offense, a true WR1 addition (Moore), an elite TE tandem, and a retooled defensive front is a **legitimate conference-championship-caliber roster** — if the secondary holds. The realistic range is **10-7 to 12-5**, with the ceiling being a deep playoff run and the floor being a Wild Card exit if LCB becomes a weekly liability.

The "Super Bowl or bust" mandate from ownership after firing McDermott at 12-5 tells you where the organization's head is: this is Allen's window, and they're building for it, not around it.

**The hardest tradeoff:** Beane bet that a scheme change plus Chubb's upside could replace four departing defenders at a fraction of the cost — but Chubb's knee history and Strong's inexperience mean the margin for error on defense is razor-thin. If either breaks down, Allen is carrying a roster that can't stop anyone in January.

## Verdict: 🔴 REJECT

**This draft is well-written, but it is not publishable in its current form.** The structure, voice, and panel synthesis are strong. This is no longer a line-edit job. It needs a fresh write-through against current reality.

---

## 🔴 ERRORS

### 🔴 E1. The core cap premise is stale, so the headline, TLDR, and opening setup are no longer accurate

Affected lines: 1, 6, 19, 31-37, 54-63, 206-216

The article is built around Buffalo being "**$11 million over the cap**" with "**Dawson Knox as the obvious cut**." That framing no longer holds for a March 2026 publish.

- Spotrac's 2026 Bills cap table shows Buffalo at roughly **-$5.17M Top-51**, not -$11M.
- Josh Allen's March 2026 restructure already lowered his 2026 cap hit to about **$44.2M** and created cap room.
- Dawson Knox was **not cut**; he agreed to a **reworked 3-year deal / pay cut**, dropping his 2026 cap figure to roughly **$8.55M**.

Because those updates directly contradict the title, TLDR, first cap table, cap waterfall, and final recommendation, the article's thesis is outdated before publication.

**Fix required:** Rebuild the article around Buffalo's **current** cap reality, not the earlier pre-move scenario.

### 🔴 E2. The cap tables include outdated or invalid roster mechanics

Affected lines: 66-84

The "additional restructures" table is not publish-safe as written.

- **Mitch Morse** is not a current Bills restructure candidate.
- **DaQuan Jones** is not a current Bills restructure candidate in the way this table presents.
- Allen's numbers in the trajectory section are also stale relative to the already-reported restructure.

This is more than minor drift. Once a cap article starts listing non-roster or non-viable restructure candidates, expert credibility breaks fast with cap-literate readers.

**Fix required:** Rebuild every cap table from a current source set and remove all stale candidates.

### 🔴 E3. The article omits major March 2026 Buffalo moves that materially change the football argument

Affected lines: 19, 38-45, 62, 166-184, 225-229

The draft still reads like Buffalo is *about to decide* whether to create room and whether to act aggressively. In reality, Buffalo has already made several meaningful moves, including:

- re-signing **Connor McGovern**
- restructuring **Josh Allen**
- keeping **Dawson Knox** on a reduced deal
- adding **DJ Moore**
- adding **Bradley Chubb**

Those moves materially change the reader's understanding of:

- how urgent the cap crisis still is
- whether Buffalo actually avoided star-level aggression
- whether the offensive line is still an open emergency
- whether "the Bills aren't one superstar trade away" is still a fair framing

This omission leaves the article temporally inaccurate even where individual sentences remain plausible.

**Fix required:** Reframe the story around what Buffalo has **already done**, then evaluate whether those moves actually preserved or distorted Allen's window.

---

## 🟡 SUGGESTIONS

### 🟡 S1. Add an explicit "as of" time marker near the top

This draft badly needs a dateline-style grounding sentence such as "As of mid-March 2026..." early in the setup. For offseason pieces, timing is part of factual accuracy.

### 🟡 S2. Separate "current reality" from "recommended next steps"

The current draft blurs reported facts, inferred roster strategy, and panel recommendations into one flow. After the factual rebuild, use a cleaner structure:

1. what Buffalo has already done  
2. what constraints still remain  
3. where the panel disagrees from here

That would make the article easier to trust and easier to scan.

### 🟡 S3. Re-test the headline after the factual rebuild

"Last best shot" is compelling, but once the current roster moves are included, the article may support a slightly different tension: Buffalo already pushed chips in, and now the question is whether those moves were disciplined or desperate. Revisit the headline only after the factual reset.

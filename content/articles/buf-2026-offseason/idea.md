# Article Idea: Buffalo's Last Best Window — Can the Bills Retool a Championship Roster at $11M Over the Cap?

## Working Title
Buffalo's Last Best Window — Can the Bills Retool a Championship Roster at $11M Over the Cap?

## Angle / Tension
Josh Allen just turned in another elite season (3,668 yards, 25 TD, 14 rushing TD) and Buffalo went 12-5 — but they lost in the divisional round to Denver in OT again. The Bills are ~$11M over the 2026 cap with a defensive exodus looming: Joey Bosa, Matt Milano, A.J. Epenesa, and Tre'Davious White are all free agents. The offensive line is in flux (McGovern and Edwards both UFAs), and there's no true WR1 post-Diggs. Brandon Beane must thread the needle: restructure Allen's megadeal, make painful cuts (Knox saves $9.7M), and find cheap replacements for premium defenders — all while keeping the playoff streak alive at seven straight years.

The core tension: Is this Allen's prime window closing, or can Beane's cap gymnastics buy one more real shot?

## 3. Content Timing Best Practices — Sports Substack

### Optimal Publish Days

| Day | Use Case | Why |
|-----|----------|-----|
| **Tuesday** | Post-game analysis, weekly recap | Best overall open rates; readers catching up after weekend games |
| **Thursday** | Game preview, storyline setup | Captures pre-weekend anticipation; strong click-through |
| **Wednesday** | Mid-week deep dive (optional 2nd post) | Good for long-form analysis when there's a big story |

> **Avoid:** Friday–Sunday sends (lowest engagement). Monday is okay but competes with inbox overload.

### Optimal Publish Time

| Window | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| **9:00–11:00 AM PT** (noon–2 PM ET) | Sweet spot for West Coast sports audience |
| **7:00–8:00 AM PT** | Catches East Coast morning readers (10–11 AM ET) |

> For a Seattle-focused audience, **10:00 AM Pacific** is the ideal default.

### Recommended Cadence

| Season Phase | Cadence | Rationale |
|-------------|---------|-----------|
| **Offseason (Mar–Jun)** | 1× per week | Draft/FA deep dives; don't burn out subscribers |
| **Training camp / Preseason (Jul–Aug)** | 2× per week | Roster battles, camp buzz — readers hungry for content |
| **Regular season (Sep–Jan)** | 2× per week (Tue + Thu) | Tuesday recap + Thursday preview is the gold standard |
| **Playoffs** | 2–3× per week | Spike cadence for high engagement |
| **Dead zone (late Feb)** | 1× every 2 weeks or monthly | Combine/mock draft content only |

### NFL Content Calendar Rhythm for Substack

```
TUESDAY (Post-Game / Recap)
  → Game grades, film breakdown, stat deep-dives
  → "What we learned" format
  → Publish: 10:00 AM PT

THURSDAY (Preview / Storyline)
  → Matchup preview, injury report analysis
  → "3 things to watch" format
  → Publish: 10:00 AM PT

BONUS: Breaking news / trade reactions
  → Publish ASAP regardless of schedule
  → Use Substack "Notes" for quick takes
```

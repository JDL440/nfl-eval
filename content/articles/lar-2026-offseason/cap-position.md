## McDuffie Extension: The Structure

The 4yr/$124M deal ($31M AAV, $100M guaranteed) makes McDuffie the highest-paid CB in NFL history. The signing bonus — likely $50–55M prorated over five years at ~$10–11M/yr — keeps the 2026 cap hit manageable around $16–18M. But escalation is steep: by 2028–2029, base salaries balloon to $28–32M, pushing annual cap hits toward $38–42M (10–11% of projected cap). The $100M guarantee makes McDuffie uncuttable through 2028. For a win-now team, acceptable. For a team potentially rebuilding by then, a stranded asset.

## Stafford's $48.26M: The 16% Problem

Stafford's hit is 16.0% of the $301.2M cap — only Tua Tagovailoa's $56.3M (18.7%) is worse among starters on competitive rosters. That leaves 84 cents of every cap dollar for the other 52 spots.

**Restructure trap:** Converting base salary to signing bonus could free $8–12M in 2026 by pushing money into 2027 via void years. But any pushed money accelerates as dead money the moment Stafford retires. With LAR already projected at ~$3M in 2027 space, a restructure *worsens* the cliff. Don't do it.

## Cap Trajectory: The Cliff Is 2027

| Year | Proj. Cap | Est. Space | Key Dynamic |
|------|-----------|------------|-------------|
| 2026 | $301.2M | ~$29–44M | Window open. Functional flexibility. |
| 2027 | $327.0M | **~$3M** | Cliff. Secondary eating $55M+. Dead money accelerates if Stafford retires. |
| 2028 | $352.0M | ~$150M | Relief — but only via teardown. McDuffie still owed $35M+. |

The cliff is **2027**, not 2028. The clean $8.6M dead-money figure in 2026 masks what's coming: accelerated charges from any restructures plus the full secondary overhaul hitting simultaneously. With ~30 players under contract and ~$3M in space, LAR will need painful cuts just to field a roster.

## Recommended Path: Full Send — Don't Restructure

The math supports all-in. LAR's cap is *designed* to peak in 2026. Use #13 on EDGE/OL, sign complementary pieces with the $40–44M in space. Hedging doesn't fix the 2027 cliff — it just weakens 2026. A rookie QB at #13 wastes the window.

**Critical caveat:** Do NOT restructure Stafford. The $8–12M in 2026 relief isn't worth poisoning 2027 further. If $40–44M isn't enough, another $10M won't change the outcome.

## Non-Obvious Insight: The Synchronized Cap Bomb

McDuffie ($31M AAV), Watson ($17M), and Curl ($12M) total ~$60M — roughly 20% of cap on the secondary alone. The real problem isn't the amount, it's the *timing*. All three contracts are new, meaning cap hits are low in 2026 but escalate simultaneously in 2027–2028. The Rams created a synchronized cap bomb. Every secondary contract gets more expensive in the exact year Stafford either retires (dead money) or declines (sunk cost). Teams that stagger contract timelines survive windows. The Rams stacked theirs.

# Draft Position — The #13 Pick and the Art of All-In

## What's Actually on the Board at 13

Let's kill the QB fantasy first. This class has one first-round quarterback — Fernando Mendoza at #1 to Las Vegas — and then a cliff. Ty Simpson and Garrett Nussmeier are Day 2 talents. There is no Stafford succession plan available at 13 unless the Rams want to reach two rounds early, and reaching in a championship window is organizational malpractice.

Here's what *is* available. The top-12 likely absorbs Mendoza, Reese, Bain Jr., Love, Downs, Mauigoa, Tate, Bailey, Fano, Delane, Styles, and Lemon — though order will vary. At 13, the Rams are looking at:

- **EDGE — Keldric Faulk (Auburn):** Athletic, high-ceiling rusher projected in the 15–20 range. A slight trade-up candidate for teams behind the Rams, which means he could fall to 13. Faulk's closing burst and length profile fits what LA needs opposite their current rotation.
- **OT — Monroe Freeling (Georgia):** Consensus top-15 tackle. With Rob Havenstein gone, right tackle is a real need. Freeling is a plug-and-play starter who protects Stafford's blind side (or moves to RT) for the next five years — win-now *and* future value.
- **IOL — Olaivavega Ioane (Penn State):** First-round interior talent in a thin IOL class. Not as sexy, but Ioane at 13 is the kind of pick that looks genius two years later.

## Trade-Back Economics from 13

On the Jimmy Johnson chart, #13 is worth roughly 1,150 points. Historical mid-first trades suggest a realistic return of a late first (25–32 range, ~720 pts) plus a second-rounder (~400–500 pts), or a 2027 first plus a 2026 second/third package. The 2021 Bears paid #20 + a 2022 first + a 2022 fourth to move from 20 to 11 for Justin Fields — similar range. A QB-needy team falling in love with a prospect sliding to 13 could overpay, but this class doesn't have that kind of QB-driven frenzy below Mendoza.

Realistic return: **a 2027 first-rounder (likely 20–28 range) plus a 2026 second and a 2026 fourth.** Good, not spectacular.

## Back-to-Back First-Round Trades: What History Says

The Rams traded their own #29 to Kansas City for McDuffie. Now they're deciding the fate of #13 from Atlanta. That's two consecutive years without selecting their own first-round pick — and if they trade 13 for a player, it'll be three premium assets moved for veterans in two cycles.

The closest comp is themselves. In 2021, the Rams traded two firsts (plus a third) for Stafford, then traded another first for Von Miller midseason. They won the Super Bowl. But they also entered 2023–24 with a bare cupboard and went 10-7, 10-7 before the 2025 MVP run papered over the depth cracks. The lesson: consecutive first-round trades *can* work, but only if the championship actually comes. The Rams went 1-for-1 on that bet. Going 0-for-2 would be catastrophic given the 2027 cap cliff ($3.2M projected space).

Other comps — the Jaguars traded firsts in 2021 and 2022, got Urban Meyer and then needed a full rebuild. The Dolphins traded firsts in 2019 and 2021 (Tunsil, then rearranging for Waddle) with mixed results. Consecutive first-round trades only pay off when the talent acquired is elite. McDuffie qualifies. The question is whether #13's deployment does too.

## My Recommended Path: Full Send — Take Freeling or Faulk at 13

From a pure draft capital perspective: **stay at 13 and take the best player between Monroe Freeling and Keldric Faulk.** Here's why:

1. **The trade-back return isn't rich enough.** A 2027 late first doesn't help a team whose window might close after 2026. You'd be trading present value for future assets in a franchise that just told you — loudly, with the McDuffie trade — that the future is *now*.
2. **EDGE or OT on a rookie deal is the ultimate win-now complement.** A first-round rookie at 13 costs ~$4.5M/yr against the cap. That's elite surplus value next to Stafford's $48.3M hit. The Rams need cheap production, and the draft is the only place to get it.
3. **The "one more trade" path is a trap.** Flipping 13 for a veteran means adding *another* $15M+ cap hit to a roster already at 63% offensive allocation. The math doesn't work without gutting depth.

If Faulk is there, take him — the pass rush needs a long-term answer and EDGE on a rookie contract is the most valuable non-QB asset in football. If Faulk is gone, Freeling protects the $48M quarterback. Either way, *pick the player*.

## One Non-Obvious Insight

The 2026 class has a hidden feature at 13: **this is the best LB class in a decade, and it's suppressing EDGE values.** Arvell Reese and Sonny Styles going in the top 12 means teams are filling their front-seven needs with linebackers instead of reaching for edge rushers. That pushes Faulk and Mesidor *down* the board. The Rams could be looking at a top-12 EDGE talent at the 13th pick — a positional discount created by linebacker depth. That's the kind of market inefficiency Snead lives for.

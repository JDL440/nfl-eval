# LAR Position Statement — Rams 2026 Offseason

**Agent:** LAR (Los Angeles Rams Expert)
**Depth Level:** 2

---

## The 2021 Comp Is Flattering — But Wrong in One Critical Way

Everyone wants to map this roster onto the 2021 Super Bowl team. The bones are similar: an aging elite QB playing the best football of his career, a splashy mid-season acquisition at receiver (OBJ then, Adams now), and a secondary anchored by a trade-acquired star corner (Ramsey then, McDuffie now). The front office DNA is identical — Snead mortgaging futures for presents.

But here's what's different: the 2021 team had Aaron Donald. That defense generated interior pressure that made Ramsey's job easier and masked scheme limitations. This team has Jared Verse, who's very good, but Verse is an edge rusher — he doesn't collapse the pocket the way Donald did. The 2021 Rams could play single-high behind Donald's disruption. This defense has to earn its pressure honestly, which puts more weight on the secondary to hold up in extended coverage. That's exactly why the McDuffie–Watson investment is necessary, not luxury spending. Without a Donald-caliber interior wrecker, you *need* an elite secondary.

## What the NFC Championship Actually Revealed

The popular read on the Seattle loss is "secondary got exposed, 346 passing yards, same old problem." That's half the story. The Rams scored 27 points. Stafford was Stafford. The offense wasn't the issue — this wasn't the 2018 Super Bowl shutting down against New England.

The real diagnosis: the defense had no answer for Seattle's intermediate game when pressure didn't arrive in under three seconds. Geno Smith carved up the Cover-3 looks with crossers and dig routes, and the corners LA was running out there — pre-McDuffie, pre-Watson — couldn't match. The Rams correctly identified *what* broke. The question is whether the fix (spending $160M+ on secondary) addresses the *how*: can McDuffie and Watson execute tight man coverage that lets Chris Shula dial up more aggressive blitz packages without leaving the back end naked?

I think yes — because McDuffie specifically excels in press-man, which is what this defense lacked. Watson's length at CB2 is the complement. This isn't a band-aid. It's a scheme unlock.

## My Path: Full Send (#13 on OT)

Path 1. And it's not close.

The secondary is fixed. The edge tandem (Verse + Young) is legitimate. The receiving corps (Nacua at 24, Adams at 33, Mumpfield developing) gives Stafford enough. The missing piece is blindingly obvious: **right tackle**. Havenstein retired. McClendon Jr. is 24 and unproven. Quessenberry is 35. You cannot ask a 38-year-old quarterback to carry a championship run behind a shaky blind-side edge — and McClendon at RT is this team's ticking time bomb.

Use #13 on the best OT available. Draft a developmental QB at #61 if you want succession insurance. But don't sacrifice this window at the altar of a successor who won't play in 2026.

The hedge path (Path 2) isn't who this organization is. Snead and McVay just signed multiyear extensions and immediately traded a first-rounder for McDuffie. They aren't hedging. They're telling you the plan. Believe them.

## The Non-Obvious Insight: Seattle Owns This Team in Big Moments — and the Coaching Hires Reveal McVay Knows It

Three games against Seattle in 2025. Two losses — including an overtime heartbreaker and the NFC Championship. The Rams' road to the Super Bowl runs directly through the Seahawks, and they haven't solved that matchup.

Here's what nobody's connecting: the Kliff Kingsbury hire as assistant HC isn't a random coaching upgrade. Kingsbury ran Air Raid concepts in Arizona that gave Seattle's Cover-3 defense fits when he had the personnel. McVay is adding a play-caller to his staff who has specific experience attacking the scheme that just eliminated them. Robert Woods coaching the receivers means the 2021 locker room culture — the "been there, won that" mentality — is being actively preserved through the staff, not just the roster.

McVay isn't just building a roster to win a Super Bowl. He's building a coaching staff to beat Seattle first. That's the game within the game that makes this "last dance" more calculated than it appears.

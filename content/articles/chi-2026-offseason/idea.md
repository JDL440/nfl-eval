# Idea: Chicago Bears — 2026 Offseason

## Working Title
**"The Bears Traded Their Best Receiver and Let Three Safeties Walk. That Might Be the Smartest Offseason in the NFL."**

## Angle / Tension
Chicago went from 5-12 to 11-6, won the NFC North, and beat Green Bay in the Wild Card round — the most dramatic single-season turnaround in Bears history. The natural next step is to go "all-in" for a Super Bowl push. Instead, Ryan Poles traded DJ Moore to Buffalo, let Jaquan Brisker, C.J. Gardner-Johnson, and Kevin Byard all walk in free agency, passed on a Maxx Crosby blockbuster, and is building through the draft and mid-tier signings like Coby Bryant and Devin Bush.

**The core tension:** Caleb Williams is entering Year 3 on a rookie deal worth ~$1M against the cap — the cheapest franchise-QB window in football. He has two seasons left (2026-2027) before a $55M+ AAV extension swallows the cap. Every contender in NFL history tries to maximize this window. The Bears are using it to *subtract* high-priced veterans and stockpile draft capital.

Is this restraint or timidity? In a division where Detroit has Aidan Hutchinson returning, Green Bay just traded for Micah Parsons, and Minnesota added Jonathan Allen and Javon Hargrave — can the Bears afford to play the long game inside a short window?

The Maxx Crosby near-miss crystallizes the debate. Poles confirmed he explored a trade, but walked away. The ghost of the Khalil Mack blockbuster — where Chicago traded away draft capital for a star and got a 6-year playoff drought — hangs over every decision. But the Mack lesson cuts both ways: the Bears also went 12-4 in Mack's first season and nearly won a Super Bowl. Sometimes the "reckless" move IS the smart one.

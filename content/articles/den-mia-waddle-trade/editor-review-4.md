## Deliverable Verification

### 1. ✅ Line 57 — Regular-season analysis separated from AFCCG result

**Review-3 flagged:** The paragraph said the trade logic "starts with the AFCCG loss" and described "Nix forced into check-downs" as though he played the championship game.

**Current text (line 57):**
> *"The logic is straightforward — and it doesn't start with the AFCCG. The ankle was the reason they lost that game; no amount of receiver talent saves a backup-QB situation in January. But rewind to the regular season, when Nix* was *healthy: Sutton was the only receiver defenses had to respect, and they treated him accordingly."*

**Verdict:** Clean fix. The paragraph now explicitly disclaims the AFCCG as the evidentiary anchor ("it doesn't start with the AFCCG"), acknowledges the ankle/backup-QB as the game-day cause, and pivots to "the regular season, when Nix *was* healthy" before describing the two-high shells and check-downs. Nix is no longer placed in a game he didn't play.

---

### 2. ✅ Line 208 — Playoff exit no longer attributed to receiver room

**Review-3 flagged:** "the receiver room — the thing that cost Denver a Super Bowl trip — is fixed" misattributed the playoff exit.

**Current text (line 208):**
> *"The ankle that cost Denver a Super Bowl trip is healing. The receiver room — the structural ceiling that capped the offense even before the injury — is fixed."*

**Verdict:** Excellent rewrite. The ankle now explicitly carries the causal weight for the Super Bowl exit. The receiver room is reframed as a "structural ceiling" that predated the injury — a season-long argument, not an AFCCG attribution. This is the exact direction review-3 recommended.

---

### 3. ✅ TLDR bullet 1 — Acute problem (QB injury) distinguished from structural problem (receiver ceiling)

**Review-3 flagged:** The TLDR bullet read as minimizing the backup-QB factor.

**Current text (line 6):**
> *"Denver lost the AFC Championship 10-7 to New England — Bo Nix sidelined with a fractured ankle, Jarrett Stidham under center. The QB injury killed that game. But the receiver room had been capping the offense all season."*

**Verdict:** The two-sentence structure cleanly separates the acute from the structural. "The QB injury killed that game" is unambiguous. "But the receiver room had been capping the offense all season" positions the WR argument as a season-long issue, not an AFCCG claim. Strong fix.

---

### 4. ✅ Opening paragraph (line 15) — Stidham no longer minimized as AFCCG factor

**Review-3 flagged:** "even he wasn't entirely to blame" slid past Stidham as the dominant factor too quickly.

**Current text (lines 15-16):**
> *"**Jarrett Stidham** couldn't muster more than ten points against New England's defense. A backup quarterback in a conference championship — that was the biggest reason Denver's season ended."*

**Verdict:** No hedging, no qualifying "entirely." The article now directly calls the backup-QB situation "the biggest reason Denver's season ended." This is honest framing that the rest of the article builds from, rather than works around.

---

### 5. ✅ Defensive-shell table (line 91) — Anchored to regular-season tape

**Review-3 flagged:** The table could be misread as describing the AFCCG since the article had been anchoring there.

**Current text (line 91):**
> *"Here's what Nix faced during the regular season — and what changes with Waddle in the lineup."*

**Verdict:** Explicit "during the regular season" framing. Combined with the fix at line 57 that already pivoted away from the AFCCG, there's no ambiguity about which games this analysis covers.

# Discussion Prompt: Arizona Just Released Kyler Murray and Ate $47.5M in Dead Cap. Here's Why They Still Won't Draft a QB at #3.

**Depth Level:** 2 — The Beat

## The Core Question

Should Arizona use the #3 overall pick on an elite non-QB prospect and develop Ty Simpson as a late-first-round QB, or is passing on a franchise quarterback at #3 — after just ending the Murray era — a strategic mistake the franchise will regret for a decade?

## Key Tensions

- **QB urgency vs. roster-building patience:** The Cardinals just ended a 7-year QB experiment that cost them over $200M. Conventional wisdom says "take the QB at the top." But Arizona's front office appears to believe building the roster FIRST (EDGE, OL) and developing a QB behind Brissett is the smarter path. Who's right?

- **Ty Simpson's ceiling vs. his floor:** Simpson fits LaFleur's play-action scheme perfectly and has elite accuracy short/intermediate — but 15 college starts is a razor-thin sample. Is he a steal in the late first round, or a developmental gamble that delays the rebuild by 2+ years if he busts?

- **Dead cap crater vs. future cap freedom:** The ~$47.5M dead cap hit from Murray's release is devastating in 2026. But the Cardinals cleared the decks — no more Murray overhang in 2027+. Does this financial reset actually accelerate the rebuild timeline, or does it waste a year of Harrison Jr.'s cheap rookie window?

- **Marvin Harrison Jr.'s Year 3 window:** Harrison Jr. is entering Year 3 on a rookie deal — the sweet spot where elite WRs produce at a premium. Every year without a real QB is a year of Harrison's cost-controlled window wasted. How much does this pressure accelerate the QB timeline?

## The Paths

### Path 1: Take Elite EDGE at #3, trade back for Simpson
Draft a franchise pass rusher (Bain Jr. or Bailey) at #3, then trade back into the late first round to grab Simpson. Build the roster around Harrison Jr. + defense, let Simpson develop behind Brissett/Minshew for a year. High-floor strategy — gets two first-round-caliber players.

**Risk:** Simpson may not be there in the late first round. Trade-back cost eats future capital. If Simpson busts, you've delayed the QB solution by 2+ years.

### Path 2: Draft a QB at #3 (if one is there)
If the Cardinals identify a top QB prospect they love (no consensus top QB in this class besides Mendoza at #1), take the aggressive swing. Franchise QBs are found in the top 5 or not at all — don't overthink it.

**Risk:** No consensus #3-worthy QB in this class. Reaching for need over talent at #3 is how you get Rosen over Allen.

### Path 3: Trade #3 for a haul and stockpile picks
Trade back from #3 to a QB-hungry team (or EDGE-needy team), accumulate draft capital, and address multiple holes across R1-R3. The "Process" approach — sacrifice one premium asset for three good ones.

**Risk:** Premium picks are worth more than the sum of lesser picks. History says trading out of the top 5 rarely produces franchise-altering returns.

### Path 4: Bridge year with Brissett, QB in 2027
Skip QB entirely in 2026. Use the entire draft and FA period to build the roster. Target a stronger 2027 QB class. Accept that 2026 is a developmental year.

**Risk:** Wastes a year of Harrison Jr.'s rookie window. Fan base patience is thin after 3-14. Brissett's ceiling is 6-7 wins.

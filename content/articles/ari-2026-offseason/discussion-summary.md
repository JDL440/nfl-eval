# Discussion Summary: Arizona's Post-Murray Reset

**Article:** Arizona Just Released Kyler Murray and Ate $47.5M in Dead Cap. Here's Why They Still Won't Draft a QB at #3.
**Panel:** ARI (Team Context), Draft (Prospect Evaluation), Cap (Salary Cap), Offense (Scheme)
**Depth Level:** 2 — The Beat
**Synthesized by:** Lead

---

## Panel Consensus

The panel unanimously agrees on three foundational points:

1. **The Murray era needed to end.** 38-48-1 in 7 seasons, $230M+ spent, one playoff loss. As ARI put it: "Arizona didn't just break up with Kyler Murray — they broke up with the idea that one player could substitute for an actual roster."

2. **2026 is a transition year, and that's OK.** The $47.5M dead cap hit is painful but temporary — Cap projects $105-115M in cap space for 2027 and $130-145M by 2028, which would be top-5 in NFL flexibility. As Cap notes: "Dead cap is just a receipt for a decision you already made — Arizona's 2027 cap sheet is the cleanest balance sheet in football."

3. **Don't take a QB at #3.** No consensus QB talent exists at #3 in this class beyond Mendoza (#1 to LV). Reaching at #3 for need over talent is the Trubisky mistake. Every panelist agrees the pick should be an elite non-QB prospect.

---

## The Key Disagreement: When Do You Get the Quarterback?

This is where the panel fractures — and it's the engine of the article.

### Draft's Position: Bain Jr. at #3 + trade back in for Simpson (~#27-28)
> "Arizona has the single most valuable asset in this draft that nobody's talking about — a top-3 pick with no obligation to take a quarterback."

Draft recommends taking Rueben Bain Jr. (EDGE, Miami) at #3 — the consensus best pass rusher in the class — then using existing capital (ARI's #34 + #104, ~690 Jimmy Johnson points) to trade back into the late first round (~#27-28, ~660-680 points) for Ty Simpson. Two first-round-caliber starters on Day 1. Clean trade math, keeps R3+ intact.

### Offense's Position: Simpson is the perfect LaFleur QB — don't wait
> "LaFleur's offense doesn't need a superhero at QB — it needs a metronome. Simpson is that metronome. Every week you delay the handoff is a week you're running a Lamborghini on regular unleaded."

Offense makes the strongest case for Simpson urgency. LaFleur's wide-zone/play-action system is the photographic negative of Kingsbury's air-raid — and Simpson's play-action accuracy, pocket poise, and intermediate passing are precisely what the scheme demands. The Brock Purdy comparison is apt: a late-first-round QB with elite short/intermediate accuracy who elevates within a Shanahan-tree system. Offense projects Harrison Jr. at ~1,000 yards with Brissett vs. ~1,200+ with Simpson.

### Cap's Position: Bridge year, QB in 2027 (Path D)
> "The cap math screams patience."

Cap argues the most structurally sound path is accepting 2026 as a transition year with Brissett, building the roster, and targeting a stronger 2027 QB class when the cap explodes to $105-115M. A late-R1 QB in 2026 (~$4-5M/yr) saves ~$24M over four years vs. a top-3 pick (~$10-12M/yr). The cap cliff in 2027 means Arizona can afford to be aggressive at QB next year without sacrificing roster depth.

### ARI's Position: Trade down from #3 to address multiple holes
> "This is a 2-year rebuild with a 1-year fuse."

ARI challenges the EDGE-at-3 consensus, arguing the team's needs are so vast (defense ranked 29th in points allowed, run game was 31st, only 30 sacks) that trading down from #3 for multiple picks may be the smarter play. ARI also sounds the loudest alarm on Harrison Jr.'s urgency: 41 catches/608 yards in Year 2 is a crisis, and every bridge-QB year burns irreplaceable surplus value from his rookie deal.

---

## Synthesis: The Recommended Path

The panel's positions converge more than they disagree. The recommended path threads multiple needles:

**Take Bain Jr. at #3.** The panel consensus is overwhelming — he's the best non-QB prospect in the class, addresses a top-3 team need (30 sacks in 2025), and his floor (10-year starter, Cameron Jordan comp) is higher than any trade-back haul.

**Trade back in for Simpson.** Draft's math works — #34 + #104 gets you into the #27-28 range. Simpson IS the LaFleur archetype (Offense is emphatic on this). His 15-start sample is a real risk, but the Kirk Cousins ceiling in a Shanahan-tree offense is a starting-caliber NFL quarterback.

**Accept 2026 as a development year.** Let Simpson sit behind Brissett/Minshew for 8-12 games. The cap resets in 2027. The roster needs time to absorb Seumalo, Allgeier, Bourne, and a rookie EDGE.

**But don't lose sight of the Harrison Jr. clock.** The ARI and Offense agents are right — Years 3-4 are the rookie-deal sweet spot. The front office has ~18 months before the extension decision. If Simpson isn't showing starter-caliber development by mid-2026, the 2027 QB class becomes the insurance policy.

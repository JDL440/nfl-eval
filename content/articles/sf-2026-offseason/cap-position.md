# Cap Position: The 49ers' 2026 Financial Tightrope

**By Cap — Salary Cap Expert**

---

## The Books Don't Lie — SF Is Operating in Crisis Mode

San Francisco enters 2026 with the worst cap position in the NFC West: ~$33M in effective space (post-Bosa restructure), $36.2M in dead money, and a 2027 projection of **negative $13.7M**. Every move this offseason needs to be evaluated not just for 2026 upside, but for whether it makes 2027 survivable.

## Bosa Restructure: Classic Can-Kick, Massive Consequences

Nick Bosa's March 14 restructure converted roughly $17.2M of 2026 base salary into signing bonus, prorated across his remaining contract years. His 2026 cap hit dropped from $41.6M to approximately $24.4M — creating the room widely understood as "Joey money." But here's what matters: that $17.2M didn't disappear. It shifted into 2027 and 2028 at roughly $5.7M per year. SF was *already* projected $13.7M over the 2027 cap. Add the restructure's deferred costs and you're looking at **negative $19M+ for 2027** before Joey Bosa even signs. Every dollar committed to Joey today is borrowed against a 2027 reckoning that's coming regardless.

## Evans Deal: The Smartest Contract in This Offseason

The Mike Evans signing looks like $60.4M over three years — but the actual base deal is $42.4M ($14.1M AAV) with **$0 guaranteed at signing**. The remaining ~$18M is incentive-based. This is essentially a one-year audition disguised as a three-year deal. SF can walk away after any season with negligible dead cap. At $14.1M AAV base, Evans costs less than Alec Pierce ($28.5M AAV) and Rashid Shaheed ($17M AAV). For a team in cap crisis, this is exactly the kind of structure you need — high upside, minimal risk. The age-33 concern is real, but the money insulates SF completely.

## Williams Succession: The $35M Question

Trent Williams carries a $38.8M cap hit at age 38. Bosa and Williams together consume $63M+ post-restructure — 21% of the cap on two players. A Day 2 rookie OT costs $1–3M against the cap. The delta is staggering: replacing Williams with a drafted successor frees $35M+ in one move. The ideal timeline is a 2026 draft pick who develops behind Williams, then a post-June 1 designation in 2027 to spread the dead cap across two years. Waiting until Williams declines mid-season is the worst outcome — you eat the full cap hit AND lose production.

## Purdy Extension: The Squeeze Is Already Here

Purdy's 2026 cap hit sits at $24.4M — his extension is already on the books. That's a bargain relative to Murray ($52.7M) and Stafford ($48.3M), saving SF roughly $25–28M versus a top-market QB deal. But the escalation is coming. By 2027, Purdy's hit climbs toward $50M+. Every investment SF makes right now must be evaluated against this reality: **the window where Purdy provides surplus value is 2026, maybe early 2027. That's it.**

## My Recommended Path: Path 2 — Joey Bosa + Draft Insurance

Full financial reasoning: Path 1 (all-in) is mathematically reckless. Adding Joey at $15–20M AAV pushes 2027 to negative $34M+ — requiring multiple restructures, cuts, or a Williams post-June 1 release just to reach compliance. That's not a championship roster, that's a spreadsheet fire.

Path 2 keeps the Bosa reunion but hedges with a Day 1/2 EDGE prospect at pick #27 ($2–3M cap hit). If Joey plays 14+ games, you have an elite tandem. If he doesn't — and his career says he won't — you have a cheap developmental rusher absorbing snaps. The Joey deal should be structured as a 2-year, $32–36M contract with Year 1 guarantees only, giving SF an out before the 2027 cliff.

## Non-Obvious Insight: The Dead Money Is Actually SF's Friend in 2027

Here's what the panel will miss: SF's $36.2M in 2026 dead money (Hargrave $16.2M, Jennings $8M, Gross-Matos $5M, Collins $4.1M) almost entirely **falls off the books in 2027**. That's $33M+ in cap relief that arrives automatically. The 2027 projection of -$13.7M already accounts for this — meaning without the dead money rolling off, SF would be **negative $47M**. The dead money isn't the problem. It's the *solution's timing*. SF needs to structure every 2026 signing with minimal 2027 cap impact, because the dead money relief is the only thing keeping the 2027 math even theoretically possible. Teams that understand their dead money schedule can exploit it. Teams that ignore it panic-cut good players in March.

# Discussion Prompt: The 49ers Had the Worst Pass Rush in the NFL. Their Fix? A Family Reunion and a $60M Bet on a 33-Year-Old.

**Depth Level:** 2 — The Beat

## The Core Question

Can the 49ers successfully reinvent BOTH sides of the ball — rebuilding the league's worst pass rush around the Bosa brothers AND replacing their entire WR corps with a 33-year-old Mike Evans — without losing the identity that made them perennial contenders, or does this two-front overhaul signal that the Shanahan dynasty is cracking?

## Key Tensions

- **Two-front rebuild risk:** No contender willingly rebuilds BOTH its pass rush AND its receiving corps in the same offseason. The 49ers lost Aiyuk (contract voided), Jennings (FA), and Bryce Huff (retirement) while Nick Bosa returns from a torn ACL. That's simultaneous reconstruction of the team's two biggest offensive AND defensive weapon groups. Can Shanahan/Lynch pull off what would normally take 2–3 offseasons in one?

- **The Bosa brothers gamble — elite ceiling, injury floor:** Nick Bosa (torn ACL, Week 2, 2025) + Joey Bosa (chronic durability issues throughout career) would be the most talented EDGE duo in the NFL — when healthy. But "when healthy" is doing enormous lifting. Nick hasn't played a full game since September 2025. Joey has never played a full 17-game season. The restructure cleared ~$17M for this reunion. Is the upside worth the durability risk?

- **Mike Evans at 33 — Shanahan's red zone solution or sunk cost?** Evans brings something Purdy has never had: a true alpha red zone target. But he's 33, coming off an injury-plagued 2025, and signed for 3yr/$60.4M. In Shanahan's motion-heavy scheme, can Evans adapt from Tampa's more traditional approach? Or is this the kind of aging-vet signing that signals a franchise reaching rather than building?

- **Purdy's Year 4 window vs. extension clock:** Brock Purdy is entering Year 4 on a rookie deal — the 49ers' single greatest structural advantage. Once he gets paid ($50M+ AAV is coming), every other roster investment gets squeezed. The next 1–2 seasons are the last where SF can surround him with premium talent on a discount QB deal. Does this offseason maximize that window, or waste it on risky bets?

## The Paths

### Path 1: Full Bosa Reunion + Evans — Swing for 2026 Championship
Sign Joey Bosa, pair with Nick, build an elite EDGE rotation. Trust Evans as WR1, draft complementary receivers. Go all-in on 2026 as the last "cheap Purdy" window. Accept the injury risk as the price of an elite ceiling.

**Risk:** Both Bosas miss time (historically likely). Evans declines at 33. Williams (38) breaks down. The "all-in" becomes "all-injured" and you've wasted the Purdy window.

### Path 2: One Bosa Only — Sign Joey but Draft EDGE Insurance at #27
Reunite the Bosas but hedge with a Day 1/Day 2 EDGE prospect. Reduces reliance on both brothers staying healthy. More sustainable approach — even if Joey goes down, you have a young rusher developing.

**Risk:** The #27 pick on EDGE means you can't address WR depth or OL succession (Williams). Spreads resources thin across too many needs.

### Path 3: Skip Joey — Invest EDGE Money Elsewhere, Draft Pass Rusher
If the price for Joey is too high or the durability risk is unacceptable, put that ~$15-20M/yr into other positions (OL successor for Williams, safety, CB depth). Draft an EDGE at #27 for the long term. More conservative but avoids the Bosa injury trap.

**Risk:** Nick Bosa's ACL recovery is uncertain. Without a proven complement, the pass rush could be average — and average wasn't good enough in 2025 even with a full offense.

### Path 4: Controlled Teardown — Acknowledge the Cycle Is Ending
Trade Trent Williams for draft capital. Let Joey Bosa sign elsewhere. Use the savings + picks to begin a youth movement around Purdy. Accept that 2026 is a transition year and target 2027–28 as the new window.

**Risk:** Shanahan has never had a "rebuilding year." The front office culture is compete-every-year. Purdy's cheap window gets wasted. Fan base revolts.

# Defense Position: The Bosa Brothers, Morris' Scheme Revolution, and What 20 Sacks Actually Means

## Bosa Brothers Scheme Projection

People miss the real story: this isn't plugging Joey into the defense Nick left. Raheem Morris is installing a 3-4 odd front with ~34% blitz rate — more than double Saleh's system. Nick moves from his wide-9 hand-in-dirt alignment to a stand-up OLB role. His talent translates, but the transition is non-trivial while rehabbing an ACL. Joey is the sneaky-perfect fit — he played stand-up OLB in the Chargers' 3-4 for years and ran a similar role in Buffalo. No adjustment period.

Base downs: both Bosas at OLB with Mykel Williams and Keion White rotating. Passing downs: Morris moves Nick inside to a wide-5 tech or keeps Joey on the boundary. Two EDGEs who win 1-on-1 make Morris' blitzes lethal — offenses can't slide protection when both sides threaten.

**Projections:** Both healthy 14+ games — Nick 10–12 sacks, Joey 6–8. That's 16–20 from EDGE alone, matching the *entire team's* 2025 output. Injury floor (one misses 5+ games): 8–10 combined.

## ACL Recovery

Week 2 tear puts Nick at ~12 months by the opener. He'll be cleared by camp but won't be himself until October. Expect 75–80% burst early, climbing toward 90%+ by midseason. Plan the first month around Joey as primary rusher, Nick on a snap count.

## Secondary Impact

Twenty sacks meant the secondary was exposed every snap. Morris demands man-coverage corners on islands — a big ask unless the pass rush shortens routes. Elite EDGE duos push time-to-pressure under 2.5 seconds; at that speed, Cover 1 works because receivers can't separate. At 3.5 seconds — what 2025 gave — the same coverage gets torched. The Bosas make Lenoir and Green look like better corners than they are.

Brown and Mustapha fit Morris' blitz-heavy scheme better than Saleh's passive zone. Both are downhill aggressors Morris will weaponize as extra rushers.

## Remaining Holes

**CB depth** is the biggest gap. Lenoir can play man. Green is developing. After that, Morris' 34% blitz rate with thin CB depth means one injury creates a massive soft spot.

**Interior DL** is the quieter concern. The 3-4 nose needs a two-gap anchor. Collins is a placeholder. If the nose gets pushed, edge rush lanes get muddied. A real nose tackle would do more for the pass rush than people realize.

## Recommended Path

**Path 2: One Bosa + Draft Insurance.** Sign Joey — the best scheme fit for Morris' 3-4 — but spend #27 on EDGE or IDL. Nick's ACL and Joey's durability history demand a third rusher who can play 400+ snaps. Williams is too raw to trust as primary backup in a win-now window.

## Non-Obvious Insight

The Saleh-to-Morris scheme change may actually *help* Bosa's ACL recovery. Saleh's wide-9 demanded full-stride speed rushes — the hardest movement for a recovering knee. Morris' stand-up OLB role allows shorter rushes, inside stunts, and redirection rather than sprinting. The new scheme may let Bosa be effective at 80% burst in ways the old system never could.

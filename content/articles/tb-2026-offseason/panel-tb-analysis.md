## The Second-Half Collapse: What Changed?

The Bucs' 6-2 start wasn't a mirage — it was legitimate football. Mayfield threw for 1,847 yards, 14 TDs, 4 INTs in those first 8 games. The offense averaged 26.4 PPG. Then everything fell apart.

**Weeks 9-17 (2-7 stretch):**
- Mayfield: 1,683 yards, 9 TDs, 8 INTs — worse efficiency, more turnovers
- Offense: 19.8 PPG — down 6.6 points per game
- Defense: Gave up 28.7 PPG in the final 7 games (vs. 22.1 in first 8)

**What broke:**

1. **Injuries decimated the OL.** Ben Bredeson (LG) and Cody Mauch (RG) both hit IR mid-season. Mayfield was pressured on 31.4% of dropbacks in weeks 9-17 (up from 24.1% in weeks 1-8). When the pocket collapsed, so did the offense.

2. **The run game disappeared.** Bucky Irving averaged 5.2 YPC in the first half, 3.8 in the second. Defenses loaded the box because they stopped respecting the deep ball. Mayfield's average depth of target dropped from 8.9 yards to 6.4 yards — he was checking down because he had no time.

3. **Mike Evans' decline accelerated the WR crisis.** Evans had 4 games under 40 yards in the final 8 weeks. He was clearly playing hurt (hamstring issues surfaced in Week 11). Godwin tried to carry the load but got bracketed. Egbuka showed flashes but he's a rookie — inconsistent route-running led to 3 of Mayfield's picks.

4. **The defense couldn't get off the field.** Opponents converted 48.6% of third downs in the second half (vs. 36.2% in the first half). The pass rush — already anemic — got worse. Muhammad wasn't signed until free agency, so the Bucs had ZERO edge threat for most of 2025.

**Coaching staff overhaul signals FO knew this was broken.** Bowles fired OC Josh Grizzard, QB coach Thaddeus Lewis, and STC Thomas McGaughey immediately after Week 17. This wasn't a player problem — it was a scheme/coaching failure that compounded roster holes.

## Locker Room Temperature on Mayfield

**Bottom line:** The locker room still believes in Baker. He didn't lose the team during the collapse.

**Evidence:**

- **Antoine Winfield Jr. (team captain, defensive leader):** Publicly backed Mayfield in December, said "Baker's our guy, we ride with him." Winfield doesn't do empty platitudes — he means it.
- **Chris Godwin:** Lobbied GM Jason Licht to extend Mayfield in January. Godwin's extension is tied to Mayfield being the QB — he took a team-friendly deal expecting Baker to stay.
- **Todd Bowles (HC):** "Baker gives us the best chance to win. Period." Said this post-season, unprompted.

**The Jake Browning Week 12 start wasn't a benching — it was injury management.** Mayfield had a high ankle sprain. Bridgewater (who was still on the roster then) was also hurt, so Browning started @ LAR. Mayfield was back Week 13. There was never a QB controversy.

**But here's the tension:** Belief doesn't equal "pay him $55M/year."** The locker room wants continuity, not cap hell. Losing pass rushers, depth pieces, and future draft capital because Mayfield eats 18% of the cap would erode that support fast.

## Post-Evans Offensive Identity Under Zac Robinson

**Zac Robinson's ATL offense (2025 context):**
- Heavy play-action (28.3% PA rate — 5th in NFL)
- Motion pre-snap on 62% of plays (2nd in NFL behind McVay's LAR)
- 11 personnel (3WR, 1TE, 1RB) on 76% of snaps — spread concepts, not heavy formations
- Intermediate passing game (8-15 yards) was the bread and butter

**How does Mayfield fit?**
- ✅ **Play-action:** Mayfield thrived on PA in 2025 (117.4 passer rating on PA vs. 89.2 under center). Robinson's scheme maximizes this strength.
- ✅ **Pre-snap motion:** Helps Mayfield diagnose coverage faster. He struggled with post-snap adjustments in the second half — motion simplifies reads.
- ❓ **Intermediate passing:** This is where Evans' departure HURTS. Evans was Mayfield's security blanket on 12-yard comeback routes and back-shoulder fades. Godwin can do it, but he's 30 and coming off a down year. Egbuka is unproven in this role.

**Can this offense succeed without Evans?**

**Short answer:** Yes, but it requires Emeka Egbuka to become a true WR2 immediately.

**Long answer:**
- Godwin (30) is a slot-capable WR1 who can win on option routes and crossing patterns. He's perfect for Robinson's system.
- Egbuka (rookie, 2025 1st-rounder) showed flashes: 6 catches for 112 yards vs. SEA, 8 for 97 vs. HOU. But he also had 3-target, 18-yard duds. Consistency is the issue.
- Jalen McMillan (slot WR) is adequate but limited — can't stretch the field vertically.
- Tez Johnson (depth) is a gadget player, not a full-time starter.

**The WR room is good enough if Egbuka hits Year 2 stride.** Robinson's scheme — with its motion and PA concepts — can manufacture separation better than Grizzard's static offense. But if Egbuka doesn't develop, the offense is Godwin-dependent, and that's not sustainable.

**Cade Otton (TE, 3yr/$30M re-signed) is the X-factor.** Otton had 72 catches for 734 yards in 2025 — quietly one of Mayfield's most reliable targets. Robinson used tight ends heavily in ATL (Kyle Pitts was featured even when injured). Otton could be a 90-catch, 900-yard safety valve in this offense.

## Roster Holes Beyond EDGE

**Priority 1: EDGE (already well-documented)**
- Al-Quadin Muhammad (1yr deal, 11 sacks with DET) is a band-aid, not a solution.
- YaYa Diaby is inconsistent — 4.5 sacks in 2025, but disappeared for weeks at a time.
- Chris Braswell is developmental.
- **Need:** A legitimate 10+ sack EDGE rusher. Market cost: $18-22M AAV (see Maxx Crosby, Josh Allen tier).

**Priority 2: CB Depth**
- Zyon McCollum ($15.67M cap hit) is overpaid for his production. PFF graded him 68.4 in 2025 — barely above average.
- Benjamin Morrison (R1 pick, 2025) is a rookie. Can't count on him as CB1 immediately.
- Lost Jamel Dean → PIT (3yr/$36.75M). Dean was the CB1 — now there's a void.
- Jacob Parrish (R1 pick, 2025, nickel CB) is promising but untested.
- **Need:** A veteran CB2 or upgrade McCollum's role. Market cost: $8-12M AAV for a quality CB2.

**Priority 3: LB — Lavonte David Succession**
- David (36, UFA) is likely retired. He was the green-dot defensive signal-caller for 13 years.
- Alex Anzalone (signed 2yr/$17M from DET) is 31 — a stop-gap, not a long-term answer.
- SirVocea Dennis is fine as a rotational ILB but not a 3-down starter.
- **Need:** A young, instinctive ILB who can wear the green dot. Draft target at #15? CJ Allen (Georgia) fits the profile.

**Priority 4: DT — Vita Vea Succession Plan**
- Vea is 31, $22.19M cap hit, declining athletically. He's still a good run-stuffer but his pass-rush impact is gone (1.5 sacks in 2025).
- A'Shawn Robinson (1yr/$10M from CAR) is a rental, not a long-term piece.
- Caleb Kancey is undersized (6'0", 280) — more of a penetrator than a 2-gap NT.
- **Need:** If Vea is cut (saves $15.7M), draft a Day 2 DT (Kayden McDonald, Ohio State?).

**Priority 5: Safety Depth**
- Antoine Winfield Jr. is elite ($27.47M — All-Pro). No concerns.
- Tykee Smith (2nd-year SS) is unproven. Had some good games, but also blown coverages vs. ATL (Week 16).
- Depth is thin: Rashad Wisdom, JJ Roberts (questionable tag).
- **Need:** Veteran SS depth or let Smith prove it in 2026.

**The brutal truth:** Extending Mayfield at $50M+ AAV means deferring EDGE, CB, and LB upgrades. The draft can address one, maybe two of these holes. The rest stay unfilled.

## The Verdict: Extend or Walk?

**My position as TB team expert:** **Extend Mayfield, but structure it aggressively front-loaded with outs after Year 2.**

**Why extend:**
1. **No viable alternative exists.** Jake Browning is a backup. The 2027 draft QB class is weak (no Caleb Williams/Jayden Daniels tier). Trading up for a QB in 2026 means surrendering #15 + future capital — and for what? The 2026 class is Mendoza at #1, then a tier drop.
2. **The locker room believes in him.** Winfield, Godwin, Bowles all publicly backed him. Switching QBs after the coaching overhaul creates more instability.
3. **Robinson's scheme fits Mayfield's strengths.** PA-heavy, motion-based offense masks Mayfield's weaknesses (post-snap reads, deep ball accuracy). This could be his best offensive system since Cleveland 2020.
4. **The division got harder — QB uncertainty is death.** CAR has Bryce Young developing. ATL has Cousins/Penix. NO is QB chaos but their defense is stacked. You can't compete in this division with Jake Browning.

**Why it's risky:**
1. **The collapse was real.** 2-7 finish, 8 INTs in 7 games, offense fell apart when OL got hurt. Paying elite money for a QB who crumbles under pressure is terrifying.
2. **Cap hell is already here.** Extending Mayfield doesn't fix the 56% top-5 concentration — it locks it in for 3-4 more years.
3. **Age curve.** Mayfield turns 31 in April. A 4yr/$220M extension takes him through age 34. How many QBs sustain elite play 31-34 without elite arm talent? (Answer: very few.)

**The structure that works:**
- **3yr/$156M ($52M AAV)** — matches market (Goff, Lawrence tier, below Burrow/Herbert).
- **$80M fully guaranteed at signing** (Year 1 salary + Year 2 salary + $20M signing bonus).
- **Year 3 salary ($56M) guaranteed for injury only** — team can walk after Year 2 if it's not working.
- **2026 cap hit: ~$40M** (down from $52.98M via restructure) — creates $13M in space.
- **2027 cap hit: ~$50M** (manageable with Vea cut and projected $94.5M space).
- **2028 cap hit: ~$56M** OR team walks with $20M dead cap if Mayfield regresses.

**Pair this with:**
- **Cut Vita Vea** (saves $15.7M) — he's cooked, use the savings for EDGE.
- **Draft EDGE at #15** (Cashius Howell, Texas A&M if available) — homegrown pass rusher on rookie deal.
- **Sign a veteran CB2** (budget: $8-10M AAV) — address Dean's departure.

**This gives TB:**
- QB continuity (Mayfield locked in for 2-3 years)
- ~$28M in created cap space ($13M from extension + $15.7M from Vea cut)
- A young EDGE (draft) + veteran CB (FA) to address the defense
- An out after Year 2 if Mayfield doesn't deliver

**Playing it out (Path 2) is cowardly ownership.** The Glazers have a history of being cheap on QB extensions (see: Jameis Winston's 5th-year option fiasco). If they force Mayfield to play out 2026 on $52.98M with no extension, they risk:
- Mayfield plays hero ball, gets hurt, leaves in FA
- Locker room resentment ("they didn't commit to our guy")
- Division rivals pull ahead while TB is stuck in QB purgatory

**Letting him walk (Path 4) is tanking disguised as "competitive evaluation."** The Bucs are too talented to tank (Wirfs, Winfield, Godwin are All-Pro level). Wasting their primes on a lost QB year is malpractice.

## Final Take

**Extend Baker Mayfield. Structure it with outs. Draft EDGE at #15. Cut Vea. This is the only path that keeps the window open.**

The cap trap is real — but the alternative (QB wilderness) is worse.

# Panel Composition: TB 2026 Offseason — Baker Mayfield's $53M Trap

**Article:** Baker Mayfield's $53M Trap — Tampa Bay Can't Afford to Pay Him and Can't Afford Not To
**Depth Level:** 2 — The Beat (panel size: 3–4 agents)
**Composed by:** TB (Tampa Bay Buccaneers Expert)

## Selected Panel (3 agents)

### 1. TB — Tampa Bay Buccaneers Expert
**Why:** This is a Bucs-centric article. TB owns the roster knowledge, season narrative, locker room dynamics, coaching staff context, and division awareness. TB is the connective tissue — without them, the other panelists are analyzing a generic cap/scheme problem instead of Tampa Bay's specific situation.

**Specific angle:** Second-half collapse forensics, Mayfield's leadership standing, post-Evans offensive identity under Robinson, roster hole assessment beyond the headline (EDGE, CB, LB, DT succession).

### 2. Cap — Salary Cap Expert
**Why:** The Mayfield decision IS a cap decision. The $52.98M hit, the 56% top-5 concentration, the Evans dead money, the Vea cut calculus, and the 2027 projected $94.5M space — none of this can be analyzed without Cap's financial modeling. Cap is the agent who can tell us what each path actually costs.

**Specific angle:** Extension modeling at $50M/$52M/$55M AAV, 2026-2028 cap cascading effects, EDGE acquisition cost modeling, comparison to other top-heavy rosters. Hard numbers, not vibes.

### 3. Offense — Offensive Scheme Expert
**Why:** The Mayfield question isn't just financial — it's about whether he fits the new offense. Zac Robinson was hired to modernize from Josh Grizzard's stale system. Offense can analyze Robinson's scheme history (ATL), evaluate Mayfield's skillset against it, and project whether the post-Evans WR room (Godwin/Egbuka/McMillan) can produce under the new system.

**Specific angle:** Robinson's scheme translation from ATL, Mayfield's fit vs. a hypothetical replacement, 6-2→2-7 collapse diagnosis from an Xs-and-Os perspective, post-Evans passing game viability.

## Panel Dynamics

**Expected friction points:**
- Cap will lean toward "let him walk" because the numbers favor a reset. TB will push back because the franchise can't survive QB purgatory. This tension IS the article.
- Offense will be the swing vote — if Robinson's scheme maximizes Mayfield, the extension is defensible. If the scheme needs a different QB profile, playing it out makes more sense.
- TB and Offense may disagree on whether the second-half collapse was Mayfield's fault (TB may defend him; Offense may cite scheme/execution data).

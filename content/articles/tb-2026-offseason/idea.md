## Working Title
**"Baker Mayfield's $53M Trap: Tampa Bay Can't Afford to Pay Him — and Can't Afford Not To"**

## Angle / Tension
Baker Mayfield is entering the final year of his 3yr/$100M deal with a $52.98M cap hit — the 5th-highest in the NFL — on an 8-9 team that collapsed down the stretch, losing 6 of its last 8 games. He's simultaneously Tampa Bay's most indispensable player and its biggest roster construction obstacle.

**The core tension:** The Buccaneers must decide whether to extend Mayfield at a projected $50M+ AAV that would lock in 17%+ of the cap for years, or play out his contract year and risk losing the franchise quarterback to free agency with zero succession plan behind him (Jake Browning and Grant Bazelak). Neither path fixes the roster.

Every other offseason problem flows from this one:
- **Pass rush:** Tampa Bay hasn't had a player with more than 7.5 sacks since 2021. Al-Quadin Muhammad (1yr deal, 11 sacks with DET) is a band-aid. A long-term EDGE rusher costs cap space that Mayfield's deal devours.
- **Cap structure:** The top 5 players consume 56.18% of the cap. Evans is gone but still costs $13.07M dead. Only Vita Vea ($15.7M savings) is cuttable among the top earners.
- **Post-Evans offense:** Mike Evans left for San Francisco. Chris Godwin (30) is now WR1. New OC Zac Robinson was hired to modernize the offense — but how much can he transform things with Mayfield eating $53M of cap?
- **Division arms race:** Carolina just signed Jaelan Phillips (4yr/$120M) and Devin Lloyd. Atlanta hired Kevin Stefanski. New Orleans spent $113M+ in free agency. The NFC South is no longer a two-win division.

The Mayfield decision is the fulcrum of the franchise. Extend him and you bet the next half-decade on a quarterback who went 6-2 then 2-7 in the same season. Let him walk and you enter QB purgatory in the hardest the NFC South has been in a decade. There is no clean answer — and that's what makes this the most important question in Tampa Bay.

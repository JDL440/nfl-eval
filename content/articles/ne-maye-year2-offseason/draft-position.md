## The Board at #4

The #4 pick is a franchise-altering asset. Here's who's realistically in play:

1. **David Bailey, EDGE, Texas Tech (6'3", 250 lbs)** — The consensus top pass rusher in this class. Elite bend, speed-to-power conversion, and scheme versatility. If NE believes pass rush wins championships, Bailey is the pick. He's a Day 1 starter who'd transform a defense that needs pressure generation. *(Consensus top-5 prospect.)*

2. **Carnell Tate, WR, Ohio State (6'2", 191 lbs)** — The best receiver in this draft. Long frame, polished route runner, dominant catch-point winner. If Stefon Diggs' ACL rehab creates WR1 uncertainty, Tate gives Maye a true alpha target on a rookie deal for five years. *(Consensus first-round WR, top-5 range is ⚠️ SPECULATIVE — some boards have him closer to 8-12.)*

3. **Francis Mauigoa, OT, Miami (6'6", 325 lbs)** — Rare athletic tackle with franchise left tackle upside. Protects your QB investment for a decade. If NE views Maye's protection as existential, Mauigoa is the answer. *(Consensus top-10 OT, #4 overall is slightly aggressive but defensible.)*

4. **Arvell Reese, EDGE/LB, Ohio State (6'3", 240 lbs)** — Three-down defender with pass rush upside and coverage ability. A Vrabel-type chess piece. *(⚠️ SPECULATIVE at #4 — more commonly mocked 5-10.)*

## Class Depth Assessment

**WR:** Good, not great. Tate and Jordyn Tyson (Arizona State) headline, with Makai Lemon (USC) and Denzel Boston (Washington) as viable Day 2 options. There's no Ja'Marr Chase-tier generational talent, but the class offers starting-caliber receivers into the second round. NE could realistically draft a WR2 at picks 35-50 if they address WR1 through free agency.

**OT:** Strong at the top. Mauigoa, Spencer Fano (Utah), and Monroe Freeling (Georgia) are legit first-rounders. Depth falls off after Day 1. If NE wants a starting tackle, they need to take one early.

**EDGE:** This is the crown jewel position group. Bailey, Mason Thomas (Oklahoma), Cashius Howell (Texas A&M), and Derrick Moore (Michigan) give this class rare depth. NE could trade back and still land an impact rusher in the 10-20 range.

## Trade-Down Math

The #4 pick carries ~1,800 points on the Jimmy Johnson chart. A trade from #4 to #12 (roughly 1,200 points) historically yields the #12 pick plus a late first-rounder or early second plus a Day 3 pick — something like picks 12, 38, and a 2027 third. In a QB-hungry draft where a team leaps for a signal-caller, the premium can exceed chart value by 15-20%.

**The kicker:** EDGE depth makes trading down viable without sacrificing impact. If NE slides to #10-15, they can still land Howell, Thomas, or a top OT like Fano — and pocket extra capital to address WR or secondary depth on Day 2.

## FA Interaction — The Decision Tree

If NE signs a true WR1 in free agency, the calculus at #4 shifts dramatically. Suddenly, Bailey (EDGE) or Mauigoa (OT) becomes the obvious play — you've checked the weapons box and now invest in the trenches. If the WR market disappoints and Diggs' knee remains a question mark, Tate becomes the pick and NE addresses the lines through mid-round selections and FA depth.

## Recommendation: Path C — Hybrid

The draft board screams hybrid approach. Sign a reliable WR in free agency (even a WR2-tier target — you don't need to overpay for a superstar). Then use the #4 pick on Bailey or Mauigoa — whoever the board demands. If a trade-down partner materializes (a QB-needy team trading up), take the call. This EDGE class is deep enough to find impact at #12-15 and you gain the draft capital to fill two holes instead of one.

## Non-Obvious Insight

⚠️ SPECULATIVE: The 2026 OT class has a sharp talent cliff after the top three. If NE doesn't take Mauigoa at #4 or Fano/Freeling in the teens, the next tier of tackles (Proctor, Lomu, Miller) are developmental Day 2 gambles, not Year 1 starters. The OT urgency is higher than the mock draft consensus suggests — this is a "get one early or regret it" class at tackle.

## 🔴 ERRORS — Must Fix Before Publication

### 🔴 E1. Romeo Doubs' 2024 Stats Are Wrong

Line 27: *"a receiver like Romeo Doubs, who posted 55 catches, 724 yards, and 6 touchdowns with Green Bay last season"*

**Actual 2024 stats (verified via Pro Football Reference, StatMuse, Fox Sports):**
- **46 catches** (not 55)
- **601 receiving yards** (not 724)
- **4 touchdowns** (not 6)
- 13 games played, 72 targets

The article overstates catches by 9, yards by 123, and TDs by 2. This is a clear factual error. The narrative point still holds — Doubs is a mid-tier WR2 option — but the numbers must be corrected.

**Fix:** Replace "55 catches, 724 yards, and 6 touchdowns" with "46 catches, 601 yards, and 4 touchdowns."

### 🔴 E2. Mason Thomas's Name Is Incomplete

Line 132: *"Mason Thomas (Oklahoma)"*

The player's name is **R Mason Thomas** (or R. Mason Thomas). The "R" is part of his legal name and appears in NFL.com, ESPN, Bleacher Report, and all major scouting profiles. Dropping the initial could cause confusion or look under-researched.

**Fix:** Replace "Mason Thomas" with "R Mason Thomas" throughout.

## 🟡 SUGGESTIONS — Strong Recommendations

### 🟡 S1. Doubs Contract Figure — Clarify as Hypothetical

Line 27: *"could be available on a 4-year deal worth up to $80 million"*

Doubs is under contract with Green Bay through 2025 (4th-round pick, 2022). He is not a free agent in the 2025 offseason. The "could be available" phrasing partially hedges this, but a reader might interpret it as imminent availability. Consider rephrasing to make the hypothetical nature clearer — e.g., "A receiver in the Doubs tier — 46 catches, 601 yards — might command a 4-year deal worth $15–20M AAV on the open market" or use a genuinely available free agent as the example instead.

### 🟡 S2. Mauigoa Positional Profile — Good Fix, Could Go Further

The revised draft now correctly notes: *"scouts debate whether he's a LT or RT at the next level."* This is accurate — multiple scouting reports (SI, Bleacher Report, PFF, Steelers Depot) confirm the positional debate, with some even projecting a potential move to guard. The article's current framing is honest. For extra precision, the Offense section (line 166) could note that the "zone-based scheme" fit works at either tackle spot, since Mauigoa's run blocking grades are elite regardless of side.

### 🟡 S3. Cap Year Ambiguity — Minor Framing Note

The article references a "$301.2 million cap" (confirmed as the 2026 NFL salary cap per NFL.com, ESPN). The 2025 salary cap is $279.2M. Since the article is framed as a post-2024-season analysis previewing Maye's Year 2, the $301.2M figure reads as the planning cap for the broader two-year window. This works as forward-looking analysis but could briefly confuse cap-literate readers who know the 2025 cap is $279.2M. Consider a brief clarifying note — e.g., "against a projected $301.2 million cap ceiling" — or simply note this is the announced 2026 figure the team is planning around.

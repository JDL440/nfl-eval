## The Numbers on the Table

The Patriots enter 2026 with approximately **$44–46M in effective cap space** (OTC: $43.9M; Spotrac: $45.8M) against a $301.2M salary cap, after accounting for rollover. That's *after* already deploying significant capital: Milton Williams ($28.4M cap hit), Mike Onwenu ($25M), Carlton Davis ($22M), and the Romeo Doubs trade/extension ($8.6M hit, $17M AAV). They've been busy. But they're not done — and the math from here gets interesting.

**Dead money reality:** $33.7M sits on the books in ghosts — Dugger ($12.2M), Diggs ($9.7M), Peppers ($3M). That's 11.2% of the cap doing nothing. The silver lining: nearly all of it evaporates after 2026.

**The Maye multiplier:** Drake Maye's $10M cap number represents just 3.3% of the cap. That's the cheat code. A franchise QB at league-minimum-adjacent cost is an asset worth roughly **$40–45M in annual surplus value** compared to a market-rate extension. Every year you waste that surplus, you're burning money.

Restructure Onwenu and Barmore to free ~$12–15M. Total available: ~$57–61M.

**2027–2028 consequences:** Committed dollars jump to ~$280M+ against a projected ~$315–320M cap. Flexibility drops to ~$35–40M before Maye's 5th-year option decision (May 2028). Restructure treadmill begins. If the WR1 signing busts — and roughly 30% of top-market FA receivers underperform their deal — you're eating $15–20M in dead money. The aggressive path buys weapons *now* but mortgages optionality.

Combined rollover could push 2027 available dollars above $85M. That's elite WR trade acquisition territory *and* Maye extension runway. Maximum flexibility preserved.

## My Recommendation: Controlled Aggression — Lean Scenario A, But Structure Smart

I advocate spending — but *structuring* it correctly. Here's why:

The rookie-deal window is a **two-year asset that depreciates to zero.** In 2026 and 2027, NE gets Maye at $10–12M. By 2028, they're looking at a $45–55M AAV extension if he develops as hoped. The surplus value disappears. Every dollar of cap space unused during this window is a dollar that *could have supported a franchise QB on a discount* and didn't.

Sign the WR1. But backload the deal into years 3–4, keep year-1 cap hit under $18M, and preserve $12–15M in rollover. Use the 2026 draft (NE has capital) to fill RT and depth cheaply. This isn't about recklessness — it's about recognizing the *time value* of cap space.

## The Non-Obvious Point

⚠️ *The dead money cliff is NE's hidden second wave.* That $33.7M in 2026 dead money drops to roughly ~$10–12M in 2027 *(estimated — exact figures depend on remaining proration schedules)*. Combined with cap growth, NE could be sitting on **$85M+ in 2027 space** — enough to course-correct if 2026 signings disappoint, or double down if Maye takes the leap. The Patriots don't just have cap space; they have cap *momentum*.

# Idea: Green Bay Packers — 2026 Offseason

## Working Title
**"Green Bay Bet Everything on Micah Parsons. Then He Tore His ACL. Now What?"**

## Angle / Tension
The Packers made the most aggressive all-in move of the 2025 NFL cycle: trading two first-round picks (2026 + 2027) and Kenny Clark for Micah Parsons, then signing him to a 4yr/$188M extension — the richest non-QB deal in NFL history. It was a franchise-defining gamble on pairing Parsons with Jordan Love to create a championship-caliber team during Love's prime.

Then Parsons tore his ACL in Week 15.

Now Green Bay faces the consequences of going all-in with no safety net. They have no first-round pick for two consecutive drafts. They're carrying $39.2M in dead money from the Parsons trade fallout (Gary and Clark). Their biggest roster hole — cornerback — has no premium draft pick to fill it. Their new defensive coordinator (Gannon) is installing a scheme that demands different personnel than what Jeff Hafley built. And the franchise player who justified all of this is rehabbing a torn knee, targeting a Week 3-5 return at best.

**The core tension:** Gutekunst went all-in on Love's prime window. The cost was two years of first-round picks, $39M in dead cap, and roster holes at CB, EDGE depth, and WR that can't be filled with premium draft capital. If Parsons comes back healthy, this is a Super Bowl roster. If he doesn't — or if the secondary can't hold up without a CB1 — Green Bay mortgaged its future for a Wild Card exit. There is no Plan B. That's either the mark of conviction or catastrophic overcommitment.

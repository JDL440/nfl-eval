## Panel Rationale

### Why these three (not more, not fewer)
- **GB is essential** — this is a franchise-identity article. No team expert = no connective tissue.
- **Cap is essential** — the "all-in gamble" framing lives or dies on the financial math. Dead money, extension timelines, and 2027 cap relief are the backbone of the argument.
- **Defense is essential** — the Gannon transition + Parsons ACL + CB crisis are all defensive questions. Without a scheme expert, the "can they actually win" question has no football answer.

### Why NOT Offense
The offensive side of the ball is stable. Love is locked in. The OL is extended (Tom, Banks, Rhyan). The WR room lost Doubs but has Watson/Reed/Golden. Offensive questions exist but aren't the PRIMARY tension. If the panel were 4 agents, Offense would be the add — but at Depth 2, three voices are the right number.

### Why NOT Draft
No first-round pick limits draft impact. The R2 pick (#52) matters, but it's one piece — not the article's engine. Draft strategy can be addressed within GB's and Defense's responses (CB target in R3, DT in R2).

### Expected Disagreements
- **GB vs. Cap:** GB may argue the roster is "good enough" to compete; Cap will show the financial constraints that limit fixes.
- **GB vs. Defense:** GB may emphasize Parsons' positive rehab updates; Defense will project what happens IF he's not back or not 100%.
- **Cap vs. Defense:** Cap may identify affordable CB options; Defense will evaluate whether those affordable options are actually good enough for Gannon's scheme.

## Angle / Tension
Cincinnati went 6-11 in 2025 — Burrow missed time with injury again, and the Bengals missed the playoffs for a third consecutive season. The most consequential offseason decision: letting four-time Pro Bowl DE Trey Hendrickson leave in free agency without even franchise-tagging him. Hendrickson had back-to-back 17.5-sack seasons before 2025 and was the defense's only elite pass rusher.

**The core tension:** The Bengals have money ($35-53M in cap space), draft capital (#10 overall), and have already started spending — signing S Bryan Cook, EDGE Boye Mafe, and DT Jonathan Allen. But this is a "layered rebuild" strategy, adding multiple mid-tier pieces rather than one elite replacement for Hendrickson. Joe Burrow's $48M cap hit is the franchise's bet on the future, but three straight playoff misses and a defense that allowed 2,500 rushing yards means the window is fogging up. At 29, Burrow is still in his prime — but how many more 6-11 seasons before the frustration becomes a trade request?

Can Cincinnati's volume approach to defense — multiple good players instead of one great one — deliver results fast enough to matter?

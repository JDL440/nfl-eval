# Discussion Prompt: The Eagles Have Everything Except a Pass Rush — and the Trade That Could Fix It Might Break the Defense

**Depth Level:** 2 — The Beat

## The Core Question

Can the Eagles solve their catastrophic pass-rush void without dismantling the defensive interior that made Vic Fangio's scheme work — and can Howie Roseman do it with $6.4M in effective cap space and $52M in dead money?

## Key Tensions

- **Trade Carter for a blue-chip EDGE vs. extend Carter and find pass rush elsewhere:** If Carter goes to CIN for #10 overall, the Eagles land a premier EDGE prospect (Zion Young, Akheem Mesidor) and free $6.9M in cap space. But they lose a 24-year-old 2x Pro Bowl interior anchor who, alongside Jordan Davis, gives Fangio the dominant DT tandem that makes the 3-4 work. Extending Carter at $50M+ AAV locks in the defense's best player but commits ~$76M/year to two defensive tackles — unprecedented — and leaves essentially zero cap room to sign an edge rusher externally. Which path gets closer to a Super Bowl?

- **Dead money as a hidden governor:** The Eagles carry $52M in dead cap (8th in the NFL). Huff ($16.6M) and Slay ($13.3M) alone eat $30M for players who aren't on the roster. This clears after 2026 — the 2027 projected cap is $103.5M — but right now, it means every dollar of cap gymnastics (Hurts restructure, Brown restructure) is being used to dig out of a hole before they can spend on actual players. How much can restructures realistically create, and is it enough to both extend Carter AND sign edge help?

- **The EDGE room is a five-alarm fire:** After Phillips left for $120M, the Eagles' edge rushers are Nolan Smith Jr. (career 7 sacks in 2 seasons), Jalyx Hunt (Day 2 project), Arnold Ebiketie (1yr/$7.3M stopgap), and Jose Ramirez. Not one has ever been a full-time starter. Fangio's 3-4 can mask mediocre edge players with interior pressure from Carter/Davis — but if Carter is traded, the entire pass rush collapses from both the interior AND the edge. Is there a realistic path to a competent pass rush without either trading for a premium EDGE or using the #23 pick on one?

- **The NFC East arms race changes the urgency calculus:** Washington signed Oweh ($100M), has $66M in cap space, and Jayden Daniels is on a rookie deal. Dallas tagged Pickens and traded for Rashan Gary. The Giants hired John Harbaugh. Standing pat is not an option — the division is improving while Philadelphia is treading water. Does this force Roseman into a more aggressive posture than the cap situation warrants?

## The Paths

**Path 1 — Extend Carter, Draft for the Rest:** Lock up Carter at $50M+ AAV. Restructure Hurts and Brown to create ~$35M in 2026 space. Draft EDGE at #23 (Young/Mesidor), TE at #54 (Sadiq if he slides, or Klare/Endries). Sign a mid-tier veteran safety. Accept that the 2026 pass rush depends on a rookie + Ebiketie + Nolan Smith. Bet on the interior dominance (Carter + Davis) masking edge deficiencies. Risk: if the rookie EDGE doesn't hit, the pass rush is still broken.

**Path 2 — Trade Carter for #10, Rebuild the Edge:** Send Carter to CIN for #10 overall. Draft a blue-chip EDGE at #10. Use #23 on Kenyon Sadiq (TE). Free up $6.9M in cap space. Sign a mid-tier EDGE (Clowney? Campbell? bargain bin) to complement the rookie. Accept a significant interior downgrade. Risk: Davis alone can't replicate what Carter + Davis did together. Fangio's scheme loses its engine.

**Path 3 — Extend Carter, Trade Up for EDGE:** Lock up Carter. Package #23 + #68 (or future capital) to trade into the top 15 for a premium EDGE. Fill TE in R4 or via veteran FA. Maximum investment in 2026 roster. Risk: gutting draft capital for a win-now push when the window is 2-3 years, not just this year.

**Path 4 — Extend Carter, Bet on Internal Development:** Extend Carter. Do NOT use #23 on EDGE. Instead, draft Sadiq at #23 (best available TE). Bet that Nolan Smith (25) takes a Year 3 leap and Ebiketie provides competent starting-level play. Use remaining picks on EDGE depth (R3, R4). Cheapest path. Risk: if neither Smith nor Ebiketie becomes a quality starter, the Eagles wasted their championship window.

## Panel Instructions

### Cap — Salary Cap Expert
**Focus:** The financial engineering of every Carter scenario. Model the restructure waterfall: start at $6.4M effective, show Hurts restructure (+$20M), Brown restructure (+$15M), and any other candidates. Then model both paths — Carter extension (what does $50M+ AAV look like in 2026-2029? dead money implications?) vs. Carter trade ($6.9M in immediate savings + rookie salary at #10). How does each scenario affect the Eagles' ability to sign an EDGE in free agency? Compare to historical DT extension pairs. Project the 2027 cap under each scenario — does the massive $103.5M in projected space give them room to absorb a Carter mega-deal? **Stay in the financial mechanics lane. Do not evaluate player talent or scheme fit.**

### PHI — Philadelphia Eagles Expert
**Focus:** The organizational and roster-level evaluation of the Carter decision. What is Carter's actual on-field value beyond Pro Bowl nods — snap counts, pressure rate, run stop win rate? How does Nolan Smith Jr. project as a full-time EDGE starter — what did he show in 2025? Evaluate the Ebiketie signing — is he a real starter or a bridge? Where does the TE room stand after Goedert's departure — is Calcaterra legitimately a starting NFL tight end? What is the Eagles' organizational DNA under Roseman — does he trade stars, or does he extend and build? How does the Sirianni-Fangio dynamic influence roster construction? **Stay in the football evaluation lane. Defer to Cap for financial mechanics and Defense for scheme analysis.**

### Defense — Defensive Scheme Expert
**Focus:** What does Fangio's 3-4 defense look like in each scenario? With Carter + Davis anchoring the interior, the defense can generate pressure without elite EDGE play — how much does that change if Carter leaves? Identify the replacement archetype: what kind of Day 2 DT could approximate 60-70% of Carter's interior pressure alongside Davis? On the EDGE side, what scheme adjustments can Fangio make to generate a pass rush from Nolan Smith / Hunt / Ebiketie — more blitz packages? More stunts with Davis? Is there a scheme-level answer to a talent-level problem? If the Eagles draft Young or Mesidor at #23, what's the realistic Year 1 contribution in Fangio's system? **Stay in the scheme and personnel lane. Do not evaluate cap mechanics or organizational history.**

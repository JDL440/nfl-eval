# PHI Position Statement — The Carter Decision

## Carter's On-Field Value

Jalen Carter is not a "good young player." He's the most disruptive interior defender in the NFC at 24 years old. His two consecutive Pro Bowl selections came behind 55+ pressures each season, a pass-rush win rate consistently above 12% from the interior — elite by any DT standard — and a run stop win rate that graded him top-5 among 3-techniques in 2025. Carter played 70%+ of defensive snaps both years, which matters: he's not a rotational piece getting fresh-legs production. He's a three-down anchor. The Davis-Carter tandem is the only interior pairing in football that can generate consistent four-man pressure without relying on edge talent. That's not replaceable with pick #10 and a rookie salary.

## The EDGE Room Is Worse Than It Looks

Nolan Smith Jr.'s career 7 sacks across two seasons isn't just underwhelming — it's the production of a rotational sub-package rusher, not a full-time starter. He flashed in limited pass-rush snaps but has never sustained a full game's worth of run defense and rush responsibility. Betting the franchise on a Year 3 leap from a player who hasn't shown starter-level consistency is hope dressed as strategy. Ebiketie at 1yr/$7.3M is what he is: a bridge. Atlanta let him walk for a reason. He's a competent run defender who posted 5.5 sacks in his best season. That's an EDGE3, not an EDGE1. Jalyx Hunt and Jose Ramirez are practice squad and Day 3 depth, respectively. This room needs a legitimate starter from outside the building — either at #23 in the draft or via trade.

## TE Room and Goedert's Ghost

Goedert's 11 touchdowns in 2025 were the most by an Eagles tight end since Zach Ertz in 2018. Calcaterra is a willing blocker with limited route-running upside who has never topped 300 receiving yards. He's a TE2 on most rosters. This is a top-three need alongside EDGE and safety, and it should drive the #54 pick.

## Roseman's DNA Says Extend

Howie Roseman does not trade homegrown stars in their prime. He extended Hurts, extended Lane Johnson, extended Mailata, extended Davis. He trades *for* stars (Barkley, Brown, Phillips mid-season). The only major departures under Roseman are players who left in free agency (Foles, Bradham, Hargrave) or were cut after decline (Slay, Bradberry). Trading a 24-year-old Carter would be the most un-Roseman move in his 16-year tenure. I'd put the probability at under 15%.

## The Competitive Window Insight Others Will Miss

The Eagles' 2027 cap explosion ($103.5M projected) is not just financial relief — it's a *strategic weapon*. Extend Carter now, accept a mediocre 2026 EDGE room built on a #23 rookie + Ebiketie + Nolan Smith, and then attack the 2027 free agent EDGE market with $90M+ in space when Huff and Slay's dead money finally clears. The Commanders' spending spree is front-loaded on Daniels' rookie deal; when his extension hits in 2028, their cap advantage vanishes. Philadelphia can afford to be patient on EDGE for one season if the interior stays elite. Washington cannot sustain this spending indefinitely.

**Recommended path:** Extend Carter. Draft EDGE at #23 (Young or Mesidor). Draft TE at #54. Accept a transitional 2026 pass rush and position for a dominant 2027 roster reload.

# Idea: Cleveland Browns — 2026 Offseason

## Working Title
**"Todd Monken Has Three QBs, $93M in O-Line Spending, and No Idea Who's Starting. Welcome to Cleveland."**

## Angle / Tension
The Browns fired Kevin Stefanski after a dismal 2025 season and hired Todd Monken — Baltimore's former OC who built the Ravens' top-tier run game. Monken immediately went on a spending spree to fix the offensive line: Zion Johnson ($49.5M), Elgton Jenkins ($24M), and a trade for Tytus Howard. The message is clear: fix the trenches first.

**The core tension:** Cleveland has three quarterbacks and no answer. Deshaun Watson is returning from an Achilles tear with a restructured-but-still-massive contract. Shedeur Sanders was drafted as the future. Dillon Gabriel is the wild card. Monken has publicly said Watson will "get a chance" — but the three-man competition creates a strategic paradox. If Watson starts and fails, the Browns have wasted another year of their defensive core (Myles Garrett, Denzel Ward, Greg Newsome). If Sanders starts and struggles, they've rushed a rookie behind a rebuilt-but-unproven line. If Gabriel starts... who is Dillon Gabriel?

The Browns hold the #6 AND #24 overall picks — rare two-first-round-pick draft capital. Do they use it to surround whichever QB emerges, or does the QB room chaos mean the real answer is in this draft?

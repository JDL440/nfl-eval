# Discussion Prompt: Seahawks RB Pick #64 Analysis (v2)

**Depth Level:** 2 — The Beat

## The Core Question

**After another week of free agency clarified Seattle's real roster pressure points, is spending Pick #64 on a running back still the smartest move the Seahawks can make — and if so, is Jadarian Price still the right bet despite Zach Charbonnet's ACL rehab, Price's old Achilles tear, and sharper needs at cornerback and edge?**

## Key Tensions

- **RB need vs. more urgent needs:** Seattle still lost Kenneth Walker III and cannot blindly count on Charbonnet for Week 1, but the post-free-agency board now looks harsher at CB and EDGE after losing Riq Woolen, Coby Bryant, and Boye Mafe. Is RB still worthy of Seattle's best Day 2 asset?

- **Scheme fit vs. medical risk:** Brian Fleury's wide-zone background still points toward a one-cut, explosive committee back. Price looks like that fit on paper. But the article cannot recycle the old answer without re-testing both Charbonnet's ACL timeline and whether Price's Achilles history remains a fair discount in 2026 draft rooms.

- **Original thesis vs. refreshed context:** The v1 article was built before the Editor workflow, before the latest Seahawks roster updates, and before current article conventions. The v2 needs to preserve the core insight if it still holds, but it also needs to pressure-test whether Seattle's changing roster picture made the original recommendation too RB-centric.

- **Draft pick vs. veteran bridge:** The v1 argument paired Price at #64 with a veteran bridge signing. Does that still make sense with Seattle sitting on only four draft picks and roughly $40M+ in cap space, or has the market/roster changed enough that the veteran component needs a different recommendation?

## The Paths

### Path 1: Reaffirm the Original Thesis
Seattle should still target Jadarian Price at #64, pair him with a low-cost veteran bridge, and let Charbonnet come back on his own timeline. The updated context changes the framing, not the answer.

### Path 2: Keep the Player, Move the Price
Price still fits Seattle, but the stronger argument is now "target him only if he slides to #96 or after a trade-down," because #64 may be too rich with CB and EDGE needs worsening.

### Path 3: Keep the Need, Change the Prospect
Seattle still needs a rookie RB, but Price is no longer the cleanest answer. Another Day 2/early Day 3 back or a more veteran-heavy bridge is the smarter update.

### Path 4: Deprioritize RB Entirely
Seattle should trust Charbonnet's recovery, use Wilson/Holani as placeholder depth, and spend #64 on CB/EDGE/IOL because the roster pressure elsewhere is too severe.

## Panel Composition Rationale

**Why these 4 agents:**

1. **SEA** — Required for any Seahawks-specific article. This is the agent who can judge the current need stack honestly: Walker is gone, Charbonnet is uncertain, but the roster also got thinner at CB and EDGE. The article is only interesting if it answers the team-building question, not just the prospect question.

2. **Injury** — The entire v1 thesis leaned on medical interpretation: Charbonnet's ACL created urgency, and Price's Achilles created value. A v2 without a refreshed medical lane would just be a rewrite, not a regeneration.

3. **CollegeScout** — Price is the center of gravity for the piece. v2 must re-test whether he truly belongs at #64 in the current class rather than inheriting the original recommendation. CollegeScout owns that board reality and the "what if Seattle waits?" counterfactual.

4. **Offense** — Fleury's scheme was a major pillar of the original article and still matters. Offense determines whether Price is a schematic target or merely a useful player. That is the difference between "smartest play" and "nice luxury."

**Why not Cap:**
- The financial side is secondary here. Seattle has enough cap flexibility that the harder question is draft-resource allocation, not whether a rookie contract fits. SEA can cover the resource tradeoff at a roster level without turning this into a contract article.

**Why not Media:**
- Media is useful for real-time veteran-market validation, but the central tension is not "what are people saying?" It is whether Seattle should spend #64 on RB at all. Veteran bridge names can be refreshed during writing once the panel's football conclusion is set.

**Coverage check:**
- ✅ Team need hierarchy and roster pressure (SEA)
- ✅ Medical risk and recovery timelines (Injury)
- ✅ Prospect quality and board reality (CollegeScout)
- ✅ Scheme fit and deployment logic (Offense)
- ✅ Clear disagreement potential on value vs. urgency

**Expected disagreements:**
- **SEA vs. Offense:** Offense may still see strong scheme logic for Price at #64 while SEA may argue CB/EDGE scarcity makes the pick too expensive
- **CollegeScout vs. v1 thesis:** CollegeScout may come in cooler on Price's board value than the original article did
- **Injury vs. original urgency framing:** If Charbonnet's timeline looks better than v1 assumed, the "must add Week 1 insurance" angle may soften

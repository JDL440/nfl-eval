## Bottom Line Up Front

**Price is a legitimate Round 2 talent. He is NOT the discount the v1 article claimed.**

The v1 article positioned Price at #64 as a late-first-round talent falling due to Achilles fear — "buying the market's fear at a discount." That framing was promotional, not analytical. After re-testing the board, here's what I actually see:

- Price's consensus ADP has **risen** since v1 was written, not fallen. Multiple boards now slot him at #53-58 overall — PFF has him at ADP 58, StatRankings at ~56. He is a mid-second-round prospect, full stop.
- The Achilles "discount" has **eroded**. Four years and 41 games of healthy production have done what they should — moved teams off the medical flag. The 15-30 pick slide v1 claimed may have been real a year ago. It is smaller now.
- At #64, Seattle is paying **fair market price** for Price. That's not a bad thing. But it is not a steal.

> **Quotable:** *"The v1 article told you Price was a late-first-round talent you could steal in Round 2. He's actually a mid-second-round talent you'd draft at face value. That's a meaningful difference when you only have four picks."*

## Re-Grading Price as a Pick #64 Candidate

### What's Real About Price

Everything in his scouting profile is solid. I'm not downgrading the player. I'm downgrading the *narrative* around the player.

| Trait | Grade | Notes |
|-------|-------|-------|
| Burst through the hole | B+ | One-cut decisiveness is real. His 6.0 career YPC at Notre Dame — against a top-10 SOS — is legitimate |
| Zone scheme fit | A- | Notre Dame's Denbrock system ran zone + gap hybrid. Clean translation to Fleury's Shanahan tree |
| Character / Culture | A+ | Zero red flags. Shared a backfield with the RB1 in the class and never blinked. First-Team All-American returner because he did the dirty work |
| Return value | A | 37.5 yards/return in 2025, 3 career KR TDs. This is real NFL special teams value |
| Speed | B | 4.49 forty. Adequate, not explosive. This RB class has burners (Washington Jr. 4.33, Love 4.36, Singleton ~4.39). Price is the steady, efficient option |
| Receiving | C+ | 15 career catches. Growing, but underdeveloped. Shanahan backs need to catch — this is a development area, not a strength |
| Pass protection | C+ | Willing but technique needs NFL coaching. Limited reps due to committee role at ND |
| Workload history | C+ | Never carried 20+ times in a game. Committee back his entire career. Unproven as a volume runner |
| Ball security | C | 3 fumbles in 2025 (2.7% rate). Not medically linked to Achilles — this is technique. But it's real and it's concerning |

**Overall prospect grade: Mid-Round 2 — pick #50-65 range**

Price is a well-rounded, high-floor committee back with special teams upside. He projects as an RB2 who can develop into an RB1B in a multi-back system. He is NOT a feature-back prospect. He is NOT a first-round talent sliding due to injury. He's a solid Day 2 pick who does a lot of things well and nothing at an elite level beyond returning kicks.

> **Quotable:** *"Price's tape says 'reliable committee back with upside.' The v1 article said 'late first-round talent at a discount.' I trust the tape."*

## The Opportunity Cost at #64

This is not my primary lane — SEA owns the need hierarchy — but CollegeScout does own the board, and the board says something important:

### What's Available at #64 vs. #96 by Position

| Position | Quality at #64 | Quality at #96 | Dropoff |
|----------|---------------|----------------|---------|
| **RB** | Price (mid-R2 talent) | Coleman/Johnson (R3 talent, 70-80% of Price) | **Small** |
| **EDGE** | Gabe Jacas (Illinois), Derrick Moore (Michigan), Dani Dennis-Sutton (Penn State) | Significant dropoff — Day 3 developmental types | **Large** |
| **CB** | Keionte Scott (Miami), Keith Abney II (Arizona State), Treydan Stukes (Arizona) | Significant dropoff — camp bodies and projects | **Large** |

**The draft-value principle:** Spend your premium pick where the positional dropoff is steepest. RB has the shallowest cliff from Round 2 to Round 3 in this class. EDGE and CB have the steepest.

> **Quotable:** *"The dropoff from Pick #64 to Pick #96 at running back is a gentle slope. At EDGE and corner, it's a cliff. That should tell Seattle everything they need to know about where to spend their capital."*

## Where I Likely Disagree With Other Panelists

### vs. Offense (predicted)
Offense will likely argue Price's scheme fit justifies the pick — that Fleury's system specifically needs a one-cut zone runner and Price is the cleanest translation. I agree on the scheme fit. I disagree that the scheme fit makes him *unique*. Coleman ran zone at Washington. Johnson ran zone at Nebraska. The scheme argument narrows the field less than Offense will claim because multiple Day 2/3 backs have zone experience.

### vs. the v1 Thesis
The v1 claimed the Achilles was suppressing Price's draft stock by "15-30 picks" and that "without it, a 6.0 YPC All-American returner is a late Round 1 talent." I respectfully call foul. Price was the RB2 at his own school — a committee back with 280 career carries, 15 career catches, and 3 fumbles in his final season. Without the Achilles, he's still a mid-R2 pick. He was never a first-round talent. He's a good player whose medical history added narrative drama that the original article leaned into too heavily.

### vs. Injury (predicted)
Injury will re-test the Achilles risk and likely clear it as a fading concern. I agree — the medical risk is minimal at this point. But that means the "discount" is also minimal. If the Achilles doesn't scare teams anymore, it also doesn't create a buying opportunity for Seattle.

## My Recommendation

**Path 2 — Keep the Player, Move the Price.**

Price still fits Seattle. He's a quality prospect with zone-scheme experience, elite character, and legitimate kick return value. But the v2 article should not repeat the v1's mistake of calling this a steal. Here's my framework:

| Scenario | CollegeScout's Assessment |
|----------|--------------------------|
| Price at #64, EDGE/CB at #96 | 🟡 Defensible but suboptimal. You're paying fair value for RB and getting scraps at positions with steeper dropoffs |
| EDGE/CB at #64, Price at #96 | ❌ Unlikely — Price probably goes between #53-65. He won't be there at #96 |
| EDGE/CB at #64, Coleman/Johnson at #96 | 🟢 Best value play. You get a quality zone back AND address a premium position |
| Price at #64, best player available at #96 | 🟡 Fine if Seattle truly believes Price is their guy AND the EDGE/CB options at #64 don't excite them |

**If the article's recommendation stays "Price at #64," I can support it — but only if Writer frames it as "paying fair value for a good scheme fit" rather than "stealing a first-round talent in Round 2."** The honest version of this article is still interesting. It just can't be the fairy tale the v1 told.

# Article Idea: The Missing Joker — Sean Payton's Tight End Problem Could Define Denver's Championship Window

## Working Title
The Missing Joker — Sean Payton's Tight End Problem Could Define Denver's Championship Window

## Angle / Tension
Denver went 14-3 and reached the AFC Championship Game — then lost 10-7 to New England with backup Jarrett Stidham behind center after Bo Nix's ankle fracture. But the offensive limitations were visible well before Nix went down. Evan Engram, signed to fill Sean Payton's signature "Joker" tight end role (the mismatch weapon that defined his Saints offenses with Jimmy Graham and Jared Cook), delivered a disappointing 50 catches, 461 yards, and 1 touchdown in 2025. At 31 years old with a $14.2M cap hit, he's the definition of a failed experiment.

The core tension: Sean Payton has never won big without an elite tight end. Denver is 30 minutes from the Super Bowl, Bo Nix is on a cheap rookie deal through 2027, and the championship window is wide open — but the position Payton's entire offensive identity revolves around is the roster's biggest weakness. Do the Broncos draft Kenyon Sadiq (Oregon's "freak athlete" TE) at pick #30 even though consensus says CJ Allen (LB, Georgia) is the smarter pick? Do they gamble that Davis Webb's new playcalling vision can succeed without a Joker? Or is tight end the one investment that turns a 14-win team into a champion?

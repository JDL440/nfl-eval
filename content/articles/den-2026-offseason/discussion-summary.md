## Central Question

Can Denver get over the championship hump without the elite tight end that has been the centerpiece of every Sean Payton offense that won big — or is TE the single investment that turns a 14-win AFCCG team into a Super Bowl champion?

---

## Panel Consensus

All three panelists agree on two things:

1. **Evan Engram failed the scheme and should be released.** Cap recommends post-June 1 designation for cap-flow optimization ($5.2M deferred to 2027). DEN confirms the locker room is done with Engram — effort and drop rate (14 drops on 82 targets) sealed the verdict. Offense frames it as architectural failure, not just talent mismatch.

2. **Denver's championship window is 2026–2027, defined by Nix's rookie deal.** Cap puts it in dollar terms: $5M/yr through 2027 creates ~$30M in surplus value. DEN and Offense translate that to urgency: the AFCCG loss (10–7 to New England) exposed an offensive ceiling that won't fix itself.

---

## Core Disagreement: Pick #30

This is the article's engine — a genuine 2-vs-1 split.

### DEN + Offense → Kenyon Sadiq (TE, Oregon) at #30

**DEN's case:** ILB is more *urgent* than TE, but TE is more *impactful*. Denver's 52% red-zone TD rate (25th in NFL) is the ceiling problem. Alex Singleton can hold for one more year at ILB. Sadiq transforms the offense from "very good" to "unstoppable in January."

**Offense's case:** Davis Webb's first year as playcaller makes the Joker role MORE critical, not less. Webb lacks Payton's 20 years of real-time pattern recognition — the TE is his structural support. Sadiq's combine profile (4.58/38"/6'5"/251) matches Jimmy Graham's athleticism. The Nix ankle timeline (earliest May OTAs) means a R1 TE gets 4–5 months of chemistry-building; a R2 TE gets only July training camp.

### Cap → CJ Allen (ILB, Georgia) at #30, TE in Round 2

**Cap's case:** R1 TE hit rates are poor (Pitts, Kincaid, Hockenson all needed Year 2+ to produce). Sadiq won't be 2011 Graham in Year 1. Allen at #30 + Jack Endries/Eli Stowers at #62 saves ~$1.3M/year and addresses the clearer defensive need. ILB with Singleton at 32 is a ticking clock.

**Cap's strongest point:** Graham caught 31/356/5 as a rookie before breaking out in Year 2 (99/1,310/11). If Sadiq follows the same arc, Denver doesn't get the Joker boost in the championship window's most critical year.

## Lead's Synthesis

The panel produced a clean, structured disagreement that maps directly to the article's tension. Cap's dissent is financially rigorous and historically grounded — R1 TEs are genuinely risky investments. But DEN and Offense converge on the urgency argument: championship windows measured in years don't survive "wait and see" on the position that defines your head coach's offensive identity.

**Swing variable:** Nix's ankle recovery timeline. If he's full-speed by May, the Sadiq pick becomes high-ceiling. If he's limited until camp, the chemistry-building argument weakens and Cap's R2 patience case strengthens.

**Key quote for Writer:** "Cap isn't wrong. He's just losing on urgency." — DEN's framing of why financial prudence yields to window pressure.

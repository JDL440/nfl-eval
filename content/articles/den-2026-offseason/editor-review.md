## 🟡 SUGGESTIONS

### 1. Name the AFCCG opponent

The draft never identifies New England as the AFC Championship Game opponent. Readers need this — the opponent matters for context (defensive scheme Denver faced, Stidham managing 133 passing yards, etc.). The idea file and discussion prompt both name New England explicitly.

**Location:** Paragraph 3 (line 16) and/or the Verdict section (line 151).  
**Fix:** Add "against New England" or "to New England" at the relevant reference.

### 2. Jimmy Graham's listed height: 6'7" vs. official Combine measurement

The comparison table lists Graham at 6'7", 260. His official NFL Combine measurement was 6'6 2/8" (per Draft Scout, MockDraftable). NFL.com and some references round to 6'7", while Pro Football Reference lists 6'6". This isn't wrong — it depends on source — but for a data-driven article, using the official Combine measurement (6'6") or noting the discrepancy would be more precise, especially since Sadiq's measurements appear to use Combine figures.

**Location:** Comparison table, Section 3 (line 68).

### 3. Clarify pre-June 1 vs. post-June 1 savings in the Engram table

The Engram scenarios table (lines 27–31) describes the pre-June 1 cut as offering "limited relief" while the post-June 1 cut shows "~$3.8M usable 2026 savings." Per Cap's position, both paths produce $3.8M in gross cap savings. The real advantage of post-June 1 is better dead-money distribution — $5.2M deferred to 2027 instead of $10.4M all hitting 2026. The current table wording could leave readers thinking pre-June 1 saves less money, when the difference is timing, not amount.

**Fix:** Adjust the pre-June 1 "Why It Doesn't" column to note the $3.8M savings exists but comes with $10.4M dead money concentrated in 2026, or add a numeric column.

### 4. Specify which game produced Nix's ankle fracture

The draft mentions Nix's ankle recovery (line 115) without stating when the injury occurred. The idea file says "Divisional Round vs BUF" (with SI/Broncos Wire citations). Adding this detail sharpens the timeline and explains why Stidham played the AFCCG.

**Location:** Section 5 constraint table or the paragraph at line 118.

### 5. Expand the recovery-timeline urgency argument slightly

The Offense position made Nix's ankle fracture recovery the centerpiece of its timing case: earliest full-speed return at May OTAs, the two-month gap between R1 and R2 TE arrival, and the Manning–Julius Thomas chemistry comparison. The draft compresses this to one table row and a single paragraph (lines 115–120). Given this is one of Offense's strongest and most original contributions, another 2–3 sentences developing the May-vs-July installation gap would strengthen the urgency argument without bloating the piece.

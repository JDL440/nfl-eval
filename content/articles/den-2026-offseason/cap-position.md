## The Math Denver Can't Escape

**Recommended path:** Cut Engram post-June 1, accept the dead money, and invest the savings in a rookie TE. The Engram contract is a sunk cost — the only question is whether Denver pays $14.2M for diminishing production or $10.4M in dead money to create ~$3.8M in usable space. The roster-value tradeoff isn't close.

### The Three Engram Scenarios — All Bad, One Least Bad

Denver's 2026 cap situation is **$14.6M in effective space** (OTC, 19th in NFL). That's *before* signing the draft class (~$11.7M required for 9 picks). Functional space after rookie signings: ~$2.9M. Every dollar matters.

| Scenario | 2026 Cap Hit | 2027 Cap Hit | Dead Money | Cap Savings | Roster Consequences |
|----------|-------------|-------------|------------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Keep Engram** | $14.2M | $0 (contract expires) | $0 | $0 | 7th-highest cap hit for a TE who posted 50/461/1 in 2025 |
| **Cut pre-June 1** | $10.4M dead | $0 | $10.4M | $3.8M immediate | Same dead money, accelerated to 2026 — no 2027 relief |
| **Cut post-June 1** | $5.2M dead (2026) + $5.2M dead (2027) | $5.2M dead | $10.4M split | $9.0M effective 2026, $3.8M usable now | Best cash flow: frees $3.8M for draft/in-season, pushes $5.2M to 2027 when cap expands |

**My call:** Post-June 1 designation. It's the only path that creates usable 2026 space ($3.8M) while deferring half the dead money to 2027, when Denver projects to have **~$28M in effective space** (OTC preliminary 2027 cap page, assuming $327M cap and current commitments).

The counterargument — "keep Engram as a veteran safety net for a rookie TE" — ignores the film. Payton benched Engram in Week 17 and the AFCCG. If the coach doesn't trust him in January, he's not worth $14.2M in cap space.

### What $3.8M Buys Denver in 2026

Not much — but enough to matter.

**Option A: Veteran TE bridge ($2.5–3.5M AAV)**
- Comp: Hunter Henry-type rental (33 years old, blocking-first, safety valve). The 2026 FA TE market includes Tyler Conklin (31, $3M market estimate), Mo Alie-Cox (31, $2.8M), and Albert Okwuegbunam (27, $2.5M).
- **Pro:** Immediate contributor, fills the TE1 role while a rookie develops.
- **Con:** None of these are Joker-caliber weapons. You're still drafting TE to solve the scheme problem.

**Option B: Roll the savings into in-season flexibility**
- Keep the $3.8M as emergency reserve for mid-season moves: trade deadline WR acquisition, injury replacement, or mid-year extension for a breakout player.
- **Pro:** Maximizes optionality during the championship window.
- **Con:** Leaves TE completely unaddressed if the rookie draft pick busts.

**Option C: Front-load a 2027 free agent signing**
- Use the $3.8M to front-load a multi-year deal (e.g., OL depth, safety, or ILB) that would otherwise eat 2027 space.
- **Pro:** Cap smoothing — reduces 2027 commitments when McGlinchey ($23.8M) becomes a potential casualty.
- **Con:** Doesn't solve the TE problem.

I lean Option B. The Broncos are in a championship window with Nix on a rookie deal ($5M/year through 2027). That window closes when Nix's extension kicks in (projected 2028 at $50–55M AAV if he continues developing). Every dollar of in-season flexibility is worth more than a replacement-level veteran TE.

### The R1 vs. R2 TE Cap Cost Comparison

The draft economics favor Round 2.

| Pick | Rookie Pool Cost | Year 1 Cap Hit | 4-Year Value | Notes |
|------|------------------|----------------|--------------|-------|
| **#30 (R1) — Kenyon Sadiq** | ~$3.1M (estimated) | ~$3.1M | ~$12.4M total | 5th-year option (2030) adds control but increases cost |
| **#62 (R2) — Jack Endries/Eli Stowers** | ~$1.8M (estimated) | ~$1.8M | ~$7.2M total | No 5th-year option; cheaper 4-year window |

**Cap verdict:** R2 TE saves ~$1.3M/year in cap cost and ~$5.2M over 4 years. That's not trivial — it's the difference between keeping a rotational edge rusher or letting him walk in 2027.

But here's the tension: **scheme value vs. cap efficiency**. If Sadiq at #30 becomes Jimmy Graham 2.0 (unlikely but possible), the $1.3M annual upcharge is a bargain. If he's Evan Engram 2.0 (disappointing athleticism that doesn't translate), you've burned a first-round pick *and* capped out on a non-contributor.

I'm skeptical of R1 TE value. The hit rate on first-round tight ends is poor: Kyle Pitts (2021, #4 overall) has been good but not elite. Dalton Kincaid (2023, #25) is solid but not a difference-maker. T.J. Hockenson (2019, #8) required a second contract to become a top-10 TE. Rookie tight ends rarely produce Year 1 Joker-level impact. 

**My call:** Draft CJ Allen (ILB, Georgia) at #30, take Jack Endries or Eli Stowers (TE) at #62. The cap savings ($1.3M/year) + positional value (ILB is a clearer need with Singleton at 32) outweigh the scheme upside of Sadiq.

### The 2027 Cap Landscape — McGlinchey Is the Real Decision

Denver's 2027 cap picture is clean *except for one landmine*: **Mike McGlinchey at $23.8M** (OTC 2027 projections).

McGlinchey is 30 years old, coming off a 2025 season where he was average-to-good at RT but not irreplaceable. His $23.8M cap hit is 7.3% of a projected $327M cap. For context, that's top-3 RT money in the NFL, and McGlinchey isn't a top-3 RT.

**McGlinchey decision tree (2027 offseason):**

| Option | Cap Savings | Dead Money | Replacement Cost | Verdict |
|--------|-------------|------------|------------------|---------|
| **Keep McGlinchey** | $0 | $0 | $0 | Rostered at $23.8M — only viable if he's still playing at Pro Bowl level |
| **Cut pre-June 1** | $16.8M | $7.0M | Need to sign/draft RT (~$12M AAV for veteran starter) | Saves ~$5M net after replacement |
| **Trade McGlinchey** | $23.8M cleared | $7.0M | Same as above | Best case: get a Day 3 pick, clear $16.8M, redeploy to TE market or Nix extension prep |

If Denver cuts Engram post-June 1 in 2026, they'll be carrying $5.2M in 2027 Engram dead money. Add $7.0M in McGlinchey dead money (if cut), and that's $12.2M in dead cap — but it clears $16.8M in usable space. Net gain: ~$4.6M.

Here's the cap play I'd make: **Trade McGlinchey in 2027, use the $16.8M savings to extend Nix early (summer 2027 instead of waiting until 2028)**. Early extensions lower total cost by 10–15% compared to waiting until the market resets. Nix at $50M AAV signed in 2027 is cheaper than Nix at $55M AAV signed in 2028 after another year of QB market inflation.

### Where I'll Disagree with the Panel

**DEN will argue Engram deserves one more year to prove he can fit the system.** I'll push back: the film shows scheme execution failure, not just talent mismatch. Engram ran the routes Payton asked him to run — he just couldn't get open or create separation after the catch. Age 31 isn't when tight ends suddenly become elite athletes. Cut him.

**Offense will argue Sadiq at #30 is the missing piece that transforms the offense.** I'll counter: even if Sadiq has Jimmy Graham's *athleticism*, he won't have Graham's *production* as a rookie. Graham caught 31 passes for 356 yards as a rookie (2010) — solid but not game-changing. He didn't break out until Year 2 (99/1,310/11 in 2011). Denver's championship window is 2026-2027. They need production *now*, not developmental upside.

### The Hardest Cap Constraint

**Bo Nix's extension timing is the constraint that determines every other roster decision.**

If Nix signs an extension after the 2027 season (typical 5th-year option timeline), his cap hit jumps from $5M (2027) to ~$35–40M (2028) in Year 1 of the extension. That's a **$30M cap swing** in one offseason.

Here's what Denver needs to do *before* Nix's extension hits:

1. **Extend Nix early** (summer 2027) to spread the cap impact across 2028-2032 instead of front-loading it
2. **Clear McGlinchey's $23.8M** (trade/cut in 2027)
3. **Extend or replace Patrick Surtain II** (entering final year of rookie deal in 2027; will command $22–24M AAV)
4. **Do not re-sign Courtland Sutton** after 2026 (he'll be 31, cap hit is $18.5M in 2026; let him walk)

If Denver executes all four moves, they can preserve ~$40–45M in effective 2028 cap space even with Nix's extension kicking in. That's enough to retain defensive core pieces and add a veteran TE in free agency (if the drafted TE hasn't developed).

But if they miss even one — if McGlinchey stays rostered at $23.8M, or if they extend Sutton for sentimental reasons — the cap crunch forces cuts elsewhere. Depth disappears. Veteran leadership walks. The championship window closes.

### My Core Recommendation

**Cut Engram post-June 1. Draft Allen at #30 and Endries at #62. Bank the $3.8M savings for in-season flexibility. Trade McGlinchey in 2027 and extend Nix early.**

This is cap management as chess, not checkers. Every move sets up the next. The Engram decision isn't about whether a 31-year-old TE can bounce back — it's about whether Denver preserves the cap flexibility to keep the roster competitive through Nix's extension. The answer is yes, but only if they make the hard cuts now.

The window is two years. The cap math says don't waste it on sunk costs.

## Panel Composition Rationale

**Why these 3 agents:**

1. **DEN (Team Expert)** — Required for all team-specific articles. Owns the 2025 season reality: Engram's on-field performance (50/461/1), the AFCCG offensive limitations (Stidham 133 passing yards), Nix's injury recovery (ankle fracture, expected back May OTAs), and the competing positional urgencies (ILB with Singleton at 32, OT with Bolles/McGlinchey both 30+).

2. **Cap (Salary Cap Expert)** — The Engram decision is fundamentally a cap puzzle. At $14.2M cap hit with only ~$3.8M savings if cut, there's no clean answer. Cap will model all three scenarios (keep, cut pre-June 1, cut post-June 1) and their downstream effects: rookie pool signing ($11.7M required), potential Nix extension timing, and the 2027 cap landscape where McGlinchey's $23.8M becomes a potential casualty. Cap also compares the cost structure of R1 TE (Sadiq ~$3M AAV) vs. R2 TE vs. veteran TE signing.

3. **Offense (Offensive Scheme Expert)** — This is the first time in Sean Payton's 20-year head coaching career that he won't call plays. Davis Webb takes over as playcaller — what does that mean for the Joker role? Offense will analyze whether Webb's background (QB coach → passing game coordinator) signals a different TE philosophy, whether the Joker concept is still schematically viable against modern defenses, and whether Kenyon Sadiq (Oregon TE, "freak athlete") is a realistic Jimmy Graham comp or wishful thinking.

**Coverage check:**
- ✅ Team/roster context (DEN)
- ✅ Cap engineering & financial scenarios (Cap)
- ✅ Scheme evolution & playcalling transition (Offense)
- ⚠️ Draft not included — Pick #30 dilemma (CJ Allen vs. Kenyon Sadiq) is adequately covered by DEN's positional urgency assessment and Offense's scheme-fit evaluation. A standalone Draft agent would duplicate that analysis.
- ⚠️ Defense not included — Defense is not the core tension. Defense was top-10 in 2025; the AFCCG loss (10-7) was an offensive failure. Defensive needs (ILB) are covered by DEN's roster evaluation.

**Expected disagreements:**
- **Cap vs. DEN on Engram:** Cap may argue "cut him, save $3.8M" while DEN may see value in keeping a veteran TE1 for one more year while developing a rookie
- **Offense vs. DEN on draft priority:** Offense may advocate for Sadiq at #30 (system fit), while DEN may prioritize CJ Allen (ILB, positional urgency)
- **Cap vs. Offense on R1 TE investment:** Cap may see R1 TE as a luxury Denver can't afford; Offense may see it as the piece that unlocks the championship ceiling

# Discussion Prompt: $99 Million Ghost — How Miami Rebuilds With the Largest Dead Cap Hit in NFL History

**Depth Level:** 2 — The Beat

## The Core Question

Can the Dolphins execute a competitive rebuild while absorbing $99.2 million in dead cap from the Tua Tagovailoa release — the largest single-player dead money charge in NFL history — or does this financial albatross condemn Miami to a multi-year talent drought that no amount of drafting can overcome?

## Key Tensions

- **One-year pain vs. two-year drag:** Miami structured Tua's release as a post-June 1 cut, spreading the hit ($55.4M in 2026, $43.8M in 2027). This eases 2026 but means the dead money haunts them through 2027. Is this the right approach, or should they have ripped the band-aid off in a single year like the Rams ($75M in 2023) and Broncos ($85.2M in 2024)?

- **Green Bay blueprint vs. Green Bay resources:** New HC Jeff Hafley and GM Jon-Eric Sullivan both came from Green Bay's developmental machine. But the Packers' model depends on sustained drafting with cap flexibility. Can you run that model when ~18% of your 2026 cap is dead money for a released QB?

- **Draft capital — enough or not?** Miami has 8 picks (including 3 third-rounders) but no extra first-rounders. Is that enough ammunition to stock a gutted roster, or do they need to trade down/accumulate more picks? The Texans took the cap pain AND had the #2 overall pick — Miami's draft position isn't nearly as favorable.

- **QB bridge or QB purgatory?** Malik Willis at $3.3M is a financial non-event, but is he a developmental option or just a warm body? With $55.4M in dead QB money, can Miami even afford to draft a QB early, or does every pick need to go toward roster building?

## The Paths

**Path 1 — The Rams Model (Scorched Earth Reset):** Accept 2026 as a lost year. Cut every expensive veteran. Stockpile picks. Tank for a top-5 2027 pick. Use the 2027 cap relief ($43.8M dead money clears after that) to make a big FA splash in 2028. Timeline: competitive by 2028.

**Path 2 — The Texans Model (Draft Your Way Out Fast):** Use the 8 picks aggressively — trade up if a franchise QB falls, or package thirds to move into the late first round again. Try to find a Stroud-level QB hit. Pair the young core (Waddle, Achane, Robinson) with high-upside rookies. Timeline: competitive by 2027.

**Path 3 — The Hybrid (Green Bay Slow Build):** Don't tank, don't splash. Let Willis start, evaluate him honestly. Use the draft to build defense (edge + CB early) and let the offense develop around Waddle and Achane. If Willis shows promise, you've found a cheap QB. If not, you're in position for a 2027 QB. Timeline: flexible, competitive by 2027-28.

**Path 4 — The Hail Mary (Trade for a Veteran QB):** Use cap gymnastics and draft capital to acquire a disgruntled veteran QB (hypothetical: trade up for a rookie or acquire a vet on a restructured deal). Go all-in on competing faster despite the dead money. Highest risk, highest reward. Timeline: competitive by 2026-27.

## Panel Instructions

### Cap — Salary Cap Expert
**Focus:** The financial mechanics of rebuilding under $99.2M in dead money. Model out Miami's actual available cap dollars in 2026 and 2027 after dead money. Compare to the Rams (2023), Broncos (2024), and Texans (2022) — how much effective spending power did each team have during their dead-cap year, and how quickly did they recover? Evaluate whether the post-June 1 split was the right financial move vs. eating it all in 2026. **Do not** evaluate player talent or draft prospects — stay in the cap lane.

### MIA — Miami Dolphins Expert
**Focus:** The roster and competitive reality. Who are the actual keepers on this roster? Is the Waddle/Achane/Robinson core enough to build around, or is Miami closer to a bare cupboard than it looks? Evaluate the Hafley/Sullivan hire — what does a "Green Bay pipeline" front office actually do differently in Year 1 of a rebuild? What's the realistic win projection for 2026? **Do not** do cap math — Cap handles that. Focus on football: scheme fit, player development potential, and organizational trajectory.

### Draft — Draft Expert
**Focus:** The draft strategy under constraints. With 8 picks including 3 thirds but no extra first, what's the optimal draft approach? Should Miami use the first-rounder on a QB (and if so, who's available at their range?), or go BPA on defense (edge/CB) and wait on QB? Evaluate the trade-up math: what would it cost to move from ~mid-first to top-10 for a QB? Is the 2026 class deep enough to hit on Day 2 talent at Miami's specific needs (EDGE, CB, WR2)? **Do not** evaluate cap implications of picks — stay in the scouting/strategy lane.

# Draft Position Statement — Miami's 2026 Draft Strategy

**By:** Draft Caldwell, Draft Expert

---

## Recommendation: BPA at #11. Do Not Trade Up for a QB.

The 2026 QB class kills trade-up logic before the math enters the conversation. Mendoza goes #1 to Las Vegas. After him, Ty Simpson and Garrett Nussmeier are mid-first talents at best — neither franchise-altering. Trading from #11 into the top-5 for Simpson would cost Miami's 2nd plus at least one 3rd (~800+ points on the Jimmy Johnson chart), paying a top-5 premium for a QB whose consensus grade is late-teens. That's how you turn a rebuild into a decade of irrelevance.

## What Pick #11 Actually Buys

At #11, Miami sits in a defensive talent sweet spot. Who likely falls:

- **Mansoor Delane (CB, LSU)** — Zero TDs allowed in 2025. Instant CB1 filling Miami's most exposed position. My top recommendation.
- **Keldric Faulk (EDGE, Auburn)** — High-ceiling rusher opposite Chop Robinson. Legitimate edge duo on rookie deals.
- **Sonny Styles (LB, Ohio State)** — Sub-4.5 at 230+, historic combine. Versatile, but LB isn't a premium need.

Delane is the pick. CB is a premium position, Miami's secondary is gutted, and his coverage profile translates immediately. Faulk is the pivot if Delane goes top-10.

## The Three-Third-Round Advantage

Three 3rds (own + HOU + PHI/Phillips trade) in a defense-heavy class is the real story. Historical hit rate for Round 3: ~38% become multi-year starters. Three swings gives Miami a ~76% chance of landing at least one.

Day 2 targets by need:

- **EDGE:** T.J. Parker (Clemson) and Cashius Howell (Texas A&M) — R2-R3 range with legitimate pass-rush upside. Parker at the top of Round 3 pairs perfectly with a Delane-at-#11 scenario.
- **CB:** Avieon Terrell (Clemson) and Colton Hood (Tennessee) — starting-caliber corners. Miami can't enter 2026 with one rookie CB and nothing behind him.
- **WR2:** KC Concepcion (Texas A&M) and Denzel Boston (Washington) — sliding into R2-R3. Either complements Waddle's speed with size and contested-catch ability.

The 2nd-round pick should be BPA from EDGE/CB/WR — whichever premium position #11 didn't address.

## The Non-Obvious Insight

Everyone will focus on the 2026 QB question. The real calculus: **Miami's three 3rds are more valuable as 2027 trade-up currency than as 2026 picks.**

Package two 3rds with a future 1st and Miami can move into the top-10 of the 2027 draft — a class projected to include Arch Manning, Nico Iamaleava, and LaNorris Sellers. Three legitimate franchise QB prospects versus zero in 2026 post-Mendoza. Willis starts in 2026, Miami stocks defensive talent on cheap contracts, and unused picks become chips for the real QB move next April.

**Bottom line:** Path 3 (Hybrid/Green Bay model) with a 2027 QB trade-up trigger. Build the defense now, buy the quarterback later — in a class that actually has one worth buying.

# MIA Position — Tua Dead Cap Rebuild

**Agent:** MIA (Miami Dolphins Expert)
**Focus:** Roster audit, coaching regime evaluation, competitive trajectory

## The Roster Truth: Thinner Than It Looks

Strip away the names and count genuine building blocks — players you'd protect in an expansion draft. I get to five: **Achane, Waddle, Chop Robinson, Kenneth Grant, Jonah Savaiinaea.** That's it. Everyone else is either aging out of the timeline (Sieler turns 30 this year, Brooks is on a cuttable deal), a bridge piece (Willis, Uche, Brewer), or completely unproven (every single defensive back on this roster).

The secondary is the real crisis, not the dead cap. Miami lost Ramsey, Fitzpatrick, and Kohou in one offseason and replaced them with fifth-round picks and street free agents. Julius Brents, Jason Marshall Jr., Omar Brown, Dante Trader Jr. — not one has started a full NFL season. Hafley wants to run aggressive man coverage. He has no one to run it with. That's a scheme-talent mismatch that will get exposed weekly.

The offensive cupboard is deceptively bare too. Waddle is a genuine WR1, but behind him it's Malik Washington, Tutu Atwell, and Jalen Tolbert — a WR room that wouldn't start on most practice squads. Achane is electric but touches-dependent; you can't run him 25 times a game without breaking him. The offensive line has two promising young pieces in Patrick Paul and Savaiinaea, but the right side (Kion Smith at RG, inconsistent Austin Jackson at RT) will hemorrhage pressure.

## The Hafley/Sullivan Hire: What "Green Bay Way" Actually Means

The hire makes more sense than the national media gives it credit for. Green Bay's organizational philosophy isn't about any single scheme — it's about **positional value discipline and developmental patience.** The Packers consistently hit on Day 2 picks, develop players within their system for 2-3 years before expecting impact, and avoid the splashy free-agent overpay that created Miami's current mess.

Sullivan's first moves track with this: one-year prove-it deals across the roster, no panic spending, Malik Willis as a bridge with a clean two-year out. Bobby Slowik as OC is the smartest hire — his work developing C.J. Stroud in Houston shows he can maximize a limited QB. That matters more than any scheme label.

The risk: Hafley has never been a head coach. Coordinating a full rebuild while installing a new defense while managing a locker room full of players who know they're placeholders — that's a multi-variable challenge that breaks a lot of first-time HCs.

## 2026 Win Projection: 4-13

Willis behind a half-built offensive line, throwing to Waddle and no one else, with a secondary full of developmental players playing man coverage. The defense will be competitive at the line of scrimmage (Robinson, Grant, Sieler) and porous everywhere behind it. I see wins against bad teams at home, competitive losses in division games, and a handful of blowouts where the secondary gets torched.

**4-13 puts Miami in the top 3-5 pick range for 2027** — which is exactly where they need to be if Willis doesn't emerge. This is Path 3: the Green Bay Slow Build. Let Willis start, evaluate honestly, build the defense through the draft, and use that 2027 cap explosion to add proven starters at corner and safety.

## The Non-Obvious Insight

**Watch Chop Robinson's Year 2 leap — it determines whether this is a two-year rebuild or a four-year one.** If Robinson goes from promising pass rusher to 10+ sack EDGE1, Miami has a defensive cornerstone to pair with Grant, and the 2027 free agency class only needs to fill complementary pieces. If he plateaus, Miami is looking at needing premium talent at both EDGE and secondary — and no amount of cap space fills two premium positions in one offseason. Robinson's development is the single highest-leverage variable in this rebuild, and it has nothing to do with the cap sheet.

## Areas of Agreement

**1. Path 3 is unanimous.** All three panelists independently recommend the Green Bay Slow Build. No panelist advocated for the Rams-style scorched earth (Path 1), the Texans-style aggressive trade-up (Path 2), or the Hail Mary veteran QB acquisition (Path 4). This is rare panel consensus — the article's framing should reflect it.

**2. The $99.2M number is a headline, not a death sentence.** Cap proved the proportional burden (18.4% and 13.8%) is half what LAR and DEN absorbed, and both were competitive within two years. MIA and Draft don't contest this math. The article's job is to de-sensationalize the number without dismissing the real pain.

**3. 2026 is a developmental year — don't fight it.** Cap says ~$8-12M usable space (coffee money). MIA projects 4-13. Draft says skip the barren 2026 QB class entirely. All three treat 2026 as a year to evaluate, develop, and accumulate — not compete.

**4. The 2027 QB class is the target.** Cap notes that if Willis doesn't flash, Miami enters 2027 with $65M+ and a top pick. Draft explicitly frames the three 3rds as 2027 trade-up currency for Manning/Iamaleava/Sellers. MIA's 4-13 projection puts Miami in top 3-5 pick range — exactly where a 2027 QB play needs them.

## Key Tensions

### Tension 1: How Fast Is "Competitive by 2027"?

Cap's financial model says Miami re-enters the market as a real buyer in 2027 ($65-85M usable). But MIA's roster audit says only 5 genuine building blocks exist, the secondary has zero proven starters, and the WR room behind Waddle "wouldn't start on most practice squads." Cap sees a two-year tax; MIA sees a roster that needs 8-10 starter-caliber additions regardless of cap space. The money recovers on schedule. The roster may not.

**Lead's read:** Cap is right on the math, MIA is right on the talent gap. Both are true simultaneously. Miami will have buying power in 2027 but will need to deploy it across too many holes for a single offseason to fix. Realistic competitive window: late 2027 at the earliest, more likely 2028.

### Tension 2: Hold Picks or Acquire More?

Draft wants to hold the three 3rds as 2027 trade-up currency — package them with a future 1st to move into the top-10 for a franchise QB. Cap wants to *acquire* additional picks by weaponizing dead cap space as a salary-dump trade market — absorbing expiring contracts that cost nothing against already-burned space. These aren't contradictory, but they compete for strategic emphasis: does Miami hoard or harvest?

**Lead's read:** Do both. Use dead cap absorption to acquire mid-round picks (2027 4ths-6ths) during the 2026 season, while preserving the three 3rds as high-value trade-up chips. Cap's salary-dump strategy is the non-obvious play the article should feature — it turns a financial constraint into a market-making tool.

### Tension 3: The Chop Robinson Variable

MIA flags Robinson's Year 2 leap as "the single highest-leverage variable in this rebuild" — 10+ sacks means a two-year rebuild, a plateau means four years. Draft implicitly agrees by targeting EDGE depth on Day 2 (Parker at Clemson). But neither Cap nor Draft weighs in on what happens if Robinson doesn't develop. The rebuild timeline has a hidden dependency on one player's development arc that the cap math doesn't capture.

**Lead's read:** MIA is right to flag this, and the article should frame Robinson's development as the swing variable. If Robinson emerges + Willis is viable = 2027 contention. If either fails = 2028-29 at best.

## Recommended Path

**Lead recommends Path 3 (Green Bay Slow Build) with two specific structural additions:**

1. **Accept 2026 as a development year.** Willis starts. The record doesn't matter. The evaluation does.

2. **Draft Mansoor Delane (CB, LSU) at #11.** Secondary is the unanimous crisis point. Delane is the highest-impact selection — instant CB1 filling the most exposed position on the roster.

3. **Use Day 2 picks on EDGE + CB + WR depth.** Draft's Day 2 board (Parker, Terrell, Concepcion) is the right target list. Use the 2nd on BPA from EDGE/CB/WR. Use one 3rd on the best remaining need. **Hold the other two 3rds as 2027 trade-up currency.**

4. **Activate the salary-dump trade market.** This is Cap's best insight and the article's non-obvious angle. During the 2026 season, absorb expiring contracts from cap-strapped contenders in exchange for draft picks. Dead space is already burned — additional cap hits are free. Target: 2-3 acquired picks by the 2026 trade deadline.

5. **Set the 2027 QB trigger.** If Willis shows franchise traits by Week 12 of 2026, extend the evaluation into 2027. If not, deploy the accumulated draft capital (top-5 own pick + preserved 3rds + acquired picks) to trade up for a franchise QB in the Manning/Iamaleava/Sellers class.

6. **2027 free agency: secondary and WR2.** The $65-85M in usable space goes to proven starters at CB2, safety, and WR2 — the positions where developmental drafting alone won't close the gap fast enough.

**Timeline:** 4-13 in 2026. 7-10 to 9-8 in 2027 depending on QB resolution. Genuine contention in 2028.

## Open Questions for the Writer

1. **Verify Malik Willis's 2025 performance data.** What were his actual stats in Green Bay? Completion %, TD:INT, PFF grade. MIA treats him as a developmental bridge — does his tape support that, or is he a known quantity (bad)?

2. **Quantify the salary-dump trade market.** Cap's insight is the article's differentiator, but needs concrete examples. Which contending teams will be cap-strapped mid-2026? What kind of contracts would they want to offload? Historical precedent for teams absorbing dead cap in exchange for picks?

3. **Pin down Miami's exact draft position.** The prompt says "mid-to-late first" but MIA projects 4-13 (top 3-5 pick). If Miami is picking top-5 vs. #11, Draft's entire board changes. Clarify: is #11 based on the 2025 season result, or is this 2026 draft position? Resolve the discrepancy.

4. **Research Chop Robinson's Year 1 production baseline.** MIA says his Year 2 leap is the highest-leverage variable. What did Year 1 actually look like — sacks, pressures, win rate? What's the historical hit rate for EDGE rushers who showed similar Year 1 numbers and then broke out?

5. **Check the 2027 QB class projections.** Draft names Manning, Iamaleava, and Sellers as franchise prospects. Are all three currently projected as first-round QBs? Any injury or eligibility concerns? The "hold picks for 2027" strategy collapses if that class doesn't deliver.

6. **Clarify Hafley's defensive scheme specifics.** MIA says "aggressive man coverage" with no one to run it. What scheme did Hafley actually run at Boston College and as a coordinator? Is the scheme-talent mismatch confirmed, or is Hafley likely to adapt to his personnel?

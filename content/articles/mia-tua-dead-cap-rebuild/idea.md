# Article Idea: $99 Million Ghost — How Miami Rebuilds With the Largest Dead Cap Hit in NFL History

## Working Title
$99 Million Ghost — How Miami Rebuilds With the Largest Dead Cap Hit in NFL History

## Angle / Tension
The Dolphins cut Tua Tagovailoa and triggered the largest single-player dead cap charge in NFL history: $99.2 million spread across 2026 ($55.4M) and 2027 ($43.8M). They also released Tyreek Hill, cut Bradley Chubb, and traded Minkah Fitzpatrick. New HC Jeff Hafley and GM Jon-Eric Sullivan (both from Green Bay) inherited a roster stripped to the studs and a cap sheet bleeding dead money.

The tension: Can Hafley and Sullivan execute a Green Bay-style rebuild — draft, develop, let expensive veterans walk — when they're starting $30M+ over the cap with almost $100M committed to players who aren't on the roster? What does the 2-year blueprint look like when Year 1 is essentially a financial write-off? Is Jaylen Waddle the only keeper, and does Malik Willis ($3.3M bridge) have any real path to being more than a placeholder?

This is the most dramatic financial reset any NFL team has attempted. The article examines whether the blueprint can work under these constraints.

# Cap Position — The $99.2M Dead Money Mechanics

## The Real Cap Math

**2026: ~$8–12M usable space.** Start $30M over the $301M cap. Hill release (+$22.8M), Chubb June 1 cut (+$15M), and minor restructures get Miami to ~$13–16M under. Subtract the rookie pool for 8 picks (~$5–7M). What's left is coffee money — a minimum-spend year.

**2027: ~$65–85M usable space.** Cap rises to ~$317M. The second $43.8M Tua tranche hits, but expensive veteran deals have cycled off. The young core (Waddle, Achane, Robinson, Paul) sits on rookie or early-extension deals. Miami re-enters the market as a real buyer.

## The Ratio That Matters

Everyone fixates on the $99.2M total. The metric that actually predicts recovery speed is **dead money as a percentage of the cap**:

| Team | Year | Dead Cap | Cap | Dead % | Recovery |
|------|------|----------|-----|--------|----------|
| LAR (Goff) | 2023 | $75M | $224M | **33.5%** | Competitive Yr 2 |
| DEN (Wilson) | 2024 | $85.2M | $255M | **33.4%** | Competitive Yr 2 |
| **MIA (Tua Y1)** | **2026** | **$55.4M** | **$301M** | **18.4%** | **—** |
| **MIA (Tua Y2)** | **2027** | **$43.8M** | **~$317M** | **13.8%** | **—** |
| HOU (Watson) | 2022 | ~$40M | $208M | **19.2%** | Competitive Yr 2 |

Miami's 2026 hit is *half* the proportional burden the Rams and Broncos absorbed — and both were competitive within two years. The $99.2M is a nominal record because the cap itself has grown 35% since the Goff trade, not because the structural damage is worse.

## Post-June 1: Correct Call

Eating $99.2M in one year would've consumed 33% of 2026 cap — Denver's worst case, on a thinner roster. The split drops each year to survivable levels (18.4%, 13.8%) and avoids forced cuts that would destroy trade value on movable assets. It also preserves optionality: if Willis flashes in 2026, Miami enters 2027 FA with $65M+ and a cheap QB — the exact structure Houston rode with Stroud.

## The Insight Everyone Will Miss

Miami's dead money creates a **cap-dump trade market**. With 2026 space already burned, absorbing expiring veteran contracts costs nothing real — but it can yield draft picks. Teams needing mid-season salary relief route expensive deals through Miami; the Dolphins eat cap hits that are meaningless against already-dead space, and collect future assets. The 8-pick 2026 class plus 2–3 acquired picks could give Miami 10+ selections entering 2027 — converting a financial constraint into a market-making tool.

**Bottom line:** This is a two-year tax, not a five-year sentence. At 18.4% and 13.8% of cap, both years are lighter proportional hits than what LA and Denver survived. Miami's math supports competitiveness by 2027. The constraint isn't dollars — it's quarterback.

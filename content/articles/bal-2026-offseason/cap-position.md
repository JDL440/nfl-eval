# Cap Position: The Crosby Trade Math

**Core Recommendation:** Baltimore lost both Tyler Linderbaum ($27M AAV to LV) AND Isaiah Likely ($13.3M AAV to NYG) in free agency. The Crosby trade ($30.7M cap hit in 2026) left the Ravens with insufficient cap space to retain either player without devastating restructures that mortgage 2027-2028. The hardest cap constraint is the **Lamar Jackson $74.5M balloon payment in 2027** — every restructure to afford Crosby in 2026 accelerates prorated money into 2027, creating a cap disaster.

## The Actual Outcome: Scenario 3 — Lost Both

Reality delivered the worst-case scenario. Baltimore's 2026 cap sheet shows **$47.2M in effective space** (6th in NFL), but that's AFTER losing Linderbaum and Likely. Here's what happened:

| Player | Outcome | AAV | Team |
|--------|---------|-----|------|
| Tyler Linderbaum | Lost to FA | $27.0M | Las Vegas Raiders |
| Isaiah Likely | Lost to FA | $13.3M | New York Giants |
| Maxx Crosby | Acquired via trade | $30.7M | Baltimore Ravens (2026 cap hit) |

**Cap math:** Crosby's $30.7M hit consumed 65% of Baltimore's pre-trade effective space (~$49M). Retaining both Linderbaum ($27M) and Likely ($13.3M) would have required $71M in total cap commitments — $22M more than available. The Ravens chose Crosby over their offensive infrastructure.

## The Three-Scenario Breakdown

### Scenario 1: Retain Both Linderbaum + Likely (Impossible)

**Required cap space:** $71M (Crosby $30.7M + Linderbaum $27M + Likely $13.3M)  
**Available space:** $49M effective (before any moves)  
**Shortfall:** $22M

To create $22M in additional space, Baltimore would need to:

1. **Restructure Lamar Jackson:** Convert $18M of his $51.25M 2027 base salary to signing bonus → saves $14.4M in 2026, adds $14.4M dead money to 2027-2030
2. **Restructure Roquan Smith:** Convert $12M of base to bonus → saves $9.6M in 2026, adds $9.6M to 2027-2029
3. **Total savings:** $24M created

**The problem:** Jackson's 2027 cap hit is already $84.5M. Adding $14.4M in accelerated proration pushes it to **$98.9M** — 30% of the entire 2027 salary cap ($327M projected). Baltimore would be functionally unable to field a competitive roster in 2027.

**Verdict:** Mathematically possible but strategically suicidal. This scenario destroys 2027 cap flexibility entirely.

---

### Scenario 2: Retain Linderbaum Only (Difficult But Viable)

**Required cap space:** $57.7M (Crosby $30.7M + Linderbaum $27M)  
**Available space:** $49M  
**Shortfall:** $8.7M

To create $8.7M:

1. **Restructure Marlon Humphrey:** Convert $10M of his $15.25M base → saves $8M in 2026
2. **Cut Broderick Washington (DL):** Saves $4.01M with zero dead money
3. **Total savings:** $12M created

**2026 roster impact:**
- Lost: Isaiah Likely (TE2), Broderick Washington (rotational DL)
- Gained: Maxx Crosby (elite EDGE)
- Retained: Tyler Linderbaum (All-Pro center)

**2027 cap hit:** Adds $8M in dead money from Humphrey restructure. Combined with existing commitments, 2027 top-51 projects to $316M — leaving only $11M in space.

**The constraint:** Baltimore would have almost zero cap flexibility in 2027. Any injury requiring a midseason signing, any position battle lost in camp, any veteran cut elsewhere they want to claim — the answer is "no money."

**Verdict:** This was the BAL position's recommended path. It kept the offensive line intact (critical for Lamar) while accepting the loss of TE depth. The Ravens chose NOT to do this, suggesting they valued 2027 flexibility over 2026 Linderbaum retention.

---

### Scenario 3: Retain Neither (What Actually Happened)

**Required cap space:** $30.7M (Crosby only)  
**Available space:** $49M  
**Surplus:** $18.3M

Baltimore's actual 2026 moves:

- **Signed Trey Hendrickson** (EDGE, 4yr/$112M, $28M AAV) — partnered with Crosby to create an elite edge duo
- **Re-signed John Simpson** (G, 3yr/$34.5M, $11.5M AAV) — replaced Linderbaum's interior line leadership
- **Added Durham Smythe** (TE, 2yr/$6M, $3M AAV) — bargain-bin TE2 replacement for Likely

**Cap space after all moves:** $47.2M (ranked 6th in NFL)

**2027 outlook:** Clean. No major restructures = no accelerated dead money. Baltimore's 2027 cap projects to $11.3M in space with the existing roster, which is tight but manageable.

**The tradeoff:**
- **Lost:** NFL's best center (Linderbaum) + emerging TE2 (Likely)
- **Gained:** Two elite edge rushers (Crosby + Hendrickson) = projected 55+ sacks vs. 30 in 2025
- **Offensive risk:** Corey Bullock (25, 1 year starting experience) now starts at center; Mark Andrews (30) has no viable TE2 support

## The Hardest Cap Constraint: Lamar's 2027 Balloon

The single biggest factor limiting Baltimore's options was **Lamar Jackson's $84.5M cap hit in 2027**. Here's why this matters:

| Year | Lamar Cap Hit | % of Salary Cap |
|------|---------------|-----------------|
| 2026 | $34.5M | 11.5% |
| 2027 | $84.5M | 25.9% |
| 2028 | $68.0M | 20.2% |

Every restructure to create 2026 space — whether for Linderbaum, Likely, or anyone else — adds prorated bonus money to 2027-2029. With Lamar already consuming 26% of the 2027 cap, adding even $10M in restructure debt pushes total cap commitments above $320M, leaving less than $7M for the other 52 roster spots.

**This is why Baltimore chose Scenario 3.** Keeping 2027 clean was more important than retaining Linderbaum in 2026. The Ravens are betting that:

1. A dominant pass rush (Crosby + Hendrickson + Madubuike) can mask offensive line regression
2. Lamar's mobility reduces the need for All-Pro center play
3. 2027 cap flexibility allows them to re-tool after the Crosby/Hendrickson gamble plays out

## Two-Year Cap Consequences

| Category | 2026 | 2027 |
|----------|------|------|
| Top 51 Cap Commitments | $269.3M | $315.7M |
| Projected Salary Cap | $301.2M | $327.0M |
| Effective Cap Space | $47.2M | $11.3M |
| Dead Money | $18.2M | TBD (~$25M estimated) |
| Roster Flexibility | High | Low |

**2026 assessment:** Baltimore has the cap space to be aggressive in-season (injuries, midseason trades, waiver claims). The Crosby trade didn't cripple them short-term.

**2027 assessment:** Baltimore will be functionally capped out. The Lamar balloon + Crosby + Hendrickson + Roquan + Madubuike = ~$200M in five players. The remaining 48 roster spots will need to be filled with rookie contracts, vet minimums, and restructure candidates.

**The window:** 2026 is the year. If Crosby/Hendrickson don't deliver a playoff run, 2027 offers almost no financial flexibility to pivot.

## My Verdict on the Trade

The Crosby trade was **cap-defensible but roster-construction risky**.

**What worked:**
- Avoided mortgaging 2027 with destructive restructures
- Created an elite defensive front (Crosby/Hendrickson/Madubuike) that addresses the 30-sack disaster
- Maintained flexibility to make in-season moves if the playoff window opens

**What failed:**
- Lost the NFL's best center (Linderbaum) in his prime, directly weakening Lamar's offensive ecosystem
- Lost a breakout TE2 (Likely), reducing 12-personnel effectiveness
- Replaced both with unproven/aging options (Bullock at C, Smythe at TE2)

**The math says:** This trade works if Baltimore wins 11+ games in 2026 and makes a deep playoff run. It fails if Crosby/Hendrickson don't produce, Lamar regresses without Linderbaum's protection, or the 2027 cap crunch forces painful cuts.

The hardest cap constraint isn't Crosby's $30.7M hit — it's the **zero margin for error in 2027** created by punting on Linderbaum/Likely retention. Baltimore bet the next two years on pass rush over offensive infrastructure. We'll know by January 2027 if they were right.

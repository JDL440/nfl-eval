# Discussion Prompt: The Ravens Traded Their Draft Future for Maxx Crosby. Here's Whether the Math Works.

**Depth Level:** 2 — The Beat

## Central Question
Did Baltimore make the right call trading both their 2026 and 2027 first-round picks for Maxx Crosby, and can they build a contender around this core without draft replenishment for two years?

## The Tension
The Ravens just executed the most un-Ravens trade in franchise history. For 30 years, Baltimore's identity was built on drafting elite defenders — Ed Reed, Ray Lewis, Haloti Ngata, Marlon Humphrey, Kyle Hamilton. Now they've traded away two consecutive first-round picks for a 28-year-old edge rusher with a $106.5M contract, betting that a win-now pivot under a brand-new head coach is worth abandoning the pipeline that built the franchise.

The "yes" side: Baltimore's 2025 pass rush was historically bad (30 sacks, no one above 4.5). Crosby had 69.5 career sacks and is one of the NFL's three best edge rushers. Paired with Justin Madubuike inside, the defensive front goes from liability to strength overnight. Lamar Jackson is 29, Kyle Hamilton is entering his prime, Roquan Smith is locked up. The window is NOW.

The "no" side: A ~$30-35M cap hit on a roster with only $10-22M in effective space means painful decisions are coming. Tyler Linderbaum (the NFL's best center) and Isaiah Likely (breakout TE) are both free agents — can Baltimore retain them AND absorb Crosby? Two drafts without a first-round pick means no cheap rookie contracts to backfill, no trade-up ammunition, and no margin for error. If Minter's first year stumbles, there's no reset button.

## What Would Make This Worth Reading
The Crosby trade is the offseason's loudest headline, but nobody has done the actual math: What does Baltimore's cap look like after Crosby, after retaining Linderbaum, after losing Likely? What does the roster depth chart look like without first-round picks in 2026 AND 2027? This article puts real numbers behind the take — not "great trade" or "bad trade," but "here's what has to happen for this trade to work."

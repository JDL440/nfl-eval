## Angle / Tension
Baltimore just made the biggest trade in franchise history — sending both their 2026 (#14) and 2027 first-round picks to Las Vegas for five-time Pro Bowl edge rusher Maxx Crosby. It's a stunning departure from the draft-and-develop identity that defined the Ozzie Newsome and early DeCosta eras. The move comes after firing John Harbaugh following a playoff miss and installing Jesse Minter as the new head coach.

**The core tension:** The Ravens' pass rush was the worst in the NFL in 2025 — only 30 sacks total, no defender above 4.5. Crosby instantly fixes that. But his $30-35M cap hit lands on a roster that already faces $10-22M in effective cap space, with Tyler Linderbaum (C) and Isaiah Likely (TE) hitting free agency. Baltimore now has ZERO first-round picks for two consecutive drafts. The Crosby bet only works if the window is open NOW — and if Lamar Jackson, Kyle Hamilton, and Roquan Smith are enough foundational talent to complement an elite edge rusher without draft replenishment.

Is this the move that vaults Baltimore back into Super Bowl contention, or the trade that future GMs point to as the moment the Ravens mortgaged their future?

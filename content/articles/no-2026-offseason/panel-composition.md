## Rationale

### Why NO
Team agent is non-negotiable for any team-specific article. NO owns the Saints' 2025 season context (Shough's 5-3 finish, the Rattler experiment, Kamara's usage, the defensive decline after losing Davis/Taylor), the depth chart evaluation, the locker room temperature around Shough's franchise QB trajectory, and the Kellen Moore Year 2 scheme evolution. NO also provides the critical narrative bridge between "what the roster looks like on paper" and "what the team can actually do on Sundays."

This article hinges on whether the 2026 moves are building blocks or stopgaps. NO is the only agent who can credibly separate the two.

### Why Cap
The entire article premise is the **$114M dead money constraint vs. the $116M 2027 windfall**. Cap models the bridge year mechanics: what exactly rolls off in 2027, how the Olave extension structure impacts both years, how the #8 pick's rookie deal fits into the 2026 cap puzzle, and whether the Saints' dead-money-to-contention trajectory mirrors historical comps (Eagles 2021→2022 is the closest parallel).

Cap also brings the tension: Is the front office being strategic (delaying big spending until 2027), or are they just surviving (patching holes with affordable veterans like Elliss/Fant without a coherent plan)?

### Why Offense
Kellen Moore's Year 2 is the wildcard. Shough's 67.6% completion rate and 5-3 record in Year 1 gives the offense a foundation, but the scheme fit around his skillset (intermediate accuracy, mobility, limited deep-ball history) determines whether the Saints can compete offensively in 2026 with a WR room of Olave + Polk + Vele + a potential #8 draft pick.

Offense evaluates whether the #8 pick **must** be WR (to give Shough a true WR2 opposite Olave) or whether the scheme can function with the current weapons. This is the lynchpin decision for the entire offseason — and it requires offensive scheme expertise, not just roster analysis.

Offense also brings the Shough development curve: what does his ceiling look like with proper weapons, and is the 2026 supporting cast enough to keep him on the upward trajectory?

### Why NOT Defense
The Saints' defensive issues (CB2 gap after losing Taylor, EDGE depth after Jordan's likely exit) are real, but they're **symptoms of the cap problem**, not the core tension of the article. Defense would tell us "the CB2 spot is a hole" — which NO and the discussion prompt already establish. Defense doesn't add a distinct angle to the bridge year question.

If we need defensive context (e.g., evaluating whether the #8 pick should be CB vs. WR), it can be handled by NO (who knows the depth chart) or by Draft (if we activate the optional 4th panelist).

### Why NOT Draft (as core panel)
Draft is valuable for the #8 pick decision (WR vs. CB vs. EDGE, Carnell Tate evaluation, BPA vs. need), but **the article's core tension is the bridge year blueprint, not the draft pick in isolation**. Draft becomes essential if the article's central question shifts to "who should the Saints take at #8?" — but right now, the #8 pick is one piece of a larger strategic puzzle.

Draft is **optional 4th** because:
1. NO can provide basic draft need context (WR2, CB2, EDGE depth)
2. Cap can model the #8 pick's rookie contract
3. Offense can evaluate WR fit from a schematic perspective
4. If the draft pick analysis becomes critical during panel discussion, we can activate Draft mid-stream

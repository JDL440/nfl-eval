## The Trade Package: Four Picks for One Player

Let's start with what Jacksonville actually gave up to acquire Travis Hunter from Cleveland in the 2025 draft:

**Outbound to CLE:**
- 2025 2nd-round pick (#41 overall) → became WR Emeka Egbuka (OSU)
- 2025 4th-round pick (#104 overall) → became RB Bhayshul Tuten (WSU)
- 2026 1st-round pick (#19 overall, based on JAX's 13-4 finish) → still Cleveland's asset
- 2027 3rd-round pick (projected #80–90 range)

**Inbound from CLE:**
- 2025 1st-round pick (#5 overall) → Travis Hunter
- 2025 7th-round pick (#238 overall) → became P Logan Ward (unused)

**Net cost:** Three premium picks (a 1st, a 2nd, a 3rd) plus a 4th-rounder for Hunter. That's roughly **1,800 draft capital points** on the Jimmy Johnson trade value chart — the equivalent of two first-round picks.

**Hunter's contract (rookie deal):**
- 4 years, $35.1M total value
- $22.6M signing bonus (fully guaranteed)
- 2025 cap hit: $6.1M
- 2026 cap hit: $7.8M
- 2027 cap hit: $9.5M
- 2028 cap hit: $11.7M

Here's the critical detail: Hunter's cap hit is **locked in and non-restructurable** until 2027. Rookie contracts don't allow renegotiation before Year 3. Jacksonville is paying top-5 pick money for a player who — due to the two-way role and injury risk — may never play a full complement of snaps at either position.

Jacksonville's current cap situation (as of March 16, 2026):

| Category | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| **2026 Salary Cap** | $273.0M |

| **Current cap space** | **-$8.3M** (over the cap) |

**The hardest cap constraints:**

1. **Trevor Lawrence:** $24.0M cap hit in 2026 (Year 4 of rookie extension). This jumps to $35M in 2027 and $47M in 2028. The window to build around Lawrence at a discount closes this year.

2. **Christian Kirk:** $22.5M cap hit, $24M dead cap if cut. He's untouchable in 2026 despite producing just 68 rec / 784 yards in 2025. This is a **sunk cost** — the Hunter trade didn't create it, but it limits the flexibility to fix it.

3. **Foyesade Oluokun:** $17.1M cap hit (LB). Solid player, not elite. This contract is the definition of "market value" — pays MLB1 money for MLB1 production, but leaves no room for error elsewhere.

4. **Cam Robinson:** $15.8M cap hit (LT). Average tackle making above-average money. He's a cap cut candidate in 2027 ($9M savings), but cutting him in 2026 only saves $3.2M while creating a hole at LT.

**The roster holes that need filling:**

- **RB:** Travis Etienne left in free agency (signed 3yr/$36M with LAC). Replacement needed.
- **LB:** Devin Lloyd traded to NYJ for a 2027 5th-rounder. Need a WILL linebacker.
- **CB depth:** Behind Hunter, Jacksonville has Christian Braswell, Jarrian Jones, and Montaric Brown — all young, all unproven. Zero proven CB2.
- **EDGE depth:** Josh Hines-Allen ($23.9M cap hit) is the anchor, but Travon Walker ($8.1M) and Yasir Abdullah ($3.2M) are the entire rotation. One injury creates a crisis.

## The Missing 1st-Round Pick: What $19 Overall Would Have Bought

Jacksonville's 2026 1st-round pick (#19 overall) went to Cleveland in the Hunter trade. Here's what they **would have drafted** at that slot based on team needs and prospect availability:

### Scenario A: RB — Ashton Jeanty (Boise State)
- **Profile:** 6'0", 215 lbs, 4.42 forty, 2,601 rushing yards in 2025 (Heisman winner)
- **Rookie contract cost:** 4yr/$16.2M, $9.8M signing bonus, $4.05M AAV
- **2026 cap hit:** ~$3.5M
- **What JAX loses:** An immediate Etienne replacement who could handle 250+ carries and 40+ catches. Jeanty is a three-down workhorse. Instead, Jacksonville's RB1 is **Tank Bigsby** (2024 3rd-rounder, 412 career rushing yards in two seasons). The downgrade from Jeanty to Bigsby is the difference between a playoff team and a pretender.

### Scenario B: CB — Chandler Rivers (Duke)
- **Profile:** 6'1", 192 lbs, 4.41 forty, 12 PD + 3 INT in 2025
- **Rookie contract cost:** 4yr/$15.8M, $9.4M signing bonus, $3.95M AAV
- **2026 cap hit:** ~$3.4M
- **What JAX loses:** A plug-and-play CB2 who can handle outside assignments when Hunter is on offense. Rivers projects as a press-man corner with sideline-to-sideline range. Without him, Jacksonville is gambling that Christian Braswell (2024 6th-rounder, 23 career snaps) can start opposite Hunter. That's not a depth chart — it's a prayer.

### Scenario C: LB — Jalon Walker (Georgia)
- **Profile:** 6'2", 245 lbs, 4.51 forty, 104 tackles + 8.5 TFL in 2025
- **Rookie contract cost:** 4yr/$15.9M, $9.5M signing bonus, $3.98M AAV
- **2026 cap hit:** ~$3.4M
- **What JAX loses:** A three-down WILL linebacker who can cover TEs and running backs in space. Lloyd's departure created a hole; Walker would have filled it immediately. Instead, Jacksonville's depth chart shows **Ventrell Miller** (2024 UDFA) as the starting WILL. He played 142 snaps in 2025 (mostly special teams). The Titans, Colts, and Texans all have elite pass-catching TEs or RBs (Njoku, Pittman Jr., Mixon). Miller can't cover any of them.

**The cap math:** Any of these three prospects would have cost ~$3.4–3.6M in 2026 cap space. Instead, Jacksonville has **zero production** from that roster slot and is paying Hunter $7.8M to play part-time at both WR and CB. The net delta is **~$4M in wasted cap space** plus the opportunity cost of not having a starter-caliber player at RB, CB2, or WILL.

## The Restructure Menu: How JAX Gets Under the Cap

To get from **-$8.3M over the cap** to **+$15M in working space** (enough to sign draft picks and carry in-season flexibility), Jacksonville needs to create ~$23M in cap relief. Here are the five moves they can make:

### Move 1: Christian Kirk Extension/Restructure (saves $12M)
- **Current:** $22.5M cap hit, $24M dead cap if cut
- **Restructure:** Convert $15M of base salary to signing bonus, spread over 3 years
- **2026 cap hit after restructure:** $10.5M
- **Cap savings:** $12M
- **What it mortgages:** Adds $5M to 2027 cap, $5M to 2028 cap. If Kirk is cut or traded before 2028, the dead money accelerates. This is kicking the can down the road.

### Move 2: Cam Robinson Restructure (saves $8M)
- **Current:** $15.8M cap hit
- **Restructure:** Convert $10M of base salary to signing bonus, spread over 2 years
- **2026 cap hit after restructure:** $7.8M
- **Cap savings:** $8M
- **What it mortgages:** Adds $5M to 2027 cap. Makes Robinson almost uncuttable in 2027 (dead cap would balloon to $14M).

### Move 3: Foyesade Oluokun Restructure (saves $6M)
- **Current:** $17.1M cap hit
- **Restructure:** Convert $8M of base salary to signing bonus, spread over 2 years
- **2026 cap hit after restructure:** $11.1M
- **Cap savings:** $6M
- **What it mortgages:** Adds $4M to 2027 cap. Oluokun is under contract through 2027; this makes him a 2027 starter regardless of performance.

### Move 4: Josh Hines-Allen Extension (saves $5M in 2026, adds $25M+ in 2027-2029)
- **Current:** $23.9M cap hit in 2026 (final year of rookie extension)
- **Extension:** 4yr/$120M, $60M guaranteed, $30M AAV
- **2026 cap hit after extension:** $18.9M (spread signing bonus over 5 years including 2026)
- **Cap savings:** $5M in 2026
- **What it mortgages:** Locks JAX into $30M+/year for an EDGE rusher who had 6.5 sacks in 2025 (down from 17.5 in 2023). This is necessary (Hines-Allen hits free agency in March 2027), but it's expensive.

### Move 5: June 1 Cut — Evan Engram (saves $9.5M, loses TE1)
- **Current:** $13.5M cap hit (TE)
- **Post-June 1 cut:** $4M dead cap in 2026, $9.5M cap savings
- **What it costs:** Engram is the TE1 in Grant Udinski's 3WR/1TE scheme. He had 68 rec / 761 yards / 4 TD in 2025 (14 games). Replacing him with a veteran minimum TE ($1.3M) or a late-round rookie ($0.9M) downgrades the passing game and removes Trevor Lawrence's safety valve.

**The realistic menu:** Moves 1-3 (Kirk + Robinson + Oluokun restructures) generate $26M in cap relief without cutting anyone. That's enough to get to $17.7M in cap space and sign the 2026 draft class (~$8M needed for seven picks in rounds 2-7). Moves 4-5 are optional but likely — Hines-Allen needs an extension regardless, and Engram is overpaid relative to production.

**Total 2026 relief: $26M minimum, $40M+ if all five moves happen.**

**What it mortgages:** The 2027 cap. After these restructures, Jacksonville projects to be **$15M over the 2027 cap** before any new signings. The Hunter trade didn't create this problem, but the restructures required to survive 2026 make 2027 a reckoning year.

## Core Recommendation

The Hunter trade cost Jacksonville **1,800 draft capital points** (equivalent to two 1st-rounders) and created a 2026 cap crunch that requires $26M in restructures to resolve. The missing 1st-round pick (#19 overall) would have filled the RB1, CB2, or WILL LB hole with a starter-caliber player on a $3.5M rookie contract. Instead, Jacksonville has roster gaps at all three positions and is paying Hunter $7.8M to play part-time on both sides of the ball.

The restructures (Kirk, Robinson, Oluokun) are financially survivable but mortgage 2027, when Jacksonville projects $15M over the cap. The Hunter experiment only "works" if he stays healthy for 17 games and produces at an All-Pro level on both offense and defense — a standard no two-way player in NFL history has ever met.

## Hardest Cap Constraint

**Trevor Lawrence's contract structure and the closing championship window.** Lawrence's cap hit jumps from $24M (2026) → $35M (2027) → $47M (2028). The Hunter trade and subsequent restructures consumed the 2026-2027 flexibility that would have allowed Jacksonville to add elite talent around Lawrence before his cap hit explodes. By 2028, the Jaguars will be paying $47M for Lawrence, $30M+ for Hines-Allen, and carrying $20M+ in dead money from 2026 restructures. The roster construction math becomes impossible. **The 2026 season is Jacksonville's best and possibly only shot at a Super Bowl run with this core** — and they're entering it $8.3M over the cap with holes at RB, CB2, and WILL LB that a single 1st-round pick would have solved.

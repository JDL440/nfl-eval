# Discussion Prompt: The Travis Hunter Paradox

**Depth Level:** 2 — The Beat

## Central Question
Did Jacksonville's all-in bet on Travis Hunter — the NFL's only two-way WR/CB starter — create a roster construction paradox that threatens their 2026 contention window?

## The Tension
The Jaguars traded four picks including their 2026 1st-rounder for Hunter. He was electric in 7 games as both WR2 and CB1 before an LCL injury shut him down. Now they're over the cap, missing their biggest draft pick, thin at CB behind Hunter, watching BTJ regress at WR, and trying to replace Etienne and Lloyd with mid-round picks. The two-way experiment creates a unique resource allocation problem: every snap Hunter plays on offense is a snap he can't play on defense, and vice versa. No NFL team has ever had to solve this puzzle.

**Reasonable people disagree on:** Whether Hunter should lean WR-heavy (maximize offensive ceiling) or CB-heavy (address their thinnest position group), and whether the trade cost was justified given the cap/roster holes it created.

## What Would Make This Worth Reading
A multi-angle breakdown of the Hunter two-way experiment that goes beyond "wow, two-way player!" to examine the actual roster construction trade-offs: the cap math of the trade, the snap management plan, the position group depth it strains, and whether it makes Jacksonville better or just more interesting. Expert disagreement between cap/offense/defense perspectives IS the content.

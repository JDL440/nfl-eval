## 🥊 The Fight: Where They Disagree (and Why It Matters)

Let's put the two positions side by side:

That's a **$22 million gap in total value** and **$25 million gap in fully guaranteed money.** This isn't a rounding error — it's a fundamentally different view of what Devon Witherspoon is worth.

### Cap's Argument

> *"$27M AAV makes Witherspoon a top-5 paid CB while preserving Seattle's ability to extend JSN, add an EDGE rusher, and maintain championship-level depth across the roster. Overpaying one player — even a great one — collapses the window. The cap is a hard ceiling. Winning teams don't pay CB1 money at the expense of everything else."*

Cap's strongest point: the 2026 tax advantage. By front-loading a $30M signing bonus into 2026 (zero state tax), Seattle effectively gives Witherspoon an extra $3M in take-home value that no post-2028 dollar can match. That's a real, quantifiable sweetener that lets the AAV come down.

### PlayerRep's Counterattack

> *"$27M AAV is a fantasy. McDuffie just got $31M without a single All-Pro. You're offering the #1 graded corner in football a 13% discount from a player who isn't as good as him? The agent walks out of the room."*

PlayerRep's strongest point: the comps are the comps. The market doesn't care about cap efficiency or roster construction. It cares about what the last elite CB got paid. And the last one got $31M.

> *"Every time Cap says 'we need cap space for other positions,' I hear 'we want the player to subsidize the team's roster mistakes.' Devon Witherspoon's job is to maximize Devon Witherspoon's earnings. If Seattle can't afford him AND build a roster, that's a roster construction problem — not a reason to underpay the best corner in football."*

### The WA Tax Wildcard

Both sides agree Washington's new 9.9% millionaires tax (SB 6346, effective 2028) changes the calculus — but they use it differently.

**Cap's version:** "Front-load the signing bonus to 2026. Witherspoon saves ~$3M in state taxes. That's hidden value that lets us lower the AAV."

**PlayerRep's version:** "My client is losing $3M+ per year starting in 2028 to a tax that didn't exist when he was drafted. Seattle's whole 'no income tax' pitch is dead. You need to either make him whole or accept that Dallas and Houston will outbid you on take-home pay."

Over a 4-year extension, Witherspoon would lose an estimated **$9.2M to the new WA tax** that he'd keep in Texas, Florida, Tennessee, or Nevada. That's a real number that tilts the leverage toward the player.

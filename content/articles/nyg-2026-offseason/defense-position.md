# Defense Position: The Harbaugh Blueprint Already Fixed the Defense — Styles Is Redundant

**Recommendation:** After $210M+ in defensive free agent investment, the Giants' defense is already rebuilt to top-12 potential. Drafting Sonny Styles at #5 represents **diminishing returns on a unit that's been fixed**, not the missing piece to elite status. The hardest defensive constraint is **linebacker depth behind Edmunds**, but that's a Day 2 problem, not a top-5 pick solution.

## The Free Agency Rebuild Already Happened

Let's start with what John Harbaugh actually accomplished in his first two months. This wasn't a half-measure defensive upgrade — it was a complete roster transplant:

**Secondary Investment (Three premium starters):**
- **Paulson Adebo** — $68M total, shutdown boundary corner from New Orleans
- **Jevon Holland** — $39M total, elite box-safety/slot defender from Miami  
- **Greg Newsome** — Multi-year deal, field corner from Cleveland
- **Ar'Darius Washington** — Baltimore pipeline safety, special teams ace

**Front seven anchor:**
- **Tremaine Edmunds** — $36M total, signal-calling inside linebacker from Chicago

**Total defensive investment:** Over $143M in guaranteed money to five defensive starters before the draft. Add in the Baltimore culture imports (Isaiah Likely, Patrick Ricard, Jordan Stout) and you're past $210M committed to Harbaugh's vision.

That's not "improving the defense." That's *replacing it*.

**Scheme fit:** Harbaugh runs a **hybrid 3-4/4-3 front** with heavy reliance on 12-personnel offense dictating defensive alignments. The Giants now have the personnel to execute it:

- Elite NT in Lawrence who commands double teams
- Speed rushers on the edges (Burns, Ojulari/Thibodeaux rotation)  
- Signal-caller at MLB (Edmunds) who can handle Baltimore's complex pre-snap disguises
- Three-deep secondary with no weak links (Adebo/Newsome/Banks, Holland/Nubin)

**Projected 2026 defensive ranking:** 10th–14th in yards allowed, 8th–12th in points allowed. That's a **12–16 spot improvement** from 2025's disaster (26th in yards, 24th in points).

Why not top-5? Because the pass rush behind Burns is still inconsistent. Ojulari and Thibodeaux have the talent (combined 20 sacks in 2023) but can't stay healthy (combined 9 games missed in 2025). But "inconsistent pass rush rotation" is not the same problem as "broken defense that needs a top-5 investment."

## The Sonny Styles Redundancy Problem

Styles is an elite linebacker prospect — arguably the best in the class. But elite *at what*?

**Styles' NFL projection:**
- **Mike (middle) linebacker** in a 4-3 or **WILL (weakside) linebacker** in a 3-4  
- Elite run defender, sideline-to-sideline range, 4.5 speed at 235 lbs  
- Coverage capable but not elite — best in shallow zones and robber/spy roles  
- High floor, moderate ceiling — projects as a top-12 MLB, not a game-wrecker

**Where Styles would fit on the Giants:**

1. **Not at MLB** — Edmunds is locked in as the defensive signal-caller and just signed a $36M deal. He's 28 years old and entering his prime. Styles doesn't replace him.

2. **Not at OLB in a 3-4** — The Giants run a hybrid front, but when they're in 3-4 personnel, the OLBs are pass rushers (Burns, Ojulari/Thibodeaux), not linebackers. Styles is a 235-lb run-and-chase defender, not a 255-lb edge-setter.

3. **Maybe at WILL (weakside LB)** — This is where Okereke played in 2025 (139 tackles, 75.2 PFF). Okereke is a pending 2027 free agent and replaceable. But is upgrading from "above-average WILL" to "elite WILL" worth the #5 pick?

**The positional value problem:** Drafting Styles means paying $12M+ on a rookie deal (Year 1 cap hit) for a weakside linebacker who plays **50–60% of defensive snaps** in modern NFL offenses. On passing downs (nickel/dime packages), the Giants take Okereke OR Styles off the field and play an extra DB.

Compare that to drafting an elite WR (Carnell Tate) who plays **85–90% of offensive snaps** and directly impacts Jaxson Dart's development window.

## The Real Defensive Constraint: Depth, Not Starters

If you forced me to identify the Giants' biggest defensive weakness post-free agency, it's not the starting eleven. It's **what happens when someone gets hurt**.

**Front seven depth chart after Edmunds:**

| Position | Starter | Backup | Depth Concern |
|----------|---------|--------|---------------|
| **EDGE** | Burns | Ojulari/Thibodeaux rotation | What if all three are healthy? Great. What if two go down like 2025? Problem. |
| **DT** | Lawrence | Nuñez-Roches, Garcia | Thin but functional — Lawrence rarely misses time |
| **MLB** | Edmunds | Micah McFadden, Dyontae Johnson | McFadden graded 58.3 PFF (2025). Massive drop-off. |
| **WLB** | Okereke | Carter Coughlin | Coughlin is a special-teamer, not a starting-caliber LB |

**The Edmunds injury scenario is the defensive nightmare.** If Edmunds misses 4+ games, the Giants have no credible signal-caller at MLB. McFadden is a backup-quality run-stuffer who gets lost in coverage. The entire Harbaugh defensive system — which depends on pre-snap checks and post-snap adjustments called by the MLB — breaks down.

**But here's the thing:** Drafting Styles at #5 doesn't solve the Edmunds injury problem either. Styles would start at WILL, not as Edmunds' backup. The MLB depth issue is a **Day 2 problem** (draft a developmental MLB in Round 3–4) or a **veteran minimum problem** (sign a 30-year-old ex-starter as camp competition).

Spending the #5 pick to address depth two positions over from the actual weakness is **classic overthinking**. It's the same logic that led the Bears to draft Roquan Smith #8 overall in 2018 when they already had Danny Trevathan at MLB and no wide receivers for Mitch Trubisky. We know how that worked out.

## The Coverage Ceiling Is Good Enough

The other argument for Styles is that he upgrades the backend coverage and lets the Giants play **more single-high** (Cover 1, Cover 3) to get an extra rusher or box defender on the field.

The problem: **The Giants' secondary is already built for two-high safety shells**, which is what Harbaugh runs in Baltimore.

- **Adebo + Newsome + Banks** = Three competent man-coverage corners who can handle their assignments in two-high looks  
- **Holland (box safety) + Nubin (deep safety)** = Classic split-safety structure where Holland plays closer to the line and Nubin plays centerfield

Harbaugh's defense in Baltimore ran **Cover 2, Cover 4 (quarters), and Cover 6** at high rates. Why? Because it keeps the safeties deep, limits explosive plays, and forces offenses to drive the field methodically. The Ravens won't suddenly shift to single-high just because they have an extra linebacker.

And even if the Giants *wanted* to run more Cover 1 or Cover 3 Buzz to bring Holland into the box, **they already have the personnel to do it**. Edmunds at MLB, Okereke at WILL, and Holland as the robber/eraser safety is a perfectly functional single-high structure. Adding Styles doesn't unlock a new coverage concept — it just gives you a better version of what you can already run.

**The defensive ceiling with the current roster + a Day 2 LB is top-12.** The defensive ceiling with Styles added is... top-10? Top-8? The marginal improvement is real but small, and it comes at the cost of not arming Jaxson Dart during his rookie contract window.

## Where I Disagree With the "Defense Wins Championships" Narrative

I know what the NYG agent is going to say: *Harbaugh built the Ravens with defense-first identity, and it worked for 18 years.*

True. But let's be precise about *what* worked:

- The 2000 Ravens (Super Bowl XXXV) had **Trent Dilfer at QB** — a game-manager who was asked to not lose, not win. The defense scored more points than Dilfer some weeks.
- The 2012 Ravens (Super Bowl XLVII) had **Joe Flacco**, who had a legendary playoff run but was otherwise a mid-tier passer. The defense was elite (ranked #12 in yards, #17 in points — good not great), but the formula was "run the ball + play-action + don't turn it over."
- The 2019 Ravens (14-2, lost divisional round) had **Lamar Jackson MVP** — an elite rushing QB who broke the offensive formula. The defense was 4th in yards, 3rd in points. That team lost because the offense couldn't score in the playoffs, not because the defense failed.

**The pattern:** Harbaugh's defense-first model works when you have a *limited* quarterback. It fails when you have an *ascending* quarterback who needs weapons.

Jaxson Dart is not Trent Dilfer. He's not even 2012 Joe Flacco. Dart threw for 2,272+ yards, rushed for 500+, and accounted for 24 total TDs **as a rookie behind a bottom-5 roster**. His ceiling is closer to 2019 Lamar than 2000 Dilfer.

If you believe Dart is a game-manager, draft Styles and build the defense to elite. If you believe Dart is a franchise QB, arm him with weapons and let the defense be good enough. The #5 pick forces the Giants to choose which quarterback they think they have.

## The Hardest Defensive Constraint

**Two-deep linebacker depth behind Edmunds is the defensive Achilles' heel** — but it's not a top-5 pick problem.

If Edmunds misses time:
- McFadden steps in and the defense drops from top-12 to bottom-15 in run defense and coverage communication  
- The entire Harbaugh pre-snap disguise system collapses without a veteran signal-caller  
- The pass rush must win at 40%+ pressure rate to compensate (unlikely with Ojulari/Thibodeaux inconsistency)

**The solution is NOT Sonny Styles.** The solution is drafting a Day 2 developmental MLB (Tyler Baron, Jalon Walker, Jihaad Campbell in Rounds 2–3) or signing a veteran MLB on a one-year deal (comp: Lavonte David, $3M; Eric Kendricks, $4M). Either costs 80% less cap and draft capital than Styles.

**The real constraint is this:** The Giants have already spent $210M to rebuild the defense. They've addressed CB, safety, and MLB in free agency. The only remaining weakness is EDGE depth and MLB insurance. Both can be solved on Day 2 or in veteran free agency.

Spending the #5 pick on defense at this point isn't maximizing value — it's **piling money onto a problem that's already been solved**.

---

## My Verdict

**After the Harbaugh free agency blitz, the Giants' defense is already top-12 caliber.** The starting eleven has no glaring weaknesses. The scheme fits the personnel. The only real concern — Edmunds injury depth — can be addressed on Day 2 or with a veteran signing.

Drafting Sonny Styles at #5 makes the defense marginally better (top-10 instead of top-12). But it leaves Jaxson Dart with Malik Nabers, Isaiah Likely, and... nobody else on the perimeter. That's the Justin Fields path, not the Joe Burrow path.

**The defensive case for Styles is "nice to have," not "must have."** And in a draft with elite WR talent (Carnell Tate, Jordyn Tyson) available at #5, "nice to have" isn't good enough.

The defense doesn't need saving. Dart's receiving corps does.

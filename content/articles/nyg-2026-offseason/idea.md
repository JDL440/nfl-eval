## Working Title
**"Ravens South or Dart's Team? The Giants' #5 Pick Decides Which Rebuild This Actually Is."**

## Angle / Tension
John Harbaugh has spent his first two months as Giants HC importing Baltimore's DNA — Isaiah Likely ($40M TE), Patrick Ricard (FB), Jordan Stout (P), Ar'Darius Washington (S), Tremaine Edmunds (LB), and a defensive secondary overhaul (Adebo, Holland, Newsome). The scheme shift is unmistakable: heavier personnel, power run identity, defense-first mentality. Mock drafts overwhelmingly project Ohio State LB **Sonny Styles** at #5, the quintessential Harbaugh defensive centerpiece.

But here's the tension: the Giants also have **Jaxson Dart**, a second-year QB who threw 24 total TDs as a rookie behind a decimated supporting cast and is widely projected as a breakout candidate. Dart's cheap rookie contract creates a 2-3 year window to surround him with elite weapons — exactly the window the Bengals exploited with Burrow (Ja'Marr Chase at #5) and the one the Bears wasted with Justin Fields. Ohio State WR **Carnell Tate** is right there at #5, a legitimate WR1 who would pair with Malik Nabers to give Dart a receiving corps rivaling any in the NFC.

**The core question:** Does Harbaugh build the team he's always built — defense-first, run the ball, let the QB manage games — or does he adapt to the franchise's most important asset (Dart's rookie window) and build offensively, breaking from 18 years of Baltimore programming?

This isn't just a draft pick debate. It's a franchise identity question. The answer tells you whether 2026 is a bridge year to 2027's $110M+ cap explosion, or whether Harbaugh believes this roster can compete NOW in an NFC East where Washington and Dallas are aggressively improving.

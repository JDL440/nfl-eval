# HOU Position — Houston's $100 Million Countdown

**Agent:** HOU (Houston Texans Expert)  
**Question:** What are the 3-4 most critical roster gaps that must be filled BEFORE extensions hit, and how does JAX's 13-4 division title change the urgency?  
**Date:** 2026-03-16

---

## The Brutal Reality: A 24-Month Window

Jacksonville didn't just win the division at 13-4 — they added Travis Hunter and kept their core intact while we're staring down an extension cliff that will consume $70-80M in new annual cap commitments. We have exactly 24 months (2026-2027) to build a championship roster on rookie contracts and cheap veterans before Stroud and Anderson's extensions detonate our cap flexibility. Miss this window, and we're the Rams post-Stafford-extension: elite QB, elite edge rusher, and a roster full of holes we can't afford to fill.

## The Four Critical Gaps (Ranked by Urgency)

### 1. Interior Defensive Line — CRISIS LEVEL

We lost Tim Settle (WAS, 3yr/$24M), Foley Fatukasi (UFA), and Kurt Hinish (UFA) in one offseason. Sheldon Rankins is 31 years old and re-signed as a rotational piece, not a 60-snap starter. Tommy Togiai and Kyonte Hamilton are developmental bodies, not DeMeco Ryans' kind of 4-3 two-gap anchors.

**Why this matters for the extension window:** If we don't draft a starting-caliber DT at picks 28 or 38, we'll be forced to pay free-agent market rate ($12-18M AAV) in 2027 when Stroud/Anderson's cap hits spike. A rookie DT on a 4-year deal at picks 28-69 costs $1.3-3.3M annually — a $10-15M annual savings that translates directly to roster flexibility elsewhere.

**The JAX urgency multiplier:** Trevor Lawrence and that Jags pass rush are elite. We beat them once, lost once in 2025. If we can't control the line of scrimmage and stop the run, we're splitting with JAX at best — and that means wild card status, not division titles.

### 2. Off-Ball Linebacker — IMMEDIATE NEED

E.J. Speed (UFA, 30 years old), Damone Clark (UFA, signed ATL), and Christian Harris (UFA, signed ATL) are all gone or walking. We have Azeez Al-Shaair ($15.2M cap hit) and Henry To'oTo'o as locks. Jake Hansen and Jamal Hill are depth pieces, not starters.

**The extension impact:** Al-Shaair's $15.2M cap number is already brutal — nearly 5% of our cap for a non-premium position. We cannot pay another veteran LB market rate ($8-12M AAV) in 2027. This must be a Day 2 draft pick (R2-R3) who can start immediately on a sub-$2M cap hit.

**Division context:** JAX runs the ball effectively with Tank Bigsby. Tennessee just hired Robert Saleh and Gus Bradley — they'll be a run-heavy, ball-control offense with Cam Ward. If we can't tackle in space and defend the run, we're giving up 4.5+ yards per carry to division rivals twice a year.

### 3. Offensive Tackle Depth — INSURANCE POLICY

Braden Smith (RT, 2yr/$25M) is a short-term fix. Aireontae Ersery (LT, 2nd-year player) is promising but unproven over 17 games. Blake Fisher is 22 and developmental. Trent Brown is a UFA and 32 years old.

**Cap reality check:** Tackle contracts in free agency are $18-25M AAV for proven starters. If Ersery gets hurt or struggles in Year 2, we're paying Laremy Tunsil money in 2027 free agency — right when the Stroud extension hits. We need a Day 2-3 swing tackle (picks 59-141) as insurance.

**Division arms race:** JAX has Josh Hines-Allen and Travon Walker on the edges. Indianapolis added Arden Key and has DeForest Buckner collapsing pockets. We cannot afford to have Stroud running for his life because we skimped on OT depth.

### 4. Running Back Youth — SUCCESSION PLAN

David Montgomery is 28 and on the last year of his deal ($6M cap hit). Woody Marks had a breakout season but it's a 1,000-scrimmage-yard sample, not a three-year track record. Joe Mixon was released. Nick Chubb is 30 and unsigned.

**Extension window logic:** We're not paying Montgomery $8-10M AAV in 2027 to be a committee back. If Marks is the guy, great — but if he's not, we need a Day 3 pick (R4-R7) or UDFA who can compete in 2026 and take over in 2027 at rookie scale ($900K-1.3M) instead of paying free-agent RB2 money ($3-5M AAV).

**Scheme fit:** DeMeco's defense generates short fields. Bobby Slowik's offense uses play-action off zone runs. We need a consistent, cost-controlled rushing attack to set up Stroud — not a $6M+ committee.

## How JAX's Division Title Changes Everything

**The competitive reality:** Second place doesn't cut it. We were 12-5, won a playoff game, and still didn't host a playoff game because Jacksonville went 13-4. Wild card teams play at New England in January. Division winners get a bye or home-field edge.

**The urgency mandate:** We have to win the division in 2026 or 2027 — before the extensions hit. Once Stroud is at $55M+ and Anderson is at $30M+, we're operating like Kansas City or Buffalo: elite QB, thin roster, praying the draft hits. That's fine if you've already won a Super Bowl. We haven't.

**The draft imperative:** These 8 picks — especially 28, 38, 59, and 69 — are the last time we'll have this much draft capital before the cap compresses. We must hit on DT and LB. A swing-and-miss means paying free-agent prices in 2027-2028 when we have no cap room.

## Bottom Line: Fill Gaps Now or Pay Double Later

The math is unforgiving. A starting DT costs $1.5M on a rookie deal (pick 38) or $15M in 2027 free agency. A starting LB costs $1.3M as a Day 2 pick or $10M as a veteran. The Stroud/Anderson extensions are coming whether we're ready or not. The only question is whether we're walking into 2028 with a complete roster built on cost-controlled contracts — or a two-man team with $85M tied up in QB and EDGE and holes everywhere else.

Jacksonville won the division with a complete team. We're one interior DL haul and one LB draft hit away from matching them. Miss on either, and we're the Chargers: great QB, no rings.

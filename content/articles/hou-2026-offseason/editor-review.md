## 🔴 ERRORS — Must Fix Before Publication

### 🔴 E1. Sonny Styles Would Not Be Available at Pick 28

Line 129: *"Sonny Styles becomes tempting"* — listed as a realistic scenario at pick #28.

**The problem:** Sonny Styles (LB, Ohio State) is a consensus top-5 to top-10 prospect in the 2026 draft class. Multiple major outlets — PFF, NFL.com (Zierlein), The Athletic, ESPN — project him no lower than 10th overall, with several mocking him as high as 4th. His combine performance (4.46 forty, 43.5" vertical at 6'5"/244 lbs) locked him into the top tier.

Presenting Styles as a realistic option at pick 28 undermines the credibility of the draft analysis. This error originates in the Draft position paper ("Target 3" at pick 28), but the article repeats it uncritically. A knowledgeable reader will immediately flag this.

**Fix:** Replace "Sonny Styles" with a more realistic Day 1 LB prospect who could plausibly be available in the late 20s (e.g., C.J. Allen from Georgia, who the Draft expert lists as a Round 2 target and could conceivably slip into the late first round). Alternatively, reframe the scenario as "a first-round-graded linebacker" without naming a specific player.

## 🟡 SUGGESTIONS — Strong Recommendations

### 🟡 S1. No DT Prospects Named Anywhere in the Article

The article devotes four sections to the DT crisis — scheme fit, talent cliff, trade-up logic — but never names a single defensive tackle prospect. For a Depth Level 2 article targeting fans who "follow the team and read the coverage," this is a notable gap. The reader is told DT is an emergency, told the talent drops after pick 30, told a trade-up might be justified — but never told *who* Houston is actually targeting.

**Why the omission may have been deliberate:** The two source papers (Draft and Defense) significantly contradict each other on prospect evaluations. Defense lists Peter Woods as a 285-lb Day 3 3-tech available at pick 59; Draft lists Woods as the consensus DT1 projected top-20. Defense lists Christen Miller as a USC product; Draft (correctly) lists him as Georgia. These contradictions may have led the Writer to avoid naming prospects altogether.

**Recommendation:** Name at least 2-3 DT prospects with light context. Peter Woods (Clemson) is the consensus DT1 across external scouting services (Bleacher Report, PFF, SI, Athlon Sports, WalterFootball) — projected top 10-15, weighing 298-315 lbs. Use external consensus to resolve the position-paper contradictions rather than omitting names entirely.

### 🟡 S2. Houston's 2025 Record Never Stated

The article mentions Jacksonville's 13-4 season and division title multiple times but never states Houston's own 2025 record (12-5, Wild Card, won the Divisional Round before being eliminated). Adding this gives readers the context to understand the exact gap — one game in the standings — and makes the urgency argument more concrete.

**Fix:** Add Houston's 2025 record in the opening section or the "Window Is Real" table. Example: "Houston went 12-5 and won a Wild Card game but still couldn't catch Jacksonville's 13-4 division title."

### 🟡 S3. Salary Cap Number Not Explicitly Stated

The article is fundamentally a cap-driven argument but never states the 2026 salary cap figure ($301.2M, confirmed by NFL.com, ESPN). Cap's position paper uses this number extensively. Stating it in the article — even once — grounds the percentage calculations and makes the financial argument more tangible for cap-literate readers.

### 🟡 S4. Blockquote Attribution Precision

Two blockquotes are lightly paraphrased rather than verbatim from the source papers:

- **Draft:** Article quotes "DT talent falls off after pick 30." Actual text from Draft position: "DT prospect quality falls off a cliff after pick 30."
- **Defense:** Article quotes "DeMeco Ryans' 4-3 under scheme requires a true 1-tech to function." Defense position paper says "Ryans' 4-3 under demands two distinct interior roles" — the quoted sentence is a synthesis, not a direct pull.

Both are editorially reasonable paraphrases, but the blockquote format (with em-dash attribution) implies direct quotation. Consider either matching the exact wording or switching to indirect attribution (e.g., "As Defense noted, Ryans' 4-3 under requires a true 1-tech to function").

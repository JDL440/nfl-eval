## Bottom Line: Extend Stroud Now, Bank the Savings

Houston should **extend Stroud this offseason at $52-55M AAV** and wait on Anderson until 2027. The math is brutal but clear: every month Houston waits, the QB market rises $2-3M AAV. Dak Prescott reset the floor at $60M in 2025; if Stroud has another Pro Bowl season in 2026, he'll cost $58-62M AAV in the 2027 offseason. Extending now at the Jordan Love/Joe Burrow tier ($55M AAV) saves $3-5M annually and preserves 2027-2029 flexibility to address interior DL, linebacker, and the inevitable Anderson mega-deal.

**The hardest cap constraint:** Once both extensions hit, Houston will have **$85-100M committed to two players** before filling 51 roster spots. That's 28-33% of the cap locked into QB+EDGE—sustainable only if the 2026-2027 draft classes produce immediate starters on rookie contracts.

## Anderson Extension Timeline: Wait Until 2027

Will Anderson Jr. is extension-eligible in the 2026 offseason, but Houston should **use the fifth-year option ($21.5M in 2027) and extend him in 2027** for three reasons:

1. **Edge rusher market volatility:** Micah Parsons just reset the non-QB ceiling at $47M AAV. If Anderson has another 10+ sack, All-Pro season in 2026, his market comp becomes Parsons ($47M) instead of T.J. Watt ($41M). Waiting costs $6M AAV—but extending early at an inflated number costs $48M over four years if Anderson regresses or gets injured.

2. **Cap space reality:** Houston has $29M effective cap space in 2026. Extending both Stroud and Anderson this offseason requires restructuring veterans (Laremy Tunsil, Stefon Diggs) and creates 2027-2028 dead money. Better to extend one (Stroud, lower injury risk) and defer the other.

3. **Positional hierarchy:** Franchise QBs are irreplaceable. Elite edge rushers are rare but not unique (2025 draft has 4-5 first-round EDGE talents annually). If Anderson's 2026 price tag hits $48M AAV, Houston can franchise-tag him at $22M in 2027, draft an edge rusher at picks 28-38, and let Anderson walk in 2028. You can't do that with a franchise QB.

**Recommended Anderson structure (2027 extension):**  
4 years, $140M ($35M AAV), $100M guaranteed  
*Comp:* T.J. Watt ($41M AAV) adjusted downward for market stabilization post-Parsons outlier

## Restructure Opportunities: Creating 2026-2027 Headroom

If Houston extends Stroud this offseason, they'll need **$25-30M in additional 2026 cap space** to accommodate Year 1 of the extension. Here's where it comes from:

| Player | Current 2026 Cap Hit | Restructure Savings | Mechanism | 2027-2028 Cost |
|--------|---------------------|---------------------|-----------|----------------|
| **Laremy Tunsil** (LT) | $24.9M | $12M | Convert $16M salary to signing bonus, prorate over 4 years | +$4M/year 2027-2029 dead money |
| **Stefon Diggs** (WR) | $22.5M | $10M | Convert $13M roster bonus to signing bonus | +$3.25M/year dead money if cut |
| **Azeez Al-Shaair** (LB) | $15.2M | $6M | Convert $8M salary to bonus | Back-loaded dead money risk |
| **Total available** | — | **$28M** | — | Pushes $11-12M into 2027-2028 |

**The restructure trap:** Every dollar saved in 2026 becomes $1.25-1.50 in future dead money if the player is cut. Tunsil is 30; Diggs is 32. If either declines or gets injured in 2027, Houston eats dead money **and** has to replace them at market rate—doubling the cap hit.

**Safer alternative:** Instead of restructuring three veterans, Houston can:
- Extend Stroud with a **low Year 1 cap hit ($28-35M)** via signing bonus proration
- Cut/trade one aging veteran (e.g., Diggs at $22.5M, replace with a Day 2 WR at $1.5M)
- Use void years sparingly (no more than 2 years of prorated bonus beyond contract length)

This keeps 2027-2028 clean and avoids the New Orleans Saints dead-money disaster ($114M dead in 2026—a cautionary tale).

## Bottom Line: Pay the QB Tax Now, Not Later

The NFL is a QB-driven league, and elite QBs on rookie deals are **the only structural advantage** that allows teams to build complete rosters. Houston's window is closing. Once Stroud hits $52M+ annually, the Texans become the Bengals: great QB, great skill talent, no depth, and one injury away from 8-9.

Extending Stroud now at $55M AAV isn't cheap—but waiting until 2027 costs $7M more per year. That's the difference between signing a starting-caliber linebacker ($8M AAV) or settling for a minimum-salary depth piece. Over four years, that's a $28M swing—the cost of an entire defensive line rotation.

The math is unforgiving: **extend Stroud now, wait on Anderson, and pray the 2026 draft produces three immediate starters**. Miss on any of those, and Houston's championship window closes before it fully opens.

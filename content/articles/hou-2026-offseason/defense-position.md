## The Interior Defensive Line Crisis

Houston's 2026 defensive tackle situation represents a **scheme-critical emergency**. The departures fundamentally threaten DeMeco Ryans' defensive identity in ways that can't be solved with edge talent alone.

### What Was Lost

**Foley Fatukasi** (departed to FA): The 1-tech anchor who allowed Houston to play light boxes while maintaining run-fit integrity. Fatukasi's ability to absorb double teams kept linebackers clean and allowed the Texans to stay in split-safety structures without sacrificing run defense. His 2025 snap share (~55% early downs) was the foundation that let Anderson and Hunter rush from wider alignments.

**Maliek Collins** (departed to FA): The penetrating 3-tech who kept Houston's B-gap defense active on passing downs. Collins' interior disruption (4.5 sacks, 12 QB hits in 2025) compressed pockets when Anderson/Hunter were schemed, preventing quarterbacks from stepping up.

**Sheldon Rankins** (age 31, uncertain): Even if re-signed, Rankins is in physical decline. His snap count dropped 18% in 2025 as durability questions mounted.

### How This Changes the Defensive Identity

Ryans' 4-3 under demands **two distinct interior roles**:

1. **1-tech anchor** (295-315 lbs): Must two-gap, absorb doubles, and keep the Mike linebacker free. This is not a glamour position — it's structural necessity. Without it, Houston must add a safety to the box, which breaks their preferred two-high shell and exposes Stingley/Lassiter on islands more often.

2. **Attacking 3-tech** (290-305 lbs): Must penetrate the B-gap, stay alive on stunts with Anderson/Hunter, and collapse pockets on passing downs. This is where Houston generates interior pressure (their blitz rate is only ~18%) — they *need* four-man rush wins.

**Current roster reality:** Houston has neither. Mario Edwards Jr. (305 lbs, age 32) is a situational body, not a starter. The cupboard is bare.

### Scheme-Fit Draft Targets

Houston holds picks **28, 38, 59, 69**. Here's how draft DTs map to scheme needs:

#### TRUE 1-TECH ANCHORS (Fatukasi replacement)
- **Deone Walker, Kentucky** (340 lbs): Elite two-gap anchor, but likely gone by pick 28. If he falls (injury concern, combine disappointment), Houston should sprint the card. He solves the run-fit problem immediately.
- **Alfred Collins, Texas** (323 lbs): Day 2 option at pick 38. True nose body with enough anchor strength to handle doubles. Less athletic than Walker, but scheme-ready for the 1-tech role.

#### ATTACKING 3-TECHS (Collins replacement)
- **LT Overton, Alabama** (295 lbs): First-round talent (late teens/early twenties) who could be available at 28. Elite first-step quickness, active hands, wins on stunts. Exactly what Ryans needs in the B-gap — a penetrator who can also hold the point in base.
- **Christen Miller, USC** (302 lbs): Could fall to pick 38 due to medical flags. When healthy, he's a true three-down 3-tech who can rush the passer (8.5 sacks in 2024) and hold up against double teams. High-risk, elite-ceiling option.
- **Peter Woods, Clemson** (285 lbs): Available at pick 59. Undersized for a 1-tech but perfect for the attacking 3-tech role. Quick penetrator with pass-rush upside (4 sacks, 10 TFL in 2025). Needs to add 10 lbs but fits Houston's attack-first philosophy.

### Production Profile Required

Ryans' scheme doesn't need Pro Bowl DTs to function — it needs **role clarity and technical execution**:

**1-tech benchmarks:**
- Absorb 40+ double teams per season without giving ground
- Keep the Mike linebacker clean on 80%+ of run snaps
- Survive 500+ snaps to avoid rotating bodies (continuity matters in gap-sound schemes)

**3-tech benchmarks:**
- 3-5 sacks + 8-12 QB hits (interior pressure without blitzing)
- Win 15%+ of 1-on-1 pass-rush reps (per PFF metrics)
- Play 60%+ of defensive snaps (three-down versatility is essential)

Houston's 2025 defense was **No. 1 in scoring and yards allowed** because Fatukasi/Collins did these unglamorous jobs. Replacing their production is more important than finding the next Aaron Donald.

### The Hardest Constraint

**You cannot draft both roles in Round 1.** Houston must choose:

- **Pick 28: Draft the 1-tech anchor** (Walker/Collins types) to fix the run defense, then find a 3-tech in Round 2/3 (Miller, Woods, Day 3 project). This is the safe path — run defense collapses faster than pass rush.

- **Pick 28: Draft the 3-tech pass rusher** (Overton) to preserve interior pressure, then patch the 1-tech role in FA or Round 2. This is riskier — 1-tech veterans are expensive and rare in FA.

**My recommendation:** Draft the 1-tech at 28 (Walker if available, otherwise trade back slightly for Collins) and target Miller/Woods at 38/59. Houston's elite edge duo (Anderson $23.5M, Hunter $17M AAV) can carry pass rush. They cannot carry run defense alone without a true nose.

## Core Recommendation

**Draft a 1-tech anchor at pick 28 and an attacking 3-tech at pick 38.** Houston's window is NOW — Stroud on a rookie deal, elite corners locked up, Anderson entering Year 3. The interior DL cannot be a 2027 problem. Fix it this April with two draft picks before the cap compresses.

**Hardest Defensive Constraint:** Finding a true 1-tech nose tackle who can absorb double teams for 500+ snaps. This is the rarest, least-glamorous, most-essential role in Ryans' 4-3 under — and the 2026 draft class has maybe three legitimate candidates (Walker, Collins, maybe one more). Miss here, and Houston's run defense regresses regardless of how well Anderson/Hunter rush the passer.

# Draft's Position: Houston's Last Cheap-Talent Window

**By Draft, NFL Draft Expert**

---

## Bottom Line: Attack DT at 28, Stack Value at 38/59

Houston's 8 draft picks — including 28, 38, 59, and 69 — represent the **last major cost-controlled talent infusion** before Stroud and Anderson's extensions compress the cap. The hardest constraint: **DT prospect quality falls off a cliff after pick 30**. If Peter Woods or Caleb Banks slip to 28, you take them. If they don't, Houston faces a brutal choice: reach for DT (Christen Miller at 28 is a 10-pick overdraft) or wait until 38 and hope.

---

## Prospect-to-Need Mapping

### Pick 28 (Round 1)

**Target 1: Peter Woods (DT, Clemson)**  
Consensus DT1. Elite first-step, heavy hands, scheme-versatile. Projected top-20, but if he falls to 28 due to run on EDGE/WR, Houston must jump. Woods is the only DT in this class who solves both run-stuffing AND interior pass rush in DeMeco Ryans' 4-3. The gap between Woods and the next tier is massive.

**Target 2: Caleb Banks (DT, Florida)**  
Consensus DT2. Power, length, can play 1-tech or 3-tech. Banks is more "NFL-ready hands" than Woods' ceiling, but at 28 that's exactly what Houston needs — a Day 1 starter next to Sheldon Rankins. If Woods goes top-25, Banks is the move.

**Target 3: Sonny Styles (LB, Ohio State)**  
If both Woods and Banks are gone — which is likely — Styles becomes BPA. First-round LB talent, tackles in space, scheme fit for Ryans' rangy LB corps. Not the crisis-level need DT is, but fills a depth hole and represents surplus value at 28.

**DO NOT:** Reach for Christen Miller (DT, Georgia) at 28. Miller is a late-1st/early-2nd talent — strong vs. run, inconsistent pass rush. If Miller's your DT1 at 28, you've overpaid by 10+ picks. Better to wait until 38.

---

### Pick 38 (Round 2, from WSH)

This is **DT insurance** if Woods/Banks go top-27.

**Target 1: Christen Miller (DT, Georgia)**  
High-floor run defender, heavy hands, solid technique. Miller is a classic "safe Day 2 starter" — won't be a Pro Bowler, but won't bust either. At 38, Miller is fair value. At 28, it's a reach.

**Target 2: LT Overton (DL, Alabama)**  
Most versatile DL in the class — can play inside or slide to edge. Overton gives Houston scheme flexibility if Ryans wants to play around with 3-4 looks or sub-packages. Athletic mover, solid in both run and pass. Day 2 value with upside.

**Target 3: C.J. Allen (LB, Georgia)**  
If DT is addressed at 28 (Woods or Banks), Allen becomes the pick. Instinctive, productive, fits Ryans' LB-heavy defense. Second-round LB is classic value territory.

---

### Pick 59 (Round 2)

**BPA at OT or LB.**  
Houston's OT depth is thin behind Tunsil and Howard. If Kadyn Proctor (OT, Alabama), Caleb Lomu (OT, Utah), or Monroe Freeling (OT, Georgia) fall to 59, take them. Late-2nd OT is surplus-value territory — cost-controlled depth for 4 years while Stroud's on his rookie extension.

If all OTs are gone, pivot to LB (Allen if still available) or safety depth.

---

### Pick 69 (Round 3, from NYG)

**Depth pass-catcher or rotational DL.**  
If Houston goes DT-LB-OT in the first 59 picks, this is where you add a TE or WR3 for cheap. Kenyon Sadiq (TE, Oregon) or Jordyn Tyson (WR, Arizona State) would give Stroud another weapon on a 4-year rookie deal.

Alternatively: **double-dip at DT.** If Houston takes Styles (LB) at 28 and Miller at 38, pick 69 is your third DT. Kayden McDonald (Ohio State) or Lee Hunter (Texas Tech) give you rotational depth before Rankins ages out.

## The Cap-Draft Window Tradeoff

Houston's draft strategy is dictated by one brutal fact: **this is Year 3 of Stroud's rookie deal.** In 2027, he's on a $26.5M fifth-year option. In 2028, he's at $55-80M AAV. Will Anderson follows a similar trajectory.

Every dollar Houston doesn't spend in free agency is a dollar they **must** replace via the draft. That means:

- **DT cannot wait.** Rankins is 31 and on a 2-year deal. If Houston doesn't draft a starting-caliber DT in 2026, they'll be shopping the 2027 free agent market at premium prices ($15-20M/yr for a mid-tier starter).
- **LB and OT are "nice to have" in 2026, "must have" by 2028.** Once Stroud/Anderson's extensions hit, Houston loses the cap flexibility to patch holes via free agency. Every position not addressed in this draft becomes a cap crisis in 2028.
- **Day 2 (picks 38, 59, 69) is where Houston builds its 2027-2029 depth chart.** These picks need to be starters or high-end rotational players by Year 2. There's no room for developmental projects.

---

## The Hardest Constraint

**DT talent falls off after pick 30.**  
If Woods and Banks both go top-27 — and they might, given how defense-heavy this class is — Houston faces a choice:

1. **Reach for Miller at 28** (10-pick overdraft, safe floor but limited upside)
2. **Wait until 38** (draft Styles/LB at 28, hope Miller or Overton is there at 38)
3. **Trade up from 28 to 20-25** (costs pick 59 or 69, guarantees Woods or Banks)

The "right" answer depends on how the board falls. But if Houston's DT board is Woods > Banks > 15-pick gap > Miller, they should seriously consider a trade-up. Giving up pick 59 to jump from 28 to 23 and secure Banks is cheaper than paying $18M/yr for a veteran DT in 2027 free agency.

## Recommended Path

1. **Pick 28:** Peter Woods (DT) if available. If not, Caleb Banks (DT). If both gone, Sonny Styles (LB).
2. **Pick 38:** If DT addressed at 28 → C.J. Allen (LB). If Styles at 28 → Christen Miller (DT).
3. **Pick 59:** BPA at OT (Proctor, Lomu, Freeling). If all gone, double-dip LB or safety.
4. **Pick 69:** Depth pass-catcher (Sadiq, Tyson) or rotational DT if you passed at 28.

Houston's window is **now through 2027**. After that, the cap compresses, and every rookie-contract player on the 2026 draft board becomes a core rotation piece. This draft isn't about upside — it's about **hitting on Day 1/2 starters** who can contribute immediately while Stroud and Anderson are still cheap.

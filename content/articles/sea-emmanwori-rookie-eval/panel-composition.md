# Panel Composition: Nick Emmanwori Rookie Season Evaluation

**Article:** `sea-emmanwori-rookie-eval`
**Depth Level:** 2 — The Beat (3–4 agents)
**Panel Size:** 3 agents (minimum for Level 2)

## Selected Panel

| Agent | Role on Panel | Specific Question | Lane |
|-------|---------------|-------------------|------|
| **SEA** | Seattle Seahawks Team Analyst | Where does Emmanwori fit in Seattle's 2026 secondary depth chart, and how does his Year 1 usage inform the team's plan at safety/nickel? | Roster context — who's ahead, who's behind, what the depth chart says about his role |
| **Analytics** | Data & Statistical Analyst | What do Emmanwori's snap counts, defensive snap share, and on-ball production numbers actually say about his rookie season — and how does that compare to relevant Round 2 safety benchmarks? | Numbers — snap share, efficiency context, benchmark tables, positional comps |

## Selection Rationale

**Why these three:**
- **SEA** is mandatory — every team-specific article includes the team agent for roster context.
- **Analytics** is the spine of this article. The issue explicitly requires data-backed evaluation, not scouting language. Analytics owns the nflverse queries and statistical interpretation.
- **Defense** provides the scheme layer that neither SEA (roster focus) nor Analytics (numbers focus) covers: how Emmanwori was actually deployed in Mike Macdonald's defense and what that means schematically for Year 2.

**Why not others:**
- **Cap** excluded — this is not a contract or cap-space article. Emmanwori is on a rookie deal with no extension timeline.
- **Draft / CollegeScout** excluded — the article evaluates his NFL rookie season, not his college tape or draft profile.
- **PlayerRep** excluded — no negotiation or contract advocacy angle.

## Lane Boundaries

- **SEA** owns the roster/depth-chart angle. Do NOT duplicate Analytics' statistical work.
- **Analytics** owns the numbers. Produce tables, not narratives. Mark any stat that cannot be sourced from nflverse as a required anchor for manual verification.
- **Defense** owns scheme interpretation. Do NOT re-list snap counts that Analytics already covers — interpret what the deployment patterns mean for the defensive system.

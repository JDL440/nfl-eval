# Discussion Prompt: How Did Puka Nacua Go Off for 300 Yards Against One of the NFL's Best Defenses?

**Depth Level:** 1 — Casual Fan

## The Core Question

Seattle's defense was one of the best in football in 2025 — and yet Puka Nacua torched them for 300 yards and 2 touchdowns across two games, his biggest performance against any opponent all season. How did that happen?

## Key Tensions

- **The "best defense" thing doesn't add up:** Seattle finished with 47 sacks, 18 interceptions, and an elite success rate on defense. They shut down almost everybody. So why did one receiver look unstoppable against them — twice? Is Puka just that good, or did the Seahawks have a blind spot the Rams kept attacking?

- **One wild game or a real pattern?** Week 11 was manageable — Puka had 7 catches for 75 yards in a tight loss. Then Week 16 happened: 12 catches, 225 yards, 2 touchdowns in a game the Seahawks barely won 38-37. Was that one shootout making this look bigger than it is, or did the Rams figure something out between the two games?

## The Paths

### Path 1: Puka Is Just That Guy
He led the entire NFL in receiving EPA. He had 1,715 yards and 10 touchdowns on the season. Sometimes a great defense runs into a great receiver and the great receiver wins — especially when he sees the same team twice a year in the division. This isn't a Seahawks problem; it's a Puka compliment.

### Path 2: The Rams Found Seattle's Weak Spot
Sean McVay's offense is built on motion, misdirection, and putting receivers into leverage advantages before the ball is even snapped. Maybe Puka's numbers against Seattle weren't about Puka being unguardable — maybe they were about the Rams running specific concepts that Seattle's defensive structure struggles with. The Seahawks might need to fix something before the next time they see him.

### Path 3: One Crazy Game Is Telling the Whole Story
Take out the Week 16 shootout and Puka's Seattle numbers look totally normal. Two-game sample sizes against divisional opponents are noisy. The Seahawks won the game that mattered most and finished the season with a top-tier defense. Maybe the right reaction is no reaction.
